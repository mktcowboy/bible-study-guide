# Fall of Babylon

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Revelation]]
- **Chapters:** Revelation 17-18
- **Summary:** John is shown the great harlot Babylon seated on many waters, arrayed in purple and scarlet and drunk with the blood of the saints; an angel proclaims her fall; the ten horns and the beast turn against her and devour her; the merchants and kings of the earth mourn her destruction, while heaven is commanded to rejoice over her judgment.

## Significance
The fall of Babylon represents the decisive overthrow of the world system that has persecuted the saints and seduced the nations through wealth, idolatry, and spiritual adultery. Her destruction in one hour demonstrates the swiftness of divine retribution against pride and corruption. The call "Come out of her, my people" underscores the ongoing summons to holiness and separation from worldly entanglements even amid tribulation.

## Key Verses
> "And upon her forehead was a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH." — Revelation 17:5 (KJV)

> "Alas, alas, that great city Babylon, that mighty city! for in one hour is thy judgment come." — Revelation 18:10 (KJV)

# Nicodemus and the New Birth

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[John]]
- **Chapters:** John 3:1-21
- **Summary:** Nicodemus, a Pharisee and ruler of the Jews, comes to Jesus by night seeking understanding. Jesus declares "Ye must be born again" (v.7), teaching that entrance into the kingdom of God requires a new birth by water and the Spirit. He concludes with the promise of John 3:16 — that God gave His only begotten Son so that whosoever believeth in Him should not perish but have everlasting life.

## Significance
This passage establishes the doctrine of regeneration as the necessary gateway to salvation, distinguishing spiritual birth from natural descent. The declaration of John 3:16 stands as the most concise expression of the gospel of grace in all of Scripture. Nicodemus's nighttime inquiry and his struggle to comprehend spiritual rebirth illustrate the limits of human wisdom and the need for divine illumination.

## Key Verses

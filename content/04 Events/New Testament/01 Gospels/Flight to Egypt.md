# Flight to Egypt

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 2:13-15
- **Summary:** After the [[Visit of the Magi]], an angel of the Lord appeared to [[Joseph (Husband of Mary)]] in a dream, warning him to take the young child and his mother and flee to Egypt, for [[Herod the Great]] sought the child's life. Joseph rose by night and departed immediately, and the family remained in Egypt until the death of Herod. Matthew explicitly applies Hosea 11:1 to this event — "Out of Egypt have I called my son" — reading Israel's exodus pattern as prophetically recapitulated in the person of Christ.

## Significance
The Flight to Egypt presents Jesus as the true Israel, reliving in his own person the defining experience of the nation — descent into Egypt and a divinely ordered deliverance. Matthew's use of Hosea 11:1 is a typological fulfillment: the words spoken of corporate Israel are seen to find their deepest meaning in the Son of God, who embodied and recapitulated his people's history. The event also demonstrates the providential protection of the infant Christ against the murderous designs of [[Herod the Great]], prefiguring the pattern of God's care over his anointed throughout his earthly ministry.

# Call of the First Disciples

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]], [[Mark]], [[John]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 4:18-22; Mark 1:16-20; John 1:35-51
- **Summary:** Jesus calls his first disciples from their work by the Sea of Galilee — Peter and Andrew from their nets, and James and John from mending their nets with their father Zebedee. John 1 records an earlier encounter where Andrew and an unnamed disciple follow Jesus after John the Baptist's testimony, and Andrew then brings his brother Peter.

## Significance
The calling of the disciples is the foundation of the new covenant community Jesus is forming. His summons — "Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men" (Matthew 4:19) — is both authoritative invitation and transformation: they are not merely changing occupations but entering a new mode of existence shaped entirely by following Jesus. The immediate, total response ("they straightway left their nets") models the unconditional obedience of discipleship. John's account (chapter 1) adds the testimony chain: John the Baptist to Andrew, Andrew to Peter, Jesus to Philip, Philip to Nathanael — each witness bringing another to Christ.

## Key Verses
> "And he saith unto them, Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men. And they straightway left their nets, and followed him." — Matthew 4:19-20 (KJV)

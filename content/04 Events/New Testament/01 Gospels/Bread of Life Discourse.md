# Bread of Life Discourse

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[John]]
- **Chapters:** John 6:22-71
- **Summary:** The day after the feeding of the five thousand, the crowd follows Jesus to Capernaum seeking bread. Jesus redirects their hunger from physical to spiritual, declaring himself the true bread come down from heaven. The discourse culminates in a saying so hard that many disciples depart, yet Peter confesses on behalf of the Twelve that there is none other to whom they can go.

## Significance
The Bread of Life Discourse is the longest and most theologically dense of the seven "I AM" statements in John's Gospel, anchoring the divine claim in the explicit contrast with the manna given through Moses. Where the manna fed Israel in the wilderness for a generation and those who ate it died, Jesus offers himself as living bread that imparts eternal life. The exodus typology is deliberate: Jesus is both the new Moses and the manna itself, collapsing the type and the antitype into one person.

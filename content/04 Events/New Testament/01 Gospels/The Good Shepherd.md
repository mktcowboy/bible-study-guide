# The Good Shepherd

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[John]]
- **Chapters:** John 10:1-30
- **Summary:** Jesus declares "I am the good shepherd" (v.11, 14), contrasting Himself with thieves, robbers, and hirelings who neither own nor care for the sheep. The good shepherd "giveth his life for the sheep" (v.11), knows His own and is known by them, and speaks of "other sheep...which are not of this fold" (v.16), pointing to the inclusion of the Gentiles. He closes with the assurance that His sheep hear His voice and that no man shall pluck them out of His hand (v.27-28).

## Significance
The discourse reveals Jesus as the fulfillment of the Old Testament shepherd imagery, particularly Ezekiel's prophecy of a faithful shepherd set over Israel (Ezekiel 34). By laying down His life voluntarily, Jesus distinguishes His sacrifice as an act of divine love rather than compulsion. The promise that no one can snatch His sheep from His hand is a foundational text on the security of the believer.

## Key Verses
> "I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep." — John 10:11 (KJV)

> "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me: And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand." — John 10:27-28 (KJV)

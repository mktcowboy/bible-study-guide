# Raising of Jairus's Daughter

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 9:18-19, 23-26; Mark 5:21-24, 35-43; Luke 8:40-42, 49-56
- **Summary:** Jairus, a ruler of the synagogue, fell at Jesus's feet and besought him earnestly for his dying daughter. On the way to his house word came that the child had already died, but Jesus said, "Be not afraid, only believe." He entered the house, took the girl by the hand, and spoke the words "Talitha cumi" — "Damsel, I say unto thee, Arise" — and she arose immediately; and he commanded that something be given her to eat.

## Significance
The raising of Jairus's daughter is a foretaste of the resurrection power that Christ would fully manifest in his own rising from the dead. By taking the inner circle of disciples as witnesses, Jesus established the testimony on more than one mouth and previewed the new-creation authority he would exercise after the cross. The detail of commanding food for the child underscores that she was raised to genuine bodily life, not an apparition.

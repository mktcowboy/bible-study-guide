# Visit of the Magi

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 2:1-12
- **Summary:** Wise men (*magoi*) from the East follow a star to Jerusalem, inquiring where the King of the Jews has been born, which troubles Herod the Great and all Jerusalem. Directed by the chief priests and scribes to Bethlehem on the basis of Micah 5:2, they follow the star to the house where the child Jesus is found with Mary His mother. They fall down in worship and present gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh. Being warned of God in a dream not to return to Herod, they depart to their own country by another way, thwarting Herod's design.

## Significance
The Visit of the Magi is the first recorded instance of Gentiles worshipping Jesus Christ, foreshadowing the universal scope of the Gospel and the ingathering of the nations prophesied in Isaiah 60:6. Matthew presents it as explicit fulfillment of Micah 5:2, establishing from the outset that Jesus is the long-awaited Messianic King born in the city of David. The gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh have been read as symbolic of Christ's kingship, priestly intercession, and anticipated suffering and burial.

# Crucifixion

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Primary Texts:** Matthew 27:32-56; Mark 15:21-41; Luke 23:26-49; John 19:16-37
- **Summary:** Jesus is crucified at Golgotha between criminals, bears mockery and darkness, gives up his spirit, and his death opens the way to God as the temple veil is torn.

## Details

After his trials, mockery, scourging, and sentencing under [[Pilate]], [[Jesus Christ]] is led to Golgotha. Simon of Cyrene is compelled to carry the cross. The soldiers crucify Jesus and divide his garments. The inscription over him identifies him as King of the Jews, a charge meant as Roman mockery but true in deeper irony.

The crucifixion is surrounded by derision. Passersby, religious leaders, soldiers, and criminals mock Jesus' claims. The one who saved others appears unable or unwilling to save himself. Yet the Gospels present this as the very means by which he saves others. Jesus refuses to come down from the cross because the cross is his obedient path.

From the sixth to the ninth hour, darkness covers the land. Jesus cries out with the words of Psalm 22: "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" He later gives up his spirit. The temple veil is torn from top to bottom, the earth quakes, and the centurion confesses that Jesus is truly the Son of God.

John adds several crucial details: Jesus entrusts [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]] to the beloved disciple, declares "It is finished," and has his side pierced, producing blood and water. The soldiers do not break his legs, fulfilling Passover and righteous sufferer patterns. The crucifixion is therefore legal execution, royal enthronement in irony, Passover fulfillment, atoning death, and cosmic judgment.

The cross stands at the center of Christian faith. It reveals human sin, divine love, covenant fulfillment, substitutionary suffering, and the victory of the obedient Son.

## Notes
- The tearing of the veil from top to bottom signals divine action and opened access to God's presence.
- The mockery at the cross is saturated with irony: Jesus is king, temple, Son, and Savior precisely as he suffers.
- The crucifixion fulfills Scripture not as detached proof-texting but as the culmination of Passover, sacrifice, righteous suffering, and Davidic kingship.
- The cross is both human injustice and divine redemption.

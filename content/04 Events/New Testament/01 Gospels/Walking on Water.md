# Walking on Water

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 14:22-33; Mark 6:45-52; John 6:15-21
- **Summary:** After the feeding of the multitude, Jesus constrained the disciples to depart by boat while he dismissed the crowd and withdrew alone to pray. In the fourth watch of the night he came to them walking upon the [[Sea of Galilee]], and the disciples were afraid. Peter stepped out of the boat and walked on the water toward Jesus, but when he saw the wind he feared and began to sink, crying, "Lord, save me." Jesus stretched forth his hand, and when they entered the boat the wind ceased and those present worshipped him.

## Significance
The episode is a direct revelation of Christ's divine nature, for in the Old Testament walking upon the sea is an attribute of God alone (Job 9:8). Peter's sinking teaches that faith falters when the eyes shift from Christ to circumstances, while Christ's immediate rescue illustrates the sufficiency of grace for the failing believer. Matthew's account alone includes the disciples' confession, "Of a truth thou art the Son of God" (Matthew 14:33).

# Stilling the Storm

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 8:23-27; Mark 4:35-41; Luke 8:22-25
- **Summary:** Jesus and his disciples set out to cross the [[Sea of Galilee]], and as they sailed a great tempest arose while Jesus slept in the stern. The disciples woke him in fear, and he rebuked the wind and the sea — "Peace, be still" — whereupon there was a great calm. The disciples marvelled, saying, "What manner of man is this, that even the winds and the sea obey him?"

## Significance
The miracle declares Christ's sovereignty over the created order, demonstrating the same divine authority over wind and wave ascribed to God in the Psalms and Job. The disciples' fear and wonder highlight the dawning recognition that Jesus is more than a teacher or prophet. The contrast between his sleep and his instant command underscores his full humanity and full deity.

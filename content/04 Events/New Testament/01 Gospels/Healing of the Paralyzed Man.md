# Healing of the Paralyzed Man

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]], [[Mark]], [[Luke]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 9:1–8; Mark 2:1–12; Luke 5:17–26
- **Summary:** Four men carry a paralyzed man to Jesus in a crowded house in Capernaum. Unable to enter, they break through the roof and lower the man on his mat. Jesus, seeing their faith, first forgives the man's sins — scandalizing the scribes — then heals him. The miracle proves the Son of Man has authority to forgive sins.

## Details

Jesus has returned to Capernaum ("his own city" — Matthew 9:1) and is teaching in a house. The crowd is so large that four men carrying a paralytic cannot reach him. Mark's vivid account (2:4) describes them removing the roof tiles and lowering the man through the opening — an act of extraordinary faith and determination. When Jesus sees their faith (plural — the four, and perhaps the man himself), he speaks to the paralytic: "Son, be of good cheer; thy sins be forgiven thee" (Matthew 9:2).

This triggers the first serious conflict with the scribes and Pharisees in the Synoptics. They reason internally: "Who can forgive sins but God only?" They are right about the logic — only God can forgive sins. Jesus addresses their unspoken objection and uses the healing as a sign: "Which is easier, to say, Thy sins be forgiven thee; or to say, Arise and walk? But that ye may know that the Son of man hath power on earth to forgive sins—" and commands the man to rise. He rises, takes up his mat, and walks out before them all. The crowd glorifies God, saying, "We never saw it on this fashion."

The miracle thus accomplishes two things simultaneously: it heals the man and it makes an unmistakable claim about Jesus's identity. Forgiveness and healing are not separate in his ministry — both flow from the same divine authority. The question "which is easier to say?" is rhetorical: forgiving sins is harder to verify (and thus easier to claim), but Jesus performs the harder-to-fake physical miracle as proof that the invisible reality is genuine.

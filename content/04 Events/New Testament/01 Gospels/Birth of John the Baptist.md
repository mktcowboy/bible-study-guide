# Birth of John the Baptist

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Luke]]
- **Chapters:** Luke 1:57-80
- **Summary:** Elizabeth fulfils her time and brings forth a son; neighbours and kinsfolk rejoice with her at the mercy of the Lord. On the eighth day the child is to be named, and contrary to expectation the family calls him John at Elizabeth's direction, confirmed by Zechariah in writing. Zechariah's tongue is immediately loosed and he utters the Benedictus, prophesying the redemption God hath raised up and the child's calling as prophet and forerunner of the Lord. The passage closes with the note that "the child grew, and waxed strong in spirit, and was in the deserts till the day of his shewing unto Israel" (v.80).

## Significance
The naming of John and the loosing of Zechariah's tongue (which had been bound since his unbelief in the temple, Luke 1:20) demonstrate God's faithfulness to His covenant promises and the arrival of a new prophetic age. The Benedictus draws on the language of the Davidic covenant and the prophets, situating John within the grand sweep of redemptive history. John's desert sojourn foreshadows his ministry as a voice calling Israel to repentance.

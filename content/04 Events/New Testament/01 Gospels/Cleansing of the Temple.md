# Cleansing of the Temple

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 21:12-17; Mark 11:15-17; Luke 19:45-46; John 2:13-22
- **Summary:** Jesus entered the temple in Jerusalem and drove out all those who bought and sold, overturning the tables of the moneychangers and the seats of the dove sellers, declaring, "My house shall be called the house of prayer; but ye have made it a den of thieves" (Matthew 21:13). The blind and the lame came to him in the temple and he healed them. John's gospel records an earlier or distinct cleansing in which Jesus added the prophecy, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up" (John 2:19), which the disciples later understood as a reference to his body.

## Significance
The cleansing of the temple is an act of prophetic judgment, recalling the zeal of Phinehas and the reforming kings of Israel, and asserting Jesus' authority over Israel's central institution of worship. By citing Isaiah 56:7 and Jeremiah 7:11 together, Jesus condemns both the exclusion of the nations from proper worship and the false sense of security that corrupt temple religion had bred. John's account links the act directly to the Resurrection, grounding Jesus' authority not in priestly lineage but in his own risen body as the new temple.

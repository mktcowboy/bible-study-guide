# Olivet Discourse

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Matthew]]
- **Chapters:** Matthew 24-25 (parallels Mark 13, Luke 21)
- **Summary:** Seated on the Mount of Olives opposite the temple, Jesus answers His disciples' questions about the destruction of Jerusalem and the signs of His coming and the end of the age. He warns of false christs, wars, famines, and persecutions; speaks of the abomination of desolation standing in the holy place; and describes the coming of the Son of Man in power and great glory. Matthew 25 extends the discourse with three parables — the Ten Virgins, the Talents, and the Sheep and Goats — calling for watchful readiness and faithful service until the final judgment.

## Significance
The Olivet Discourse is the longest prophetic sermon of Jesus recorded in the Gospels and stands as the New Testament's most concentrated block of eschatological teaching. It draws heavily on Daniel and the prophetic tradition, presenting Christ as the Son of Man who comes on the clouds to gather His elect and sit in judgment over all nations. The discourse holds both near-term fulfillment in the AD 70 destruction of Jerusalem and ultimate fulfillment in Christ's return, and has shaped Christian eschatology across every era of the church.

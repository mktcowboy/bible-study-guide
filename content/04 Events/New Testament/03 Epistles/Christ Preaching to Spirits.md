# Christ Preaching to Spirits

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[1 Peter]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Peter 3:18-22
- **Summary:** Peter refers to Christ made alive in the Spirit and proclaiming to imprisoned spirits, alongside Noah and baptism typology.

## Details
This is one of the most debated passages in the New Testament. Peter's main pastoral point is clear even where interpretive details are difficult: Christ suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, to bring believers to God, and he now reigns with angels, authorities, and powers subjected to him.

## Key People Involved
- [[Jesus Christ]] - Suffered, was made alive, and reigns
- [[Noah]] - Background figure in the ark and flood analogy
- [[Peter]] - Author

## Cross-References
- [[The Flood]] - Noah and the ark

## Notes
The passage has interpretive complexity, but its logic serves encouragement: suffering believers belong to the victorious Christ.

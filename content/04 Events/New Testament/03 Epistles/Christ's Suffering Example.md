# Christ's Suffering Example

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[1 Peter]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Peter 2:18-25
- **Summary:** Peter presents Christ's unjust suffering as the pattern for endurance and the ground of salvation.

## Details
Peter addresses servants suffering unjustly and points them to Christ, who committed no sin, did not revile in return, entrusted himself to the righteous Judge, and bore sins in his body on the tree. The passage is saturated with Isaiah 53.

## Key People Involved
- [[Peter]] - Author
- [[Jesus Christ]] - Suffering shepherd and sin-bearer

## Cross-References
- [[The Suffering Servant]] - Isaiah 53 background
- [[Crucifixion]] - Christ bearing sins on the tree
- [[Living Hope]] - Suffering interpreted through resurrection hope

## Notes
Peter does not romanticize suffering; he anchors endurance in Christ's saving suffering and God's final justice.

# No Favoritism

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[James]]
- **Chapters:** James 2:1-13
- **Summary:** James condemns partiality toward the rich as a denial of the royal law of love.

## Details
James imagines a worship gathering where a wealthy visitor receives honor while a poor believer is pushed aside. The scene exposes how social status can quietly govern the church's behavior. James answers that God has chosen the poor to be rich in faith, and that favoritism violates the command to love one's neighbor.

## Key People Involved
- [[James (Brother of Lord)]] - Author
- The rich and poor in the assembly - The test case for partiality

## Notes
For James, the test of orthodoxy is not only confession but whether the congregation honors the poor with the dignity God gives them.

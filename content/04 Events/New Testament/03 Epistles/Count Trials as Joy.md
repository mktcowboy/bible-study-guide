# Count Trials as Joy

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[James]]
- **Chapters:** James 1:2-4
- **Summary:** James opens by reframing trials as the testing ground where faith produces patience and maturity.

## Details
James does not call suffering pleasant. He calls believers to count trials as joy because God uses the testing of faith to produce steadfast endurance. Patience is not merely passive waiting; it is faithful durability under pressure until the believer becomes "perfect and entire, wanting nothing."

## Key People Involved
- [[James (Brother of Lord)]] - Author and pastoral teacher

## Cross-References
- [[Living Hope]] - Peter's parallel picture of faith refined through trials
- [[Job]] - Old Testament witness to tested endurance
- [[Romans]] 5 - Tribulation producing patience and hope

## Notes
James begins with a wisdom move: the trial is interpreted by its end, not only by its pain.

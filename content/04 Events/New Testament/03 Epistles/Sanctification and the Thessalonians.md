# Sanctification and the Thessalonians

## Overview

- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[1 Thessalonians]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Thessalonians 4:1-12
- **Summary:** [[Paul (Apostle)]] exhorts the Thessalonians to abound more and more in the walk he had already taught them, centering his appeal on the will of God for their sanctification: that they abstain from fornication, that each man possess his own vessel in honour and holiness, and that they love one another with a growing brotherly love. He calls them to live quietly, mind their own business, and work with their own hands, so that they may walk honestly toward them that are without and have need of nothing.

## Significance

This passage presents sanctification as a concrete, embodied reality touching sexuality, relationships, community love, and daily labor — not an abstract spiritual state. Paul grounds the call to sexual purity in the character of God himself ("God hath not called us unto uncleanness, but unto holiness," v. 7) and warns that to disregard this is to despise not man but God. The command to work with one's own hands was of particular relevance in a culture where manual labor was sometimes disdained; Paul insists that visible, dignified industry is part of the gospel witness before a watching world.

## Key Verses

> "For this is the will of God, even your sanctification, that ye should abstain from fornication: That every one of you should know how to possess his vessel in sanctification and honour; Not in the lust of concupiscence, even as the Gentiles which know not God." — 1 Thessalonians 4:3-5 (KJV)

> "And that ye study to be quiet, and to do your own business, and to work with your own hands, as we commanded you; That ye may walk honestly toward them that are without, and that ye may have lack of nothing." — 1 Thessalonians 4:11-12 (KJV)

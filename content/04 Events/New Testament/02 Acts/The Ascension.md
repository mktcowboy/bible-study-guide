# The Ascension

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Acts]]
- **Chapters:** Acts 1:9-11; Luke 24:50-53
- **Summary:** Forty days after the Resurrection, Jesus led the disciples toward Bethany, blessed them, and was "taken up; and a cloud received him out of their sight" (Acts 1:9). As the disciples gazed upward, two angels appeared and declared that Jesus would return "in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven" (Acts 1:11). Luke records that the disciples "returned to Jerusalem with great joy" and continued in the temple praising God (Luke 24:52-53).

## Significance
The Ascension marks the exaltation of the risen Christ to the right hand of the Father, fulfilling his own words that he would go to prepare a place for his disciples (John 14:2). It is the event that precedes and necessitates the sending of the Holy Spirit at [[Pentecost and the Birth of the Church]], as Jesus had promised the Comforter would not come unless he departed. The angelic promise of a visible return anchors the Christian hope of the Second Coming in the same bodily reality as the departure.

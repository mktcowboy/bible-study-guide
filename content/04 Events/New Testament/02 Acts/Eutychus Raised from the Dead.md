# Eutychus Raised from the Dead

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Acts]]
- **Chapters:** Acts 20:7-12
- **Summary:** At Troas on the first day of the week, Paul preaches until midnight in a room lit with many lamps; a young man named Eutychus, sitting in a window, falls asleep and falls from the third floor; he is taken up dead; Paul goes down, embraces him, and declares "his life is in him"; the disciples take him home alive, "not a little comforted."

## Significance
The account is embedded in a "we passage" — Luke is present and narrates as an eyewitness. The timing (first day of the week, breaking of bread) locates this in a Sunday Eucharistic assembly, one of the earliest explicit references to the church's weekly Lord's Day gathering. The resurrection of Eutychus echoes Elijah raising the widow's son (1 Kings 17) and Elisha raising the Shunammite's son (2 Kings 4), marking Paul as standing in the line of prophetic miracle-workers. The lightness of the narrative's ending — the congregation simply returns to the meal — reflects the early church's steady confidence in the resurrection power at work among them.

# Planting of the Philippian Church

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Book:** [[Acts]]
- **Chapters:** Acts 16:11-40
- **Summary:** Following the [[Macedonian Vision]], Paul and his companions arrived at [[Philippi]], a leading Roman colony in Macedonia. There Lydia, a seller of purple from Thyatira, was converted by the riverside and her household baptised, becoming the first European Christian household. Paul and Silas were subsequently imprisoned after casting out a divining spirit from a slave girl; a midnight earthquake freed them, and the terrified jailer and his household were converted before sunrise. The magistrates, learning that Paul was a Roman citizen, offered a public apology before releasing him.

## Significance
The Philippian episode is the founding narrative of the first Christian congregation on European soil. It displays the gospel breaking across three representative social strata simultaneously: Lydia, a successful merchant woman; a nameless slave girl held in spiritual bondage; and a Roman soldier-administrator exercising colonial authority. Each is met and transformed. The church born from these events became Paul's most faithful and personally beloved congregation, the recipient of his most warmly affectionate letter and the only church from which he accepted financial support during his later imprisonments.

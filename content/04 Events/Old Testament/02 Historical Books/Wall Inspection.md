# Wall Inspection

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Nehemiah]]
- **Chapters:** Nehemiah 2:11-20
- **Summary:** Nehemiah arrived in Jerusalem and, three days later, secretly inspected the ruined walls at night with a small group before revealing his plan to the community leaders.

## Details
After receiving the king's authorization and letters of safe passage, Nehemiah arrived in Jerusalem. He rested three days, then at night he took a small group — he told no one what God had put in his heart — and set out on an animal to inspect the walls. He went out by the Valley Gate and surveyed the walls to the south, the Dung Gate, the Fountain Gate, the King's Pool. At a point the terrain made it impossible to continue on the animal, so he went on foot up the valley and inspected the wall before returning by the Valley Gate. The inspection revealed the full extent of the damage — broken walls, burned gates.

The next morning Nehemiah called together the priests, nobles, rulers, and all who would be doing the work. He told them of God's hand upon him and the king's authorization, then said: "Come, and let us build up the wall of Jerusalem, that we be no more a reproach." The response was immediate: "Let us rise up and build." They strengthened their hands for the good work. Sanballat the Horonite, Tobiah the Ammonite, and Geshem the Arabian heard and mocked: "What is this thing that ye do? will ye rebel against the king?" Nehemiah answered: "The God of heaven, he will prosper us."

## Notes
The secrecy of the night inspection reflects Nehemiah's political prudence — he needed to assess the situation before committing publicly, in an environment of active enemies. The pattern of personal investigation before public declaration is a leadership principle embedded in the narrative. Nehemiah's answer to Sanballat's mockery establishes the theological frame for everything that follows: this is God's project, not merely a civic improvement program.

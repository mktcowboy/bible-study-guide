# Commissioning of Joshua

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Joshua]]
- **Chapters:** Joshua 1
- **Summary:** After [[Death of Moses]], the LORD commissions [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] to lead Israel across the [[Jordan River]] and into [[Canaan]].

## Details
God promises Joshua the same covenant presence given to [[Moses]] and commands him to be strong, courageous, and careful to obey the Law. Joshua then commands the officers and reminds the eastern tribes of their pledge to help conquer the land west of the Jordan.

## Key People Involved
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Commissioned leader
- [[Moses]] — Servant of the LORD whose work Joshua continues

## Key Places
- [[Plains of Moab]] — Israel's staging ground before crossing
- [[Jordan River]] — Boundary Israel must cross
- [[Canaan]] — Land promised to the patriarchs

## Important Verses
> "Have not I commanded thee? Be strong and of a good courage..." — Joshua 1:9

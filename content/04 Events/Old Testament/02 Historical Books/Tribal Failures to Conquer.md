# Tribal Failures to Conquer

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Judges]]
- **Chapters:** Judges 1
- **Summary:** Israel's tribes begin well but repeatedly fail to drive out the inhabitants of the land.

## Details
Judah has early success, but the chapter turns into a catalog of incomplete obedience. Many Canaanites remain among the tribes, setting the stage for compromise and idolatry.

## Key People Involved
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — His death frames the transition
- [[Caleb]] — Earlier faithfulness contrasts with later compromise

## Key Places
- [[Judah (territory)]] — First tribe to go up
- [[Canaan]] — Land not fully possessed

## Important Verses
> "And it came to pass, when Israel was strong, that they put the Canaanites to tribute, and did not utterly drive them out." — Judges 1:28

## Cross-References
- Related events: [[Division of the Land]], [[Angel of the LORD at Bochim]]

## Notes
Judges begins with partial obedience, and partial obedience becomes a doorway to disaster.

# Abiathar Exiled to Anathoth
## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[1 Kings]]
- **Chapters:** 2:26–27
- **Summary:** [[Solomon]] thrusts the priest [[Abiathar]] out to his fields at [[Anathoth]] for supporting Adonijah, fulfilling the LORD's word against the house of Eli.
## Details
When [[Solomon]] secured his throne, he dealt with those who had backed Adonijah's bid for the kingdom. To Abiathar the priest the king said, "Get thee to Anathoth, unto thine own fields; for thou art worthy of death: but I will not at this time put thee to death, because thou barest the ark of the Lord GOD before David my father, and because thou hast been afflicted in all wherein my father was afflicted." Solomon spared Abiathar's life on account of his long faithful service to [[David]], but he removed him from the priesthood and banished him to his hometown of Anathoth in the territory of Benjamin. By this act Solomon thrust out Abiathar from being priest unto the LORD, and the high-priestly line passed to Zadok. The narrator explicitly notes that this fulfilled the word the LORD had spoken at Shiloh concerning the house of [[Eli]], whose descendants were warned that the priesthood would be taken from them.

- [[Solomon]] — King who carried out the sentence

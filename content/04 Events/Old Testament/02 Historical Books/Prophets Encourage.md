# Prophets Encourage

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Ezra]], [[Haggai]]
- **Chapters:** Ezra 5:1-2; Haggai 1-2
- **Summary:** After years of halted construction, the prophets [[Haggai]] and [[Zechariah]] stirred [[Zerubbabel]] and the people to resume building the temple.

## Details
The temple construction had been stopped by official opposition and a Persian stop-work order (Ezra 4). For years — roughly 15-18 years by most reconstructions — the foundation lay unfinished while the returned exiles focused on their own houses. Into this stagnation came two prophets.

Haggai's message was direct and searching: "Is it time for you, O ye, to dwell in your cieled houses, and this house lie waste?" (Haggai 1:4). He connected the community's economic hardship — drought, poor harvests, inflation — directly to their failure to prioritize God's house. The message landed: within three weeks of Haggai's first oracle, Zerubbabel, Jeshua, and the people began work (Haggai 1:14-15).

Zechariah's prophecies came alongside Haggai's, providing the visionary foundation for the renewed work: eight night visions, the messianic "Branch," and the famous word to Zerubbabel in Zechariah 4:6 — "Not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the LORD of hosts." Both prophets' names appear explicitly in Ezra 5:1 and 6:14 as the motivating force behind the completion.

## Key People Involved
- [[Haggai]] — Prophet who challenged the community to resume building
- [[Zechariah]] — Prophet whose visions undergirded the resumed work
- [[Zerubbabel]] — Governor who led the renewed construction
- [[Jeshua]] — High priest who co-led with Zerubbabel

## Notes
- The combination of Haggai (practical challenge) and Zechariah (visionary hope) models a prophetic ministry that is both confrontational about present failure and expansive about future promise.
- Ezra 6:14 says the elders finished the building according to the commandment of the God of Israel and the decree of Cyrus — with the prophets' work named alongside the imperial permission.

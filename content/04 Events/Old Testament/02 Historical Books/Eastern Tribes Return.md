# Eastern Tribes Return

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Joshua]]
- **Chapters:** Joshua 22
- **Summary:** Reuben, Gad, and half-Manasseh return east of the [[Jordan River]] and build a memorial altar that nearly causes civil war.

## Details
The western tribes fear the altar is a rival place of sacrifice. Phinehas and the leaders investigate, and the eastern tribes explain that the altar is a witness, not a substitute sanctuary. The conflict is resolved peacefully.

## Key People Involved
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Dismisses the eastern tribes
- [[Eleazar]] — Father of Phinehas

## Key Places
- [[Jordan River]] — Boundary at the heart of the dispute
- [[Shiloh]] — Worship center of the western tribes

## Important Verses
> "The LORD God of gods, the LORD God of gods, he knoweth..." — Joshua 22:22

## Cross-References
- Related events: [[Division of the Land]], [[Covenant Renewal at Shechem]]

## Notes
The episode shows the danger of assuming rebellion without investigation, and the importance of preserving one worshiping people across geographic distance.

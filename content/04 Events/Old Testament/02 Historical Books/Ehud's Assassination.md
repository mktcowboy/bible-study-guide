# Ehud's Assassination

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Judges]]
- **Chapters:** Judges 3:12-30
- **Summary:** [[Ehud]] kills [[Eglon]] and leads Israel to victory over Moab.

## Details
Ehud brings tribute to Eglon while carrying a hidden dagger on his right thigh. After killing the king in private, Ehud escapes, rallies Israel, and cuts off Moab's escape at the Jordan fords.

## Key People Involved
- [[Ehud]] — Judge and deliverer
- [[Eglon]] — Moabite king

## Key Places
- [[Jordan River]] — Escape route cut off by Israel

## Important Verses
> "Follow after me: for the LORD hath delivered your enemies the Moabites into your hand." — Judges 3:28

## Cross-References
- Related events: [[Cycle of Apostasy]]

## Notes
The story's irony undercuts Moabite power and highlights the LORD's use of unexpected weakness.

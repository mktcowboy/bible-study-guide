# David's Flight

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[1 Samuel]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Samuel 21-27
- **Summary:** [[David]] flees from [[Saul]] through priests' towns, caves, wilderness strongholds, and Philistine territory.

## Details
David's wilderness years include receiving bread at [[Nob]], gathering followers in the [[Cave of Adullam]], sparing Saul at [[En-gedi]], and living for a season at [[Ziklag]]. These chapters form David's testing ground, where suffering shapes the future king without letting bitterness rule him.

## Key People Involved
- [[David]] - Fugitive under pressure
- [[Saul]] - Pursues David
- [[Ahimelech]] - Helps David at Nob
- [[Abigail]] - Redirects David away from revenge

## Key Places
- [[Nob]] - Bread and sword from the priests
- [[Cave of Adullam]] - Gathering place for David's followers
- [[En-gedi]] - David spares Saul
- [[Ziklag]] - Philistine-border refuge

## Notes
David's flight proves his character before it proves his kingship. He learns to wait for God rather than seize what was promised.

# Demand for a King

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[1 Samuel]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Samuel 8
- **Summary:** Israel's elders come to [[Samuel]] at [[Ramah]] and demand a king like the nations.

## Details
Troubled by Samuel's age and his sons' corruption, Israel asks for a human king. The LORD tells Samuel that the request is ultimately a rejection of God's kingship, and Samuel solemnly warns the people what a king will take from them. Still, the people insist.

## Key People Involved
- [[Samuel]] - Warns the nation
- [[Saul]] - The request prepares the way for his rise

## Key Places
- [[Ramah]] - Samuel's base where the elders come

## Important Verses
> "They have not rejected thee, but they have rejected me, that I should not reign over them." - 1 Samuel 8:7

## Cross-References
- Related events: [[Samuel's Judgeship]], [[Saul Anointed]]
- Related books: [[1 Samuel]], [[Deuteronomy]]

## Notes
The demand for a king is not evil simply because of monarchy, but because of motive: Israel wants to be like the nations rather than distinct under God's rule.

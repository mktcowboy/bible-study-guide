# Famine in Bethlehem

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Ruth]]
- **Chapters:** Ruth 1
- **Summary:** A famine in [[Bethlehem]] drives [[Elimelech]], [[Naomi]], and their sons into [[Moab]].

## Details
The opening problem of Ruth is not romance but emptiness. A famine strikes Bethlehem, the "house of bread," and Elimelech's family leaves the covenant land to sojourn in Moab. The move sets the stage for death, widowhood, and the eventual return that God will fill with redemption.

## Key People Involved
- [[Elimelech]] - Leads the household out of Bethlehem
- [[Naomi]] - Leaves with her husband and sons
- [[Mahlon & Chilion]] - Sons taken into exile with the family

## Key Places
- [[Bethlehem]] - Home left behind in famine
- [[Moab]] - Land of sojourning and later grief

## Important Verses
> "There was a famine in the land." - Ruth 1:1

## Notes
Ruth begins with Bethlehem emptied. The whole book will show God turning famine into harvest and loss into fullness.

# Crossing the Jordan

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Joshua]]
- **Chapters:** Joshua 3-4
- **Summary:** Israel crosses the flooded [[Jordan River]] on dry ground as the ark of the covenant goes before them.

## Details
The priests step into the river carrying the ark, and the waters stop upstream. Israel passes over, and twelve stones are set up as a memorial so future generations will remember the LORD's mighty work.

## Key People Involved
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Leads the people
- [[Eleazar]] — High priestly representative in the conquest generation

## Key Places
- [[Jordan River]] — Miraculously parted
- [[Gilgal]] — Memorial stones set up after the crossing

## Important Verses
> "That all the people of the earth might know the hand of the LORD, that it is mighty..." — Joshua 4:24

- Related events: [[Red Sea Crossing]], [[Commissioning of Joshua]]
- Foreshadowed in: [[Death of Moses]]

## Notes
The crossing publicly confirms Joshua's leadership and connects the conquest generation to the Exodus generation.

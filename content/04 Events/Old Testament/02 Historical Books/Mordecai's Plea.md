# Mordecai's Plea

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Esther]]
- **Chapters:** Esther 4
- **Summary:** [[Mordecai]] urged [[Esther (Queen)|Esther]] to risk approaching the king, asking whether she had come to the kingdom for such a time as this.

## Details
After the genocide decree went out, Mordecai tore his clothes, put on sackcloth and ashes, and mourned near the [[King's Gate]]. Esther sent garments to clothe him, but he refused them and sent back the details of the decree.

Mordecai warned Esther not to imagine that life in the palace would save her from the fate of her people. He confessed confidence that deliverance would arise, but pressed the possibility that Esther's position itself was providential. His plea led directly to [[Esther's Fast]].

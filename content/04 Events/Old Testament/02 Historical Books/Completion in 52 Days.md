# Completion in 52 Days

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Nehemiah]]
- **Chapters:** Nehemiah 6:15-16
- **Summary:** The entire wall of Jerusalem was completed in 52 days on the 25th of Elul; when all surrounding nations heard of it, they perceived that this work had been accomplished with the help of God.

## Details
Despite continued threats and attempts to distract or intimidate Nehemiah — Sanballat and Geshem sent messages inviting a conference (a trap), a false prophet tried to lure Nehemiah into the temple to hide, Tobiah sent letters to terrify him — Nehemiah refused every distraction: "I am doing a great work, so that I cannot come down: why should the work cease, whilst I leave it, and come down to you?" He prayed for each threat and continued the work.

The wall was finished on the twenty-fifth day of Elul, in 52 days. The reaction of the enemies was immediate: "And it came to pass, that when all our enemies heard thereof, and all the heathen that were about us saw these things, they were much cast down in their own eyes: for they perceived that this work was wrought of our God." After the wall was completed, Nehemiah assigned gatekeepers, singers, and Levites, and arranged for the city to be populated by assigning one in ten of the people to live in Jerusalem.

## Notes
52 days for rebuilding the walls of an ancient city is remarkably rapid. Josephus (Antiquities 11.5.8) extends the period to two years and four months, which may reflect the total repair time rather than the initial circuit. The theological punchline of chapter 6 is the enemies' recognition that the project was divine: the rapid completion was itself apologetic evidence of God's involvement. Nehemiah's repeated short prayers throughout the narrative ("I prayed to the God of heaven," 2:4; "Remember thou me," 5:19; etc.) model the integration of prayer with practical action.

# Wool Test

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Judges]]
- **Chapters:** Judges 6:36-40
- **Summary:** [[Gideon]] asks for signs with a fleece of wool to confirm that God will save Israel by his hand.

## Details
Gideon first asks that dew fall only on the fleece, then asks that the fleece remain dry while the ground is wet. God patiently grants both signs.

## Key People Involved
- [[Gideon]] — Seeks reassurance

## Key Places
- [[Ophrah]] — Gideon's setting during the call narrative

## Important Verses
> "If thou wilt save Israel by mine hand, as thou hast said..." — Judges 6:36

## Cross-References

## Notes
The fleece is less a general method for decision-making and more a sign of Gideon's hesitant faith.

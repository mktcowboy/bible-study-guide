# Cycle of Apostasy

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Judges]]
- **Chapters:** Judges 2:11-19
- **Summary:** Judges explains Israel's repeated pattern of sin, oppression, crying out, deliverance, and relapse.

## Details
After Joshua's generation dies, Israel serves the Baals. The LORD gives them into enemy hands, then raises judges to deliver them. After each judge dies, the people return to corruption worse than before.

## Key People Involved
- [[Othniel]] — First example of the pattern
- [[Gideon]] — Later example of deliverance and decline
- [[Samson]] — Final major judge in the downward spiral

## Key Places
- [[Canaan]] — Land where the cycle unfolds

## Important Verses
> "And the LORD raised up judges, which delivered them out of the hand of those that spoiled them." — Judges 2:16

## Cross-References
- Related events: [[Angel of the LORD at Bochim]], [[Othniel's Deliverance]]

## Notes
The pattern is not mechanical repentance; the book shows a nation growing more disordered with each turn.

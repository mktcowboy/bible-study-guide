# Abimelech's Usurpation

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Judges]]
- **Chapters:** Judges 9
- **Summary:** [[Abimelech]] murders Gideon's sons and seizes power in [[Shechem]].

## Details
Abimelech gains support from Shechem, kills his brothers, and is made ruler. [[Jotham]] survives and speaks a curse through the parable of the trees. Violence eventually consumes both Abimelech and Shechem.

## Key People Involved
- [[Abimelech]] — Violent ruler
- [[Jotham]] — Surviving son who condemns him
- [[Gideon]] — Father whose legacy is corrupted

## Key Places
- [[Shechem]] — Center of Abimelech's support

## Important Verses
> "Thus God rendered the wickedness of Abimelech..." — Judges 9:56

## Cross-References
- Related events: [[Gideon's Ephod]]

## Notes
Abimelech is not called a judge; his story is a warning about kingship without covenant righteousness.

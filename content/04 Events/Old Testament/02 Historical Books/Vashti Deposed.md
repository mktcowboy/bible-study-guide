# Vashti Deposed

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Esther]]
- **Chapters:** Esther 1
- **Summary:** Queen Vashti refused King Ahasuerus's command to appear before his banquet guests; she was removed from her royal position, creating the vacancy that Esther would fill.

## Details
In the third year of his reign, the Persian King Ahasuerus (Xerxes I, reigned 486-465 BC) hosted a grand banquet in Susa lasting 180 days to display his wealth and glory, followed by a seven-day feast for all the people of the citadel. On the seventh day, when the king was merry with wine, he commanded his seven chamberlains to bring Queen Vashti before him wearing her royal crown, to display her beauty to the people and princes. She refused to come at the king's command, and the king was furious.

He consulted his seven princes, asking what was to be done with Vashti for her defiance. Memucan advised that Vashti had wronged not only the king but all the princes and peoples of every province — for when word spread, all women would despise their husbands. He recommended that a royal decree be issued removing Vashti from her position as queen and giving it to another more worthy. The king accepted this counsel; royal letters were sent throughout the empire declaring that "every man should bear rule in his own house." Vashti's refusal thus set the stage for the beauty search that would bring Esther to the throne.

## Notes
Vashti's refusal has been interpreted in multiple ways in the history of interpretation. Some have seen her as admirably dignified — refusing to be paraded before drunken men. Others see the narrative making no moral judgment; Vashti simply functions as the necessary removal that opens the door for Esther. The book of Esther is notable for not explicitly mentioning God — Providence works through ordinary events, royal decrees, and human decisions. Vashti's deposition is the first of these providential pivots.

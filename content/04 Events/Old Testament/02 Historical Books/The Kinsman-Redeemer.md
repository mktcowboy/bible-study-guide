# The Kinsman-Redeemer

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Ruth]]
- **Chapters:** Ruth 4
- **Summary:** [[Boaz]] secures the right of redemption at [[The Gates of Bethlehem]] after [[The Closer Kinsman]] declines.

## Details
Boaz meets the nearer kinsman before the elders at the gate and presents the legal obligation tied to Naomi's land and Ruth's marriage. When the nearer relative refuses, Boaz redeems the inheritance himself and publicly takes Ruth as his wife. The scene joins law, mercy, and lineage in one covenant act.

## Key People Involved
- [[Boaz]] - Redeemer who acts
- [[The Closer Kinsman]] - Declines the obligation
- [[Ruth (Person)|Ruth]] - Taken under Boaz's redeeming care
- [[Naomi]] - Her inheritance and family line are restored

## Key Places
- [[The Gates of Bethlehem]] - Site of the legal transaction
- [[Bethlehem]] - Community that witnesses the redemption

## Notes
This is the legal and theological climax of Ruth. What began with famine and death is answered by a redeemer who is willing, able, and publicly committed.

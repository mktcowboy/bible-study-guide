# Haman's Rise

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Esther]]
- **Chapters:** Esther 3
- **Summary:** [[Haman]] was promoted above the king's princes, demanded honor, and turned [[Mordecai]]'s refusal to bow into a plot against all Jews.

## Details
After Esther became queen, [[Ahasuerus]] advanced Haman and set his seat above the other officials. The king's servants bowed before Haman, but Mordecai refused. When Haman learned that Mordecai was a Jew, his anger expanded beyond one man into a plan to destroy Mordecai's people throughout [[Persia]].

Haman cast Pur, the lot, to select the day of destruction and then persuaded the king to authorize a decree against the Jews. His rise therefore becomes the beginning of the book's central crisis.

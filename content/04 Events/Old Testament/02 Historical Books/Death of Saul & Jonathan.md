# Death of Saul & Jonathan

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[1 Samuel]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Samuel 31
- **Summary:** [[Saul]] and [[Jonathan]] die in battle on [[Mount Gilboa]], ending Saul's reign and opening the way to David's kingship.

## Details
The Philistines press hard against Israel, Jonathan and Saul's sons fall, and Saul, mortally wounded, falls on his own sword. The event closes a tragic reign and leaves the nation in grief. It also creates the transition point into 2 Samuel.

## Key People Involved
- [[Saul]] - First king of Israel, dies in battle
- [[Jonathan]] - Loyal son and friend of David
- [[Abner]] - Military figure tied to the house of Saul

## Key Places
- [[Mount Gilboa]] - Battlefield of Saul's final defeat

## Important Verses
> "So Saul died, and his three sons..." - 1 Samuel 31:6

## Notes
1 Samuel ends without triumph. Saul's death is tragic, Jonathan's death is grievous, and the kingdom passes forward through sorrow rather than celebration.

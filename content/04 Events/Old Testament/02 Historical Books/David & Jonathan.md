# David & Jonathan

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[1 Samuel]]
- **Chapters:** 1 Samuel 18-20
- **Summary:** [[Jonathan]] binds himself to [[David]] in covenant friendship and helps him escape Saul's hostility.

## Details
Jonathan recognizes God's hand on David and loves him as his own soul. Their covenant stands in contrast to Saul's jealousy and fear. Jonathan's willingness to strengthen David, even at the cost of his own succession, marks one of Scripture's clearest examples of loyal friendship.

## Key People Involved
- [[David]] - Future king under threat
- [[Jonathan]] - Loyal friend and covenant partner
- [[Saul]] - Jealous father and king

## Key Places
- [[Ramah]] - Samuel's sphere during the broader conflict

## Important Verses
> "Then Jonathan and David made a covenant, because he loved him as his own soul." - 1 Samuel 18:3

## Notes
Jonathan's friendship is remarkable because it is sacrificial. He protects David even when David's rise means Jonathan's own crown will never come.

# Southern Campaign

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Joshua]]
- **Chapters:** Joshua 10
- **Summary:** After defending [[Gibeon]], Joshua conquers key southern cities in [[Canaan]].

## Details
The five Amorite kings are captured and executed, then Israel strikes Makkedah, Libnah, Lachish, Eglon, Hebron, Debir, and the surrounding region. The text emphasizes that the LORD God of Israel fought for Israel.

## Key People Involved
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Commands the campaign
- [[The Gibeonites]] — Their treaty triggers the southern coalition

## Key Places
- [[Gibeon]] — Coalition target
- [[Hebron]] — Southern city taken
- [[Canaan]] — Land being subdued

## Important Verses
> "And all these kings and their land did Joshua take at one time, because the LORD God of Israel fought for Israel." — Joshua 10:42

## Notes
The southern campaign widens the conquest from a single rescue battle into regional judgment.

# Vision of the Plumb Line

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Amos]]
- **Chapters:** Amos 7:7–17
- **Summary:** The LORD stands with a plumb line beside a wall, measuring Israel and finding it crooked, declaring "I will not pass by them any more"; the vision is immediately followed by Amaziah the priest of Bethel commanding Amos to leave Israel, and Amos's bold response.

## Details

The vision of the plumb line is the third of five visions in Amos 7–9. The first two visions — locusts and fire — end with Amos interceding and the LORD relenting: "The LORD repented for this: It shall not be, saith the LORD" (7:3, 6). The plumb line vision is structurally different: it ends without intercession and without reprieve. "The LORD said, Amos, what seest thou? And I said, A plumb line. Then said the LORD, Behold, I will set a plumb line in the midst of my people Israel: I will not again pass by them any more" (7:8). The plumb line — an instrument of measurement, held against a wall to determine whether it is straight and true — implies a standard by which Israel is being evaluated. The wall that seemed solid is revealed to be out of plumb; there is no salvaging it.

The specific consequences are stated without softening: "the high places of Isaac shall be desolate, and the sanctuaries of Israel shall be laid waste; and I will rise against the house of Jeroboam with the sword" (7:9). It is this last phrase — the threat against Jeroboam's house — that triggers the confrontation in the narrative immediately following. Amaziah, the priest of [[Bethel]], sends word to Jeroboam II: "Amos hath conspired against thee in the midst of the house of Israel: the land is not able to bear all his words" (7:10). He then turns to Amos directly and tells him to "flee thee away into the land of Judah, and there eat bread, and prophesy there: But prophesy not again any more at Bethel: for it is the king's chapel, and it is the king's court" (7:12–13).

Amos's response is one of the most memorable statements of prophetic authority in the OT: "I was no prophet, neither was I a prophet's son; but I was an herdman, and a gatherer of sycomore fruit: And the LORD took me as I followed the flock, and the LORD said unto me, Go, prophesy unto my people Israel" (7:14–15). He claims no institutional standing, no prophetic school affiliation, no professional credentials — only the direct commission of God. His authority derives entirely from that commission. He then pronounces specific judgment on Amaziah himself: his wife will become a harlot in the city, his sons and daughters will fall by the sword, his land divided, and he himself will die in a polluted land (7:17). The priest who tried to silence the plumb line becomes the immediate object of its verdict.

## Key People Involved
- [[Amos (Prophet)|Amos]] — the shepherd-prophet who receives the vision and refuses to be silenced by Amaziah
- [[Amaziah]] — priest of Bethel; attempts to expel Amos; receives a personal judgment oracle in response
- [[Jeroboam II]] — king whose house is threatened; the political authority behind Amaziah's expulsion order

## Key Places
- [[Bethel]] — the royal sanctuary where the confrontation occurs; "the king's chapel and the king's court"
- [[Samaria]] — the northern kingdom capital, broader context of the judgment announced
- [[Tekoa]] — Amos's homeland; the place Amaziah tells him to return to

# Joshua the High Priest Clothed

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Zechariah]]
- **Chapters:** Zechariah 3
- **Summary:** In Zechariah's fourth night vision, the high priest Joshua stands in filthy garments before the Angel of the LORD while Satan accuses him; the LORD rebukes Satan and clothes Joshua in clean robes, accompanied by a "BRANCH" prophecy pointing to the coming Messiah.

## Details
The fourth of Zechariah's eight night visions is the most theologically direct. The prophet sees Joshua the high priest—the same Jeshua who stood beside Zerubbabel in the building project—standing before the Angel of the LORD. His garments are filthy, representing the defilement of the priesthood and the nation. At his right hand stands Satan, "the adversary," in the act of accusing him. The LORD's response is immediate and decisive: not vindication of Joshua's personal righteousness, but an act of sovereign grace rooted in God's own election. "And the LORD said unto Satan, The LORD rebuke thee, O Satan; even the LORD that hath chosen Jerusalem rebuke thee: is not this a brand plucked out of the fire?"

The imagery of a brand plucked from fire recalls the nearness of destruction from which Israel had been delivered. The returned exiles were not a triumphant people but a rescued remnant, barely snatched from the consuming judgment of exile. On this basis—not merit but mercy—the LORD commands the filthy garments removed: "Take away the filthy garments from him. And unto him he said, Behold, I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I will clothe thee with change of raiment." Zechariah himself calls for a clean turban to be set on Joshua's head. The scene enacts a symbolic act of priestly re-consecration: Joshua, representing the whole nation, is pronounced clean by divine declaration, not by ritual purification.

The vision then moves to a conditional word for Joshua (faithfulness in his priestly calling will grant him access among the angels standing by) and to an unconditional messianic promise: "Behold, I will bring forth my servant the BRANCH." The "BRANCH" (Hebrew tsemach) is a recurring messianic title in the prophets, used also in Isaiah 4:2, Jeremiah 23:5, and Zechariah 6:12. The accompanying sign is a single stone with seven eyes laid before Joshua, on which the LORD will engrave an inscription and remove the iniquity of the land in a single day. Christian interpreters have read the removal of iniquity in one day as a preview of the atoning work of the cross. The vision holds together priestly justification, satanic accusation, divine rebuke, costly cleansing, and messianic hope in a remarkably compressed narrative.

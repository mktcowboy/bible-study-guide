# Valley of Decision

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Joel]]
- **Chapters:** Joel 3
- **Summary:** God summons all nations to the Valley of Jehoshaphat for final judgment, indicting them for scattering Israel and dividing the land, with the harvest imagery — "Multitudes in the valley of decision" — marking the ultimate reckoning before the LORD dwells in Zion.

## Details

Joel 3 opens with a declaration that "in those days, and in that time" — the same era as the Spirit outpouring — God will reverse the fortunes of Judah and Jerusalem. The chapter immediately turns to the nations. All nations are summoned to the Valley of Jehoshaphat, a location whose name in Hebrew means "the LORD judges" and which has been associated since antiquity with the Kidron Valley east of Jerusalem. The charge against them is specific: "they have scattered my people among the nations, and parted my land" (3:2), and they have traded Judah's sons and daughters to the Greeks for wine and harlotry (3:3). Tyre, Sidon, and Philistia are called out by name for their slave trade in Israelites (3:4–8), and the word of reciprocity is given: their sons and daughters will be sold to the distant Sabeans (3:8).

The summons to judgment takes on the imagery of military preparation — "Beat your plowshares into swords, and your pruninghooks into spears" (3:10) — the deliberate inversion of the peacetime images of Isaiah 2:4 and Micah 4:3. This reversal underscores that the nations are being called not to peace but to judgment. The language that follows is that of the harvest: "Put ye in the sickle, for the harvest is ripe: come, get you down; for the press is full, the fats overflow; for their wickedness is great" (3:13). The winepress imagery will reappear in Revelation 14:14–20 as a New Testament image of final judgment.

The pivotal verse of the chapter is 3:14: "Multitudes, multitudes in the valley of decision: for the day of the LORD is near in the valley of decision." The "valley of decision" (*charuts*, meaning "threshing" or "decisive cutting") is the same valley of divine judgment, now named for the definitive verdict God will render. The cosmic signs recur (3:15 — sun and moon darkened, stars withdraw), after which "the LORD also shall roar out of Zion, and utter his voice from Jerusalem" (3:16). The chapter closes in complete contrast: while the nations face judgment, "Judah shall dwell for ever, and Jerusalem from generation to generation" (3:20), and "the LORD dwelleth in Zion" (3:21).

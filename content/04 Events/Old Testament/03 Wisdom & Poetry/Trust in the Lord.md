# Trust in the Lord

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Proverbs]]
- **Chapters:** Proverbs 3:1-12
- **Summary:** Solomon instructs his son to bind mercy and truth around his neck and write them on his heart, to trust wholly in the LORD rather than his own understanding, and to acknowledge God in all his ways. The passage further calls for honouring the LORD with firstfruits and for receiving divine chastening as a mark of the Father's love.

## Significance
These twelve verses form one of the most concentrated statements of covenant trust in all of Scripture. The command to "lean not unto thine own understanding" strikes at the root of human pride and self-sufficiency, directing the heart back to dependence on divine wisdom rather than worldly prudence. The promise that God will "direct thy paths" anchors the entire practical ethic of Proverbs in a relational walk with the LORD.

## Key Verses
> "Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding. In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths." — Proverbs 3:5-6 (KJV)

> "Honour the LORD with thy substance, and with the firstfruits of all thine increase: so shall thy barns be filled with plenty, and thy presses shall burst out with new wine." — Proverbs 3:9-10 (KJV)

> "For whom the LORD loveth he correcteth; even as a father the son in whom he delighteth." — Proverbs 3:12 (KJV)

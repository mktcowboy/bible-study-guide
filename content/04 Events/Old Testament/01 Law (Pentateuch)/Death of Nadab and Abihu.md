# Death of Nadab and Abihu

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Leviticus]]
- **Chapters:** 10
- **Summary:** Nadab and Abihu offered unauthorized ("strange") fire before the Lord, and divine fire consumed them.

## Details
On the very first day of Aaron's priestly ministry, his eldest sons Nadab and Abihu each took their censers, put fire in them, and added incense — but it was "strange fire, which the LORD commanded them not." Fire came out from the presence of the Lord and consumed them, and they died before the Lord (Leviticus 10:1–2). God then instructed Moses and Aaron: "Among your own kin you shall lament, but outside the camp shall you go" (10:4). Aaron's silence in response (10:3) showed acceptance of God's judgment. This event established that worship must be conducted exactly as God prescribes — not with human innovation. Moses then commanded the surviving priests not to drink wine and beer while ministering (10:8–11), and instructed them on clean and unclean animals (10:12–15).

## Notes
This shocking event teaches that holiness in worship is non-negotiable. God is not impressed by enthusiasm without obedience. The text does not explain WHY the fire was "strange" — perhaps wrong fire, wrong incense, wrong timing, or wrong attitude — leaving the principle clear: God's ways, not ours.

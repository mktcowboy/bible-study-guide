# Bronze Serpent

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** 21:4–9
- **Summary:** Israelites complain against God and Moses; God sends fiery serpents; thousands die; God provides healing through a bronze serpent on a pole.

## Details
After the people grew weary of the tedious way and despised the "bread of heaven" (manna), God sent "fiery serpents" among them, and many Israelites were bitten and died. The people came to Moses in repentance: "We have sinned, because we have spoken against the LORD and against you." Moses prayed for the people, and the Lord instructed him: "Make a fiery serpent, and set it on a standard; and it shall come to pass, that everyone who is bitten, when he sees it, shall live." Moses made a bronze serpent and put it on a pole, and anyone who looked at it lived. This simple act of faith — looking at a symbol of their judgment — brought healing. The bronze serpent remained as a national memorial until the time of King Hezekiah, who destroyed it because the people had begun burning incense to it (called Nehushtan, "the bronze thing").

## Key People Involved
- [[Moses]] — Made the bronze serpent at God's command
- [[Israel]] (the people) — Complained, were bitten, looked and were healed

## Key Places
- Wilderness journey — Between Kadesh and Mount Hor

## Notes
One of the most Christ-centered passages in the Pentateuch. The serpent that brought death became, when lifted up by God, the means of life. The remedy for the bite was to look at the symbol of the judgment — parallel to the cross, where the punishment for sin becomes the means of salvation.

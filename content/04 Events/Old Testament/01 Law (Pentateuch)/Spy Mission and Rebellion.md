# Spy Mission and Rebellion

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** 13–14
- **Summary:** Twelve spies scout Canaan for 40 days; ten bring a fearful report, Caleb and Joshua urge faith; the people rebel and are sentenced to 40 years of wandering.

## Details
At the request of the people, [[Moses]] sent one spy from each tribe to scout the land of Canaan from the wilderness of Zin through [[Red Sea]] to Rehob. For forty days, they explored from Shirim to [[Hazor]] to [[En-mizpah]]. They returned with a cluster of grapes so large it required two men to carry it on a pole. All twelve agreed the land "flowed with milk and honey," but ten spies gave a terrifying report: "We cannot attack the people, for they are stronger than we... all the people whom we saw in our midst are men of great height... we seemed to ourselves like grasshoppers" (13:31–33). Only [[Caleb]] and [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] urged immediate conquest, trusting God's promise. The people wept, murmured, and even chose to elect a new leader to take them back to Egypt. God judged them severely: that generation would die in the wilderness, one year for each day of spying, and only their children would enter the land. Caleb and Joshua would enter. The Lord swore that the rebellious generation would not see the rest He gave Israel.

## Notes
This is the defining moment of the wilderness generation — a choice between faith and fear. The forty days of spying produced forty years of wandering. Caleb's faith ("Let us go up at once and inherit the land") contrasts with the ten spies' fear.

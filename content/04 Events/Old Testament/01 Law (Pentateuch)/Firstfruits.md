# Firstfruits

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both
- **Book:** [[Leviticus]], [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** Leviticus 23:9-14; Numbers 28:26
- **Summary:** The presentation of the first sheaf of the barley harvest to God on the day after the Sabbath during Unleavened Bread; a pledge that the full harvest was God's.

## Details
The Feast of Firstfruits was a single-day observance on the day after the weekly Sabbath that fell within the Feast of Unleavened Bread. The priest waved a sheaf of the firstfruits of the harvest before the LORD, accompanied by a burnt offering, grain offering, and drink offering. No bread from the new crop could be eaten until the firstfruits were presented.

The offering acknowledged that the entire harvest — and the agricultural life it sustained — was God's gift. By presenting the firstfruits, Israel dedicated the whole to God before consuming any of it.

Paul explicitly applies this to the resurrection of Christ in 1 Corinthians 15:20-23: "But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them that slept." Christ's resurrection is the wave offering — the first presented to the Father — guaranteeing the full harvest of resurrection for all who belong to him.

## Notes
- The "firstfruits" image extends throughout Paul's letters: the Spirit is the firstfruits of the new creation (Romans 8:23); Christ is the firstfruits of the resurrection.
- The traditional dating placed Christ's resurrection on the day of the Feast of Firstfruits, making the connection explicit.

# Covenant Renewal

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Deuteronomy]]
- **Chapters:** 29–30
- **Summary:** Moses makes a new covenant with the generation standing at the border of Canaan, calling them to faithfulness and promising restoration after rebellion.

## Details
Moses formally renewed the covenant that was made with the old generation at [[Mount Sinai]]. He emphasized that this new generation — who had not been party to the original covenant — were now entering a binding agreement with God. "You stand today, all of you, before the LORD your God... that you may enter into a covenant with the LORD your God" (29:1, 10–11). Moses warned that even those not present — future generations, even a "brother" who might be far away — were included in this covenant (29:14–21). He then laid out a stunning prophecy: Israel would inevitably rebel, fall into idolatry, be scattered among the nations, but eventually "return to the LORD your God, and obey His voice... and the LORD will restore your fortunes and have compassion on you, and gather you again from all the nations" (30:1–3). This passage is one of the most hopeful in the entire Old Testament — rebellion is real, but so is restoration.

## Key People Involved
- [[Moses]] — Mediator of the covenant
- Heads of Israel, elders, officers — Representatives of the people
- "The little ones" and strangers — All included in the covenant

## Important Verses
- Deuteronomy 29:10–11 — "You stand today... that you may enter into a covenant with the LORD your God, which the LORD makes with you this day."
- Deuteronomy 30:1–3 — "And it shall come about when all these things come upon you... that you will return to the LORD your God, and obey His voice... and the LORD will restore your fortunes."
- Deuteronomy 30:6 — "The LORD your God will circumcise your heart and the heart of your descendants, to love the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul."

## Notes
This covenant renewal is the bridge between the Law and the New Covenant. The promise of heart circumcision (30:6) finds its fulfillment in the New Birth. The pattern of rebellion → exile → restoration foreshadows the entire biblical narrative.

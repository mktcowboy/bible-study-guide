# First Census

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** Numbers 1–4
- **Summary:** God commanded [[Moses]] to count all fighting men of Israel; the total came to 603,550, organized by tribe and camp arrangement around the Tabernacle.

## Details
At Sinai, one month after the Tabernacle was erected, God commanded Moses and Aaron to take a census of all men twenty years old and above who were able to go to war — excluding the Levites, who were set apart for Tabernacle service. Each tribe appointed a leader to assist, and the count proceeded tribe by tribe.

The total of 603,550 fighting men (excluding Levi) established the military capacity of Israel before the wilderness march. The Levites were separately counted (22,000 males) and assigned to carry and care for the Tabernacle. The census also established the camp layout: three tribes per side surrounding the Tabernacle, with the Levites closest to it, forming a sacred buffer around the presence of God.

Numbers 1-4 thus present Israel not merely as a migrating people but as a disciplined military camp organized around the dwelling place of God. Order, rank, and assignment all reflect the holiness of God at the center.

## Notes
- The tribe of Levi was excluded from military census and instead assigned as guardians of the Tabernacle — a pattern of sacred exclusivity.
- The total of 603,550 is debated by scholars; some read the Hebrew to indicate something other than 600,000+ individuals.
- The organized camp around the Tabernacle is a key image of God as the center of Israel's national life.

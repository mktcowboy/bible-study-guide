# Joshua Appointed

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Deuteronomy]]
- **Chapters:** 31, 34
- **Summary:** Moses publicly commissions Joshua as his successor, lays hands on him, and God promises Joshua leadership as He had with Moses.

## Details
In Deuteronomy 31, Moses called [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] and commanded him in the hearing of all Israel: "Be strong and courageous. For you must go with this people to the land which the LORD has sworn to their fathers to give them, and you shall cause them to inherit it." Moses stood in the doorway of the tabernacle and gave the command. The text says "Joshua the son of Nun was full of the spirit of wisdom, for Moses had laid his hands on him; and the children of Israel listened to him" (34:9). God had previously told Moses, "Behold, [[Aaron]] will die... and Joshua shall enter there" (31:2). God also commanded Moses and Joshua to appear before the priest [[Eleazar]] at the tabernacle, where Eleazar would inquire for them using the Urim. God warned Moses that after his death, the people would "whore after foreign gods" and break the covenant, which is why the Song of Moses was written as a witness. The book of the Law was placed beside the Ark of the Covenant as a permanent witness.

## Notes
The transition from Moses to Joshua is one of the great leadership transitions in Scripture. The Hebrew names are nearly identical — Mosheh (Moses) and Yehoshua (Joshua). Joshua's name means "Yahweh saves" — the same meaning as "Jesus." The pattern of divine calling, empowerment, and public commissioning establishes a template for all biblical leadership succession.

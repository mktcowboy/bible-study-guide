# Esau's Birth and Birthright
## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]]
- **Chapters:** 25:21–34
- **Summary:** [[Rebekah]] bears twins, [[Esau]] and [[Jacob]]; the LORD declares the elder shall serve the younger, and Esau later despises and sells his birthright to Jacob for a meal of pottage.
## Details
Isaac entreated the LORD for his barren wife [[Rebekah]], and she conceived twins who struggled within her. When she inquired of the LORD, he answered that two nations were in her womb, that the one people would be stronger than the other, and that the elder should serve the younger. At birth the first came out red and hairy and was named [[Esau]]; the second came forth grasping his brother's heel and was named [[Jacob]]. Esau grew to be a cunning hunter and a man of the field, loved by Isaac, while Jacob was a plain man dwelling in tents, loved by Rebekah. One day Esau came in from the field faint with hunger and found Jacob boiling pottage. He begged for the red stew, and Jacob required his birthright in exchange. Esau, saying he was at the point to die and asking what profit the birthright was to him, swore and sold it. He ate and drank and rose up and went his way; thus Esau despised his birthright.

## Notes
Esau is held up as a profane man who sold his lasting inheritance for a single meal, while God's sovereign choice of the younger sets the pattern of grace over natural birthright.

# Peace Offering

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Leviticus]]
- **Chapters:** Leviticus 3; 7:11-36
- **Summary:** A fellowship offering expressing communion with God; the fat went to God on the altar, the breast and right thigh to the priests, and the rest to the worshipper for a communal meal.

## Details
The peace offering (Hebrew: *shelamim*, from *shalom*) was the only major sacrifice where the worshipper ate a portion. After the blood was sprinkled and the fat burned on the altar as God's portion, and the breast and thigh given to the priests, the worshipper and family enjoyed a feast from the meat — a communal meal in the presence of God. This made it an expression of restored and celebrated fellowship.

Three types of peace offerings are described in Leviticus 7: the *todah* (thank offering), the *neder* (vow offering), and the *nedavah* (freewill offering). The thank offering had to be eaten the same day; vow and freewill offerings could be eaten the day of and the day after.

The peace offering's structure — God receives his portion, the mediating priests receive theirs, and the people feast — pictures the fullness of covenant relationship. All three parties share in the sacrifice.

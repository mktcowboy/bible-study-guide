# Death of Miriam

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** Numbers 20:1
- **Summary:** In the first month of the fortieth year of Israel's wilderness wandering, the congregation arrived at Kadesh in the wilderness of Zin, and there Miriam died and was buried. The notice is brief — a single verse — yet it marks the passing of one of Israel's three sibling leaders alongside [[Moses]] and [[Aaron]]. Immediately following her death the community erupted in conflict over the absence of water, and it was in the same place that [[Moses]] struck the rock at Meribah and was disqualified from entering Canaan.

## Significance
Miriam had served as prophetess and worship leader from the time she watched over the infant [[Moses]] in the bulrushes of Egypt through the triumphant Song of the Sea after the [[Red Sea Crossing]]. Her death at Kadesh, without ceremony or mourning recorded in the text, stands in stark contrast to the elaborate public grief that would follow the deaths of both [[Aaron]] and [[Moses]]. The proximity of her death to the Meribah crisis has led many readers to see a connection between the loss of her prophetic leadership and the community's sudden and desperate fracturing, suggesting her presence had been a stabilising force throughout the years in the wilderness.

## Key Verses

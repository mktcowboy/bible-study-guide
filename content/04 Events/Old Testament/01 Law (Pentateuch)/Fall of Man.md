# Fall of Man

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]] 3
- **Summary:** [[Adam]] and [[Eve]] disobey God by eating the forbidden fruit, bringing sin, shame, and death into the world.

## Details
The serpent, described as "more subtil than any beast of the field," tempted [[Eve]] by questioning God's word and lying — "Ye shall not surely die." Eve saw that the tree was good for food, pleasant to the eyes, and desirable for wisdom, and ate. She gave to [[Adam]], and he ate.

Immediate consequences:
- Eyes opened — they knew they were naked
- Shame — sewed fig leaves
- Fear of God — hid themselves
- God confronted them — each tried to blame someone else
- Judgment pronounced on serpent, woman, and man
- Expelled from [[Eden]]
- God clothed them in animal skins (first sacrifice/atonement)

Despite judgment, God promised a Redeemer: "It shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel" (Genesis 3:15) — the proto-evangelium, first gospel promise.

## Key People Involved
- [[Adam]] — Ate the fruit, blamed Eve
- [[Eve]] — Tempted by serpent, ate first
- The serpent — Identified as Satan (Revelation 12:9, 20:2)

## Key Places
- [[Eden]] — Where the Fall occurred

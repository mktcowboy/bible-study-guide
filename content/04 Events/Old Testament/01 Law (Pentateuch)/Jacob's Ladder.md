# Jacob's Ladder

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]] 28:10-22
- **Summary:** Fleeing from [[Esau]], [[Jacob]] dreams of a stairway reaching to heaven with angels ascending and descending. God reaffirms the covenant.

## Details
Jacob had just deceived his father and stolen his brother's blessing. Fleeing to [[Haran]], he stopped at an unnamed place, used a stone as a pillow, and dreamed. A stairway (Hebrew: semikhah — "leaned") connected earth and heaven, with angels moving both ways.

God stood above it and reaffirmed the Abrahamic covenant — land, descendants, blessing to all nations, and His personal presence: "I am with thee, and will keep thee."

Jacob awoke in awe: "Surely the LORD is in this place; and I knew it not." He named the place [[Bethel]] ("house of God"), set up the stone as a pillar, poured oil on it, and made a vow.

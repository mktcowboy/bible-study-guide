# Burning Bush

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Exodus]] 3:1 — 4:17
- **Summary:** God appears to [[Moses]] in a bush that burns but is not consumed, calling him to deliver Israel from Egypt.

## Details
While Moses was tending Jethro's flock in the desert of [[Mount Sinai]] (Horeb), he saw a bush burning with fire that was not consumed. God called from the bush, identifying Himself as the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. He revealed He had seen Israel's affliction and would deliver them.

God gave Moses His divine name: "I AM THAT I AM" (Yahweh). Moses objected five times — "who am I?", "who is the LORD?", "they won't believe me", "I can't speak well", "send someone else." God answered each objection with signs (staff to serpent, leprous hand, water to blood) and appointed [[Aaron]] as spokesperson.

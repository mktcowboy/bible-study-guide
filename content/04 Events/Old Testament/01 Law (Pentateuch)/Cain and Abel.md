# Cain and Abel

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]] 4
- **Summary:** [[Cain]] murders his brother [[Abel]] out of jealousy after God accepts Abel's offering but rejects Cain's.

## Details
[[Cain]], a tiller of the ground, brought fruit of the ground as an offering. [[Abel]], a keeper of sheep, brought the firstlings of his flock and their fat portions. God had respect for Abel's offering but not for Cain's.

God warned Cain: "If thou doest well, shalt thou not be accepted? and if thou doest not well, sin croucheth at the door." Cain refused to repent and murdered Abel in the field.

God asked "Where is Abel thy brother?" — Cain's famous reply: "Am I my brother's keeper?" God cursed Cain from the ground and banished him to Nod, but placed a protective mark on him.

## Key People Involved
- [[Cain]] — First murderer
- [[Abel]] — First martyr
- [[Adam]] & [[Eve]] — Parents, grieved

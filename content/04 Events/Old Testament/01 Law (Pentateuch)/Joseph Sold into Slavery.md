# Joseph Sold into Slavery

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]] 37
- **Summary:** [[Joseph]]'s brothers, jealous of their father's favoritism, sell him into slavery to Ishmaelite traders for 20 shekels of silver.

## Details
[[Jacob]] favored [[Joseph]], giving him a "coat of many colors" (Hebrew: ketonet passim — ornate, long-sleeved robe). At 17, Joseph had dreams of supremacy. When he reported them to his brothers, their hatred intensified.

When Joseph went to check on them at Dothan, they stripped him of his coat, threw him in a pit, then sold him to Ishmaelite/Midianite traders for 20 shekels of silver. They dipped his coat in goat's blood and showed it to Jacob, who mourned, believing a wild animal had torn him.

Joseph was taken to [[Egypt]], where he was sold to Potiphar, captain of the guard.

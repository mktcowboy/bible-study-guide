# Death of Moses

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Deuteronomy]]
- **Chapters:** 34
- **Summary:** Moses climbs [[Mount Nebo]], views the Promised Land one final time, dies at age 120 with perfect health, and is buried by God Himself in an unknown location.

## Details
In his final act, Moses left the [[Plains of Moab]] and climbed Mount Nebo, specifically Pisgah Peak. From this vantage point, God showed him all of Canaan — from [[Gilgal]] to Dan, all of Ephraim, all of Judah, the south, and the western sea. God reminded him this was the land He swore to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, but "of that land you may not set foot" (34:4). Moses, the servant of the Lord, died there at the age of 120 years. Remarkably, "his eye was not dim, nor his natural force abated" — he died at the peak of physical vitality. God Himself buried him in a valley in the land of Moab, opposite Beth-peor, "but no man knows of his sepulchre unto this day" (34:6). The text notes that "there has not arisen a prophet since in Israel like Moses, whom the LORD knew face to face" (34:10). After Moses' death, Israel mourned for thirty days — the complete period of mourning — and then the days of weeping were ended.

## Notes
Moses' death is unique in Scripture — God Himself buried him. His unknown grave prevented idolatrous veneration. The statement that no prophet like Moses has arisen is both a compliment and a prophecy — Jesus, who also knew God "face to face," fulfills and surpasses Moses. Moses dies not in defeat but in strength, having completed his mission.

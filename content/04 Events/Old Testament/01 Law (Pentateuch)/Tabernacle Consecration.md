# Tabernacle Consecration

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Leviticus]]
- **Chapters:** 8–9
- **Summary:** Aaron and his sons are consecrated as priests, the first sacrifices are offered, and divine fire consumes the offerings.

## Details
On the first day of the first month of the second year after the exodus, [[Moses]] carried out the seven-day consecration of [[Aaron]] and his sons ([[Nadab]], [[Abihu]], [[Eleazar]], and [[Ithamar]]). Moses washed them, clothed them in priestly garments, offered a sin offering (bull), a burnt offering (two rams — one for consecration, one burnt outside), and an ordaining offering. He put consecrated oil and blood on Aaron and his garments seven times. On the eighth day, Aaron offered all five types of sacrifices on behalf of himself and the people. Fire came out from before the Lord and consumed the burnt offering and fat on the altar, and the people saw it and shouted, and fell on their faces (Leviticus 9:23–24).

## Notes
This event marks the beginning of organized worship in Israel. The divine fire accepting the offerings confirmed God's approval of the priesthood and sacrificial system.

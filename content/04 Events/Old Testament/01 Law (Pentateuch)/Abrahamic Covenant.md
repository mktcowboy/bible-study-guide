# Abrahamic Covenant

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]]
- **Chapters:** Genesis 12:1-3; 15; 17; 22:15-18
- **Summary:** God made an unconditional covenant with [[Abraham]], promising land, a great nation, and blessing to all families of the earth through his seed.

## Details
The covenant unfolds in stages. God's first promise came at Abraham's initial call from [[Ur]] (Genesis 12:1-3): leave your homeland, and I will make you a great nation, bless you, and through you bless all the families of the earth. Abraham obeyed without knowing where he was going.

The formal covenant was ratified in Genesis 15 in a ceremony where God alone passed between the animal pieces while Abraham slept — signaling that fulfillment depended entirely on God, not on Abraham's obedience. God swore by himself, promising the land from the river of Egypt to the Euphrates and descendants as numerous as the stars.

In Genesis 17 the covenant was renewed with the sign of circumcision and with the name change from Abram to Abraham ("father of a multitude"). Isaac is confirmed as the covenant heir. The covenant climaxes in Genesis 22:15-18 after the [[Binding of Isaac]], where God swears an oath that through Abraham's seed all nations will be blessed.

## Notes
- The covenant is unconditional — God alone walks between the pieces in Genesis 15, making the promise dependent on his faithfulness, not Abraham's.
- Paul cites Genesis 15:6 in Romans and Galatians to argue that justification by faith predates and underlies the Mosaic Law.
- The "seed" language (Genesis 22:18) is quoted in Galatians 3:16 as pointing singularly to Christ.

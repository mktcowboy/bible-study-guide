# Wilderness Wanderings
## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** 14–36 (through [[Deuteronomy]])
- **Summary:** Because of unbelief at Kadesh, Israel wanders forty years until the rebel generation dies in the wilderness, sustained by manna and water yet judged for repeated murmuring.
## Details
After the spies' evil report, the LORD swore that no one of that generation aged twenty and upward, save [[Caleb]] and [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]], would enter Canaan; instead Israel would wander forty years, a year for each day the land was searched. Through those decades God both judged and provided. He fed them daily with manna and brought water from the rock, yet the people murmured against [[Moses]] and [[Aaron]] again and again. The rebellion of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram brought the earth to swallow them; renewed complaining brought fiery serpents, answered by the bronze serpent lifted on a pole. At Meribah Moses struck the rock in anger and was himself barred from the land. As the years closed, the old generation died and a new one arose; Numbers ends on the plains of Moab, and Deuteronomy records Moses' final charge before they crossed Jordan.

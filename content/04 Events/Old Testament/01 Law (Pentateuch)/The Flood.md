# The Flood

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Genesis]] 6:1 — 9:17
- **Summary:** God sends a worldwide flood to judge human wickedness, saving [[Noah]] and his family in the ark.

## Details
Seeing that "every imagination of the thoughts of man's heart was only evil continually," God decided to wipe out humanity. But Noah found grace — a just and blameless man who walked with God.

God instructed Noah to build an enormous ark (300 x 50 x 30 cubits) with three decks, using gopher wood coated with pitch. Noah and his family (8 people) entered with pairs of every animal.

The flood:
- Rained 40 days and nights
- Waters prevailed 150 days
- Every living thing perished on dry land
- Ark rested on mountains of Ararat
- Noah sent out a raven, then three doves
- After a year, they disembarked

After the flood, God established His covenant — never again to destroy the earth by flood, symbolized by the rainbow.

## Key People Involved
- [[Noah]] — Builder of the ark
- Noah's wife, three sons ([[Shem]], Ham, Japheth), and daughters-in-law

## Key Places
- Mountains of Ararat — Where the ark rested

# Passover

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Exodus]] 12
- **Summary:** The tenth plague — death of every firstborn in Egypt. Israel is spared by the blood of a lamb on their doorposts. God "passes over" their homes.

## Details
God instructed each Israelite household to select a lamb without blemish on the 10th of Abib (April). On the 14th, they killed it at evening, applied its blood to their doorposts and lintel, and ate the lamb with bitter herbs and unleavened bread, ready to depart.

At midnight, the LORD struck every firstborn in Egypt — from Pharaoh's son in his high castle to the captive in the dungeon. Pharaoh called for Moses and Aaron and released Israel.

God established the Passover as a perpetual feast — a memorial of His deliverance. This event became the central feast of Israel and the ultimate foreshadowing of Christ, the Lamb of God.

## Key People Involved

# Balaam's Donkey

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** 22:20–39
- **Summary:** God sends [[Balaam]] to meet Israel, but an angel blocks the road with a drawn sword. Balaam's donkey sees the angel three times and refuses to proceed; God opens the donkey's mouth; Balaam's eyes are opened.

## Details
[[Balak]], king of Moab, had hired the diviner [[Balaam]] to curse Israel. God told Balaam to go with Balak's messengers but "only the word that I speak to you shall you do" (22:20). Balaam rode his donkey, but the angel of the Lord stood in the road as a adversary with a drawn sword. The donkey saw the angel and turned off the road into a field. Balaam beat the donkey. The donkey saw the angel again at a narrow path between vineyard walls and squeezed against Balaam, breaking his foot. Balaam beat her again. The third time, the angel stood where there was no room to turn, and the donkey lay down. Balaam flew into a rage and tried to kill her with his sword. Then the Lord opened the donkey's mouth, and she asked: "What have I done to you? For these three times I have withheld myself from you. Was it not right that I should withhold myself from you? For behold, now I have seen the angel of the Lord standing in the road before me with a drawn sword in his hand, and he has turned aside from me these three times." Then the Lord opened Balaam's eyes, and he saw the angel. The angel rebuked Balaam: "I came out to withstand you, because your way is perverse before me... the donkey saw me and turned aside from me these three times. If she had not turned aside from me, now I would surely have killed you, but I would have spared her." Balaam acknowledged his sin and turned back, but the angel told him to go on.

## Notes
This is one of the most remarkable stories in Scripture — a donkey with more spiritual perception than a prophet. The angel appeared three times (like divine warnings), and only the humble beast perceived divine presence. Balaam's greed blinded him to spiritual reality. The New Testament uses Balaam as a warning about false teachers motivated by profit.

# Holiness Code

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Leviticus]]
- **Chapters:** Leviticus 17–26
- **Summary:** Leviticus 17–26 is the "Holiness Code," a wide-ranging collection of moral, ritual, and social laws unified by the refrain "Be ye holy, for I am holy."

## Details
Scholars call Leviticus 17-26 the Holiness Code because its laws are bracketed and interwoven with variations of the phrase "You shall be holy, for I the LORD your God am holy" (19:2). The code covers an unusually wide range of topics: sexual ethics (18), social justice (19), forbidden practices including child sacrifice and consulting mediums (20), priestly holiness (21-22), the sacred calendar (23), the lampstand and bread of the presence (24), and the Sabbatical Year and Jubilee (25).

The social justice section (Leviticus 19) is especially rich. It prohibits harvesting field edges and vineyards completely, reserving them for the poor. It commands honesty in business, no partiality in judgment, no taking advantage of the deaf or blind, and love of the foreigner as the native-born. Leviticus 19:18 — "thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself" — is one of the most-cited Old Testament verses in the New Testament.

The Jubilee (25) commanded land to return to its original owners and Israelite debt-slaves to be released every fifty years, preventing permanent economic stratification. The principle was that the land ultimately belongs to God: "the land is mine; for ye are strangers and sojourners with me" (25:23).

## Important Verses
> "Ye shall be holy: for I the LORD your God am holy." — Leviticus 19:2
> "Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself: I am the LORD." — Leviticus 19:18

## Notes
- Jesus quotes Leviticus 19:18 as the second great commandment (Matthew 22:39) — showing that the Holiness Code anticipated what the New Testament calls the law of Christ.
- The Holiness Code's demand that holiness pervade all of life — sexual, economic, agricultural, liturgical — challenges any separation of "religious" from "secular" conduct.
- The Jubilee (ch. 25) is the text Jesus reads in Luke 4:18-19 when he announces the year of the Lord's favor.

# Rebellion of Korah

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** 16–17
- **Summary:** Korah, Dathan, and Abiram challenge Moses and Aaron's authority; God judges dramatically — earth swallows the rebels, fire consumes 250 men, Aaron's rod buds.

## Details
[[Korah]], a Levite from the family of Kohath, gathered [[Dathan]] and [[Abiram]] (Reubenites) and 250 princes of the assembly to challenge Moses and Aaron. Their argument: "All the congregation are holy, every one of them, and the LORD is among them. Why then lift you yourselves up above the congregation of the LORD?" (16:3). Moses fell on his face and proposed a test: each man a censer, the next day. God spoke to tell Moses and Aaron to separate themselves from the congregation. The earth opened and swallowed Dathan, Abiram, and their tents, wives, and children. Fire from the Lord consumed the 250 men offering incense. The people then murmured against Moses and Aaron for "killing" the Lord's people, and a plague broke out, killing 14,700 until Aaron made atonement with a censer between the dead and the living. The next day, all the princes of the tribes brought their rods; Aaron's rod budded, bloomed, and produced almonds — divine confirmation of the Aaronic priesthood.

## Notes
Korah's rebellion is the classic example of using religious language to mask pride and ambition. His argument sounded democratic ("all are holy") but was fundamentally a rejection of God's chosen order. Aaron's budding rod — dead wood producing life — is one of the most miraculous signs in the Old Testament.

# Second Census

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** Numbers 26
- **Summary:** After the forty wilderness years, [[Moses]] and Eleazar counted the new generation; the total was 601,730 — remarkably close to the first census of 603,550.

## Details
After the plague at Baal Peor that killed 24,000, God commanded Moses and Eleazar to take a new census of the second generation — all men twenty and above able to go to war. The census served two purposes: military preparation for the conquest of Canaan, and the basis for apportioning the land once it was taken (larger tribes receiving larger inheritances).

The total of 601,730 was almost identical to the original 603,550 from Numbers 1. The text pauses to note explicitly that not one of those counted in the first census was included in the second — they had all died in the wilderness exactly as God had said at Kadesh-barnea (Numbers 14), with only [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] and [[Caleb]] as exceptions.

The Levites were counted separately at 23,000 males. The census immediately precedes the request of the [[Daughters of Zelophehad]] about inheritance and sets the stage for the land distribution described in Joshua.

## Key People Involved
- [[Moses]] — Commanded to take the census
- Eleazar — Son of Aaron; conducted the census with Moses
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — One of only two surviving members of the first generation
- [[Caleb]] — The other surviving member of the first generation

## Important Verses

## Notes
- The near-identical numbers across 40 years and a complete generational turnover is theologically significant: God's purposes are not frustrated by human failure.
- The census's use for land apportionment ties military readiness to covenant inheritance — the land is not just conquered territory but promised portion.

# Balaam's Oracles

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Numbers]]
- **Chapters:** Numbers 22-24
- **Summary:** King Balak of Moab hired the seer Balaam to curse Israel; God constrained Balaam to bless Israel four times instead; the fourth oracle culminated in the messianic prophecy of a star rising from Jacob.

## Details
As Israel camped on the plains of Moab, King Balak son of Zippor was terrified and sent for the renowned diviner Balaam from Pethor in Mesopotamia to curse Israel. God told Balaam not to go, then permitted him to go with the warning that he could only say what God told him. On the journey, the angel of the LORD blocked the path; Balaam's donkey saw the angel and turned aside three times, earning beatings each time. Then the donkey spoke, rebuking Balaam, and the angel revealed himself to Balaam warning: "If his way be perverse before me, I will even slay thee: but the ass saw me, and turned from me these three times."

Balak met Balaam and brought him to high places overlooking Israel's camp for the oracles. Despite Balak's repeated attempts to position Balaam for cursing, Balaam declared four oracles of blessing:
- First oracle (23:7-10): "How shall I curse, whom God hath not cursed?" — Israel cannot be numbered, let me die their death.
- Second oracle (23:18-24): "God is not a man, that he should lie" — he has blessed and none can reverse it; no sorcery against Jacob.
- Third oracle (24:3-9): The beauty of Israel's camp; "Blessed is he that blesseth thee, and cursed is he that curseth thee."
- Fourth oracle (24:15-19): The messianic prophecy — "There shall come a Star out of Jacob, and a Sceptre shall rise out of Israel, and shall smite the corners of Moab, and destroy all the children of Sheth." This would later be understood as prophesying the Davidic king and, in NT interpretation, Jesus himself.

Balak dismissed Balaam in anger. Numbers 31:16 and Revelation 2:14 record that Balaam subsequently advised Balak to use Moabite women to seduce Israel into idolatry — the Baal Peor incident.

## Notes
The Star from Jacob oracle (24:17) is one of the most directly messianic prophecies in the Torah. The Magi who followed a star to Bethlehem (Matthew 2) may have been informed by this tradition. The Jewish rebellion of 132-135 AD was led by Bar Kokhba ("Son of a Star"), indicating that some Jews understood this prophecy as unfulfilled in their own day. Peter's description of Jesus as "the day star" (2 Peter 1:19) and Revelation's "the root and the offspring of David, and the bright and morning star" (22:16) are likely in dialogue with this oracle.

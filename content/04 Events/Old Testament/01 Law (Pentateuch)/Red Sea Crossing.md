# Red Sea Crossing

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Exodus]] 14
- **Summary:** Israel crosses the Red Sea on dry ground. Egyptian army pursues and is destroyed when the waters return.

## Details
Pharaoh changed his mind and pursued Israel with 600 chariots. The Israelites were trapped between the Egyptian army and the sea. God told Moses to stretch out his staff, and He divided the sea with a strong east wind, creating a wall of water on both sides. Israel walked through on dry ground.

When the Egyptians pursued into the sea, God caused Pharaoh's chariots to stumble. At Moses' command, the waters returned, drowning the entire Egyptian army. Israel saw "this great work which the LORD hath this day done upon the Egyptians."

[[Miriam]] led the women in song and dance — the first recorded worship song after a deliverance.

## Key People Involved
- [[Moses]] — Stretched out his staff
- [[Miriam]] — Led worship with timbrel
- [[Pharaoh]] — Lost army, changed his mind

# Song of Moses

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Deuteronomy]]
- **Chapters:** 32
- **Summary:** Moses composes a lengthy poetic testimony that will serve as a witness against Israel when they rebel.

## Details
God commanded Moses to write a song and teach it to the children of Israel, saying "it will be a witness for Me against the children of Israel" (31:19). The song (chapter 32) is one of the longest poems in the Bible and contains some of the most theologically rich language in Scripture. It begins with a call to heaven and earth to listen, then describes God's character — "The Rock, His work is perfect, for all His ways are justice, a God of truth and without falsehood" (32:4). It recounts Israel's rebellion — "They provoke Him to jealousy with foreign gods; they provoke Him to anger with their abominable works" (32:16). It describes God's response — hiding His face, bringing calamity — but also His compassion — "For I will not destroy them completely, lest the enemy misunderstand and say, 'My hand, and not the LORD, has done all this'" (32:28–29). The song ends with the terrifying statement that God will take vengeance on His adversaries and repay His enemies, and that "the land will drink the blood of the slain" (32:42–43). Jesus quoted this song in [[Luke]] 20:13, applying it to Himself.

## Notes
The Song of Moses is a microcosm of the entire biblical story — God's faithfulness, human rebellion, divine judgment, and ultimate compassion. It's also one of the most quoted Old Testament passages in the New Testament, showing its central importance to biblical theology.

# Conspiracy Against Jeremiah
## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Jeremiah]]
- **Chapters:** 11:18–23 (also 18:18)
- **Summary:** The men of [[Anathoth]], [[Jeremiah (Prophet)|Jeremiah]]'s own townsmen, plot to kill him to silence his prophesying, but the LORD reveals the plot and pronounces judgment upon them.
## Details
[[Jeremiah (Prophet)|Jeremiah]] confesses that he had been like a gentle lamb led to the slaughter, not knowing that his neighbors had devised devices against him. The LORD opened his eyes to the scheme: the men of Anathoth, of the priestly town where the prophet himself was born, sought his life, saying, "Prophesy not in the name of the LORD, that thou die not by our hand." That his own kinsmen and townsmen turned against him made the betrayal especially bitter. Jeremiah appealed to the LORD, the righteous judge who tries the reins and the heart, asking to see God's vengeance on them. The LORD answered with a sentence against the men of Anathoth: their young men would die by the sword, their sons and daughters by famine, and no remnant would be left, for he would bring evil upon them in the year of their visitation. A parallel scheme appears in Jeremiah 18:18, where opponents plot to smite him with the tongue and ignore his words.

# Daniel in the Lions' Den

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Daniel]]
- **Chapters:** Daniel 6:1-28
- **Summary:** Jealous administrators in [[The King's Court]] of Darius the Mede conspire against [[Daniel]] by persuading Darius to sign an irrevocable law forbidding prayer to any god or man except the king for thirty days. Daniel continues praying three times daily toward Jerusalem and is cast into the lions' den. God shuts the lions' mouths, and Daniel emerges unharmed, leading Darius to decree that all people must reverence the God of Daniel.

## Significance
The account demonstrates that faithful obedience to God takes precedence over the decrees of earthly rulers, a theme that unifies [[Daniel]] with the earlier trial of [[Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego]]. Daniel's deliverance vindicates the sovereignty of the God of Israel before a pagan empire and foreshadows the ultimate triumph of God's people over their persecutors. Darius's concluding doxology—"He is the living God, and stedfast for ever" (Dan 6:26)—echoes the witness extracted from Nebuchadnezzar in earlier chapters, marking a repeated pattern of Gentile rulers acknowledging Israel's God.

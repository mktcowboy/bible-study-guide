# Murder of Gedaliah

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Jeremiah]]
- **Chapters:** Jeremiah 40-41
- **Summary:** Gedaliah, Babylon's appointed governor over the remaining Judeans, was assassinated by Ishmael son of Nethaniah, triggering fear and flight toward Egypt.

## Details
After Jerusalem fell, Gedaliah was appointed over those left in the land. He urged the remnant to serve Babylon and live in the land, but Ishmael son of Nethaniah came with men and killed him at Mizpah.

The assassination shattered the fragile post-destruction community. Fearing Babylonian retaliation, the people sought guidance from [[Jeremiah]] but ultimately fled toward Egypt against his warning.

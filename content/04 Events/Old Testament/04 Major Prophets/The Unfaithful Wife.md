# The Unfaithful Wife

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Book:** [[Ezekiel]]
- **Chapters:** Ezekiel 16
- **Summary:** The longest single allegory in the prophets: Jerusalem is portrayed as a foundling girl whom God took in, dressed, and married, who then became a prostitute worse than her sisters Samaria and Sodom.

## Details
Ezekiel 16 is one of the most extended and confrontational prophetic texts in the Bible. God narrates Jerusalem's entire history as an allegory. She was born of Canaanite parents, unwanted, cast out in an open field on the day of her birth — her navel uncut, her body unwashed. God passed by, saw her in her blood, and declared "Live!" Years later, God passed by again and she was mature; he spread his garment over her (the covenant metaphor), clothed her, adorned her with jewels, and made her his wife.

Then the wife used everything God had given her — the oil, the bread, the fine linen, the jewelry — in prostitution with foreigners. Not like a prostitute who takes payment, but worse: she gave payment to her lovers. The text catalogs the litany of alliances with Egypt, Assyria, Babylon, and Canaan. Even the children born of God's covenant were sacrificed to idols.

The allegory concludes with both judgment and an unexpected turn: God will bring the lovers to strip and expose her, and her covenant breaking will be punished. But then — and this is Ezekiel's surprise — God will remember the covenant he made with her and establish an everlasting covenant. She will be ashamed, and in her shame she will be silent; but God will atone for all that she has done.

## Notes
- Ezekiel 16 is the harshest of the three great "unfaithful wife" allegories (Hosea 1-3, Jeremiah 3, Ezekiel 16), going further in both accusation and shame.
- The unexpected ending — an everlasting covenant despite everything — makes this one of the most astonishing grace passages in the prophets.
- The claim that Jerusalem was worse than Sodom shattered the self-justifying religious nationalism that assumed Jerusalem's special status guaranteed safety.

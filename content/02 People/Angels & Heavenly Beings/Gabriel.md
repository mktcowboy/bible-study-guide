# Gabriel

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both
- **Role:** Angel who announced to Zacharias the birth of John the Baptist and to Mary the conception of Jesus
- **Family:** Not applicable; an angel
- **Key Books:** [[Daniel]], [[Luke]]

## Biography
Gabriel is one of only two angels named in the Protestant biblical canon (the other being [[Michael]]). His name means "God is my strength" or "hero of God" in Hebrew. He appears four times in Scripture — twice in Daniel and twice in Luke — and in each appearance he serves as a messenger entrusted with divine revelation concerning the unfolding of God's redemptive purposes.

In Daniel 8:16, Gabriel is commanded to "make this man understand the vision" and approaches the terrified [[Daniel (Prophet)|Daniel]] to interpret his vision of the ram and the goat. In Daniel 9:21–22, Gabriel appears again while [[Daniel (Prophet)|Daniel]] is in prayer, arriving "in swift flight" to give him "insight and understanding" — specifically the famous Seventy Weeks prophecy (Daniel 9:24–27), one of the most discussed messianic and eschatological passages in the Old Testament.

Gabriel's New Testament appearances are both annunciatory and momentous. He first appears to Zechariah (Zacharias) in the temple, standing at the right side of the altar of incense, to announce that his aged wife [[Elizabeth]] would bear a son who was to be named John. When Zechariah doubted, Gabriel identified himself: "I am Gabriel. I stand in the presence of God, and I was sent to speak to you and to bring you this good news" (Luke 1:19). As a sign of judgment for his unbelief, Zechariah was struck mute until the child's birth.

Six months later, Gabriel was sent to [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]] in Nazareth with the annunciation of the incarnation: "Greetings, O favored one, the Lord is with you!" (Luke 1:28). He announced that she would conceive and bear the Son of the Most High, who would sit on David's throne forever. Mary's question was not unbelief but genuine inquiry: "How will this be, since I am a virgin?" (Luke 1:34). Gabriel's answer introduced the doctrine of the virgin conception: "The Holy Spirit will come upon you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you; therefore the child to be born will be called holy — the Son of God" (Luke 1:35).

## Key Events
- Interprets Daniel's vision of the ram and goat (Daniel 8:16–26)
- Delivers the Seventy Weeks prophecy to [[Daniel (Prophet)|Daniel]] (Daniel 9:21–27)
- Announces John the Baptist's birth to Zechariah in the temple (Luke 1:11–20)
- Strikes Zechariah mute for his unbelief (Luke 1:20)
- Announces the incarnation to [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]] in Nazareth (Luke 1:26–38)
- Explains the virgin conception through the Holy Spirit (Luke 1:35)

## Notes
- Gabriel's role across both Testaments is consistently one of revelation and announcement concerning God's redemptive timeline — he appears at the interpretive moments of Scripture's grand narrative
- His self-identification — "I stand in the presence of God" — distinguishes him as a throne attendant, one of the highest orders of angelic beings, not merely a messenger angel
- The symmetry between his appearance to Zechariah (announcing the forerunner) and to Mary (announcing the Messiah) frames the entire Lukan birth narrative as a divine orchestration

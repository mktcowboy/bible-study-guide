# Satan (the Accuser)

> **The canonical node for the adversary.** The same fallen being appears throughout Scripture under many names — Satan ("adversary"), the Devil ("slanderer"), the serpent, Lucifer, the tempter, the accuser, the prince of this world. Link to `[[Satan (the Accuser)]]` everywhere (aliases above all resolve here). For the Revelation vision of him as the great red dragon, see [[The Dragon]]; for his end-time agents, see [[The Beast]], [[The Man of Sin]], and [[The Antichrists]].

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both — present from Eden (Genesis 3) to the lake of fire (Revelation 20)
- **Names:** Satan (Hebrew, "adversary") · Devil (Greek *diabolos*, "slanderer/accuser") · the serpent · Lucifer (Isaiah 14:12) · Beelzebub · Belial · the tempter · the evil one · the prince of this world
- **Nature:** A created, fallen spiritual being — not God's equal; powerful but finite, and always subject to God's permission
- **Role:** Tempter, deceiver, accuser, liar, and murderer; the adversary of God, His people, and His Christ
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]], [[Job]], [[Zechariah]], [[Matthew]], [[John]], [[2 Corinthians]], [[Ephesians]], [[1 Peter]], [[Revelation]]

## Biography
Satan is a created spiritual being who fell through pride and became the great adversary of God. He first appears in action as the serpent who deceives [[Eve]] in the garden (Genesis 3; see [[Fall of Man]]), and first appears by the name "the satan" in the heavenly council of [[Job]], where he accuses Job of serving God only for reward and is permitted — within strict limits — to afflict him. Across Scripture he tempts, deceives, accuses, and destroys: he resists Joshua the high priest (Zechariah 3), tempts [[Jesus Christ]] in the wilderness (see [[Temptation of Jesus]]), enters [[Judas Iscariot]], sifts [[Peter]], blinds the minds of unbelievers, and prowls "as a roaring lion." Yet his head was crushed at the cross, and his doom is certain: he will be bound and finally cast into the lake of fire forever. He is a defeated enemy whose remaining time is short.

## Names and Titles
Each name reveals a facet of his work:
- **Satan** — "adversary"; the opponent of God and man (Job 1:6).
- **The Devil** — Greek *diabolos*, "slanderer, false accuser" (Matthew 4:1).
- **That old serpent** — the deceiver of Eden, named explicitly in Revelation 12:9 (Genesis 3:1).
- **Lucifer, son of the morning** — the fallen "day star" of Isaiah 14:12.
- **The tempter** — who entices to sin (Matthew 4:3; 1 Thessalonians 3:5).
- **The accuser of the brethren** — who accuses God's people "before our God day and night" (Revelation 12:10).
- **The prince of this world** — its present usurping ruler (John 12:31; 14:30).
- **The god of this world** — who blinds unbelievers (2 Corinthians 4:4).
- **The prince of the power of the air** — over the spirit of disobedience (Ephesians 2:2).
- **Beelzebub** — "prince of the devils" (Matthew 12:24).
- **Belial** — "worthlessness," set against Christ (2 Corinthians 6:15).
- **The wicked one / the evil one** — who snatches away the word (Matthew 13:19, 38).
- **A liar and a murderer from the beginning** — the father of lies (John 8:44).

## Origin and Fall
Satan is a creature, not eternal; he was made good and fell. Two prophetic taunts — against the king of Babylon and the king of Tyre — are traditionally read as also unveiling the pride and ruin of the spiritual power behind them:
- **Isaiah 14:12–15** — "How art thou fallen from heaven, O Lucifer... For thou hast said in thine heart, I will ascend into heaven... I will be like the most High." Pride is the root of his fall.
- **Ezekiel 28:12–17** — "Thou art the anointed cherub that covereth... Thou wast perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee." A glorious created being corrupted by self-exaltation.
- **Luke 10:18** — "I beheld Satan as lightning fall from heaven."
- **Revelation 12:7–9** — war in heaven: [[Michael]] and his angels cast the dragon down. See [[The Dragon]].
- **Jude 1:6 / 2 Peter 2:4** — "the angels which kept not their first estate," reserved in chains for judgment; see [[Angels]].

## Character and Works
- **Tempter** — entices to sin: the serpent in Eden ([[Fall of Man]]), the wilderness test of Jesus ([[Temptation of Jesus]]).
- **Deceiver** — "deceiveth the whole world" (Revelation 12:9); transforms himself "into an angel of light" (2 Corinthians 11:14).
- **Accuser** — charges the saints before God (Job 1–2; Zechariah 3:1; Revelation 12:10).
- **Liar and murderer** — "no truth in him... the father of it" (John 8:44).
- **Adversary** — "as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour" (1 Peter 5:8).
- **Hinderer and afflicter** — hindered Paul (1 Thessalonians 2:18); a "messenger of Satan" buffeted him (2 Corinthians 12:7).
- **Sower of evil** — sows tares among the wheat (Matthew 13:38–39).
- **Holder of the power of death** — until broken by Christ (Hebrews 2:14).

## Subject to God's Sovereignty
A central biblical safeguard: Satan is **not** a rival deity. In [[Job]] he can act only by God's permission and within God's limits ("only upon himself put not forth thine hand"). [[God]] reigns over the test; the accuser cannot exceed the boundary set for him. Satan's malice is real, but it is finally bent to serve God's purposes and his own ruin.

## Satan and Jesus Christ
- **The promised conqueror** — Genesis 3:15: the seed of the woman "shall bruise thy head."
- **The wilderness duel** — Jesus answers every temptation with Scripture: "Get thee hence, Satan" (Matthew 4:10); see [[Temptation of Jesus]].
- **The cross as victory** — "Now shall the prince of this world be cast out" (John 12:31); Christ "spoiled principalities and powers" (Colossians 2:15) and through death destroyed "him that had the power of death" (Hebrews 2:14). See [[Sacrifice and Atonement]], [[The Blood]].
- **The purpose of the incarnation** — "the Son of God was manifested, that he might destroy the works of the devil" (1 John 3:8).

## His Defeat and Doom
- **Crushed at the cross** — Colossians 2:15; Hebrews 2:14; John 12:31.
- **Under the saints' feet** — "the God of peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly" (Romans 16:20).
- **Cast down and enraged** — overcome "by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony" (Revelation 12:11).
- **Bound and destroyed** — seized and bound (Revelation 20:2), then cast into the lake of fire "and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever" (Revelation 20:10). See [[Judgment]], [[The Day of the LORD]].

## Resisting the Adversary
- **Submit and resist** — "Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you" (James 4:7).
- **Be watchful** — "Be sober, be vigilant" (1 Peter 5:8).
- **Stand armed** — "Put on the whole armour of God" against "the wiles of the devil" (Ephesians 6:11–12).
- **Give him no place** — "Neither give place to the devil" (Ephesians 4:27).
- **Overcome by the Lamb** — victory is by Christ's blood and faithful testimony, not by human strength (Revelation 12:11); see [[Faith]].

## His Agents and Manifestations
- [[The Dragon]] — Satan unveiled in the Revelation vision as the great red dragon
- [[The Beast]] — the political power that receives the dragon's authority
- [[The Man of Sin]] — the eschatological "son of perdition," empowered "after the working of Satan" (2 Thessalonians 2:9)
- [[The Antichrists]] — those who deny the Father and the Son, animated by the spirit of antichrist
- Demons / unclean spirits — his subordinate fallen angels (Matthew 12:24; see [[Angels]])

## Notes
- The title in [[Job]] is literally "the satan," *the adversary* or *accuser*, highlighting his courtroom function in the prologue.
- "Devil" translates the Greek *diabolos*, "slanderer" — the same accusing role carried into the New Testament.
- Scripture never presents Satan as God's equal or opposite. He is a creature who fell; his power is real but derivative, and his defeat is already accomplished at the cross and yet to be consummated.
- Isaiah 14 and Ezekiel 28 address the kings of Babylon and Tyre directly; the church has long read them as also disclosing the pride and fall of the spiritual power behind such rulers.

# Adversaries of Judah and Benjamin

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Local opponents who resisted the returned exiles' temple rebuilding
- **Family:** Mixed regional populations around Judah and Samaria
- **Key Books:** [[Ezra]]

## Biography
The adversaries of Judah and Benjamin first offered to help build the temple, claiming shared worship because they had sacrificed to the God of Israel since the days of Esarhaddon. [[Zerubbabel]], [[Jeshua]], and the family heads refused the compromised partnership, insisting that Cyrus's decree had assigned the work to the returned community.

After the refusal, the adversaries discouraged the people, hired counselors against them, and later participated in accusations sent to Persian authorities. Ezra uses their opposition to show that restoration required both perseverance and covenant clarity.

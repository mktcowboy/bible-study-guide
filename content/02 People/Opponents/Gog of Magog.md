# Gog of Magog

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Eschatological enemy leader from the far north
- **Family:** Associated with Magog, Meshech, and Tubal
- **Key Books:** [[Ezekiel]], [[Revelation]]

## Biography
Gog appears in Ezekiel 38-39 as the leader of a vast coalition that comes against restored Israel. The battle is framed to reveal God's holiness among the nations: Gog's assault is met by divine intervention rather than ordinary military deliverance.

Revelation later reuses "Gog and Magog" language for the final rebellion against God's people, showing the figure's apocalyptic significance beyond Ezekiel's immediate horizon.

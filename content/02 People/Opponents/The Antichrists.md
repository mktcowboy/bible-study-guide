# The Antichrists

> Satan's agents in their present, many-membered form. In John's letters the "antichrists" are Christ-denying false teachers animated by "the spirit of antichrist." For the singular end-time figures, see [[The Man of Sin]] and [[The Beast]]; for the power behind them, [[Satan (the Accuser)]].

## Overview
The antichrists in [[1 John]] and [[2 John]] are secessionist false teachers who deny the truth about [[Jesus Christ]]. The Greek *antichristos* carries both senses — "against Christ" and "in place of Christ" — opposition and counterfeit at once. John writes that although his readers have heard "that antichrist shall come, even now are there many antichrists; whereby we know that it is the last time" (1 John 2:18).

## Many Antichrists, One Spirit
John makes a striking move: the expected future Antichrist is already present in pattern. The single coming opponent is anticipated by "many antichrists" — and behind them all is "that spirit of antichrist, whereof ye have heard that it should come; and even now already is it in the world" (1 John 4:3). The last hour is marked not only by a final figure but by the spread of Christ-denying teaching now.

## The Doctrinal Test
John gives a clear test for identifying them:
- **They deny the Christ:** "Who is a liar but he that denieth that Jesus is the Christ? He is antichrist, that denieth the Father and the Son" (1 John 2:22). To reject the Son is to lose the Father also (2:23).
- **They deny the incarnation:** "every spirit that confesseth not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh is not of God: and this is that spirit of antichrist" (1 John 4:3). "Many deceivers are entered into the world, who confess not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh. This is a deceiver and an antichrist" (2 John 1:7).

## They Went Out From Us
The antichrists are apostates from within the community: "They went out from us, but they were not of us; for if they had been of us, they would no doubt have continued with us" (1 John 2:19). Their departure exposes that they never truly belonged. See [[The False Teachers]].

## Relationship to the End-Time Figures
John's "antichrists" are the present, plural manifestation of the same rebellion that comes to a head in the singular eschatological opponents — Paul's [[The Man of Sin]] and Revelation's [[The Beast]] — all empowered by [[Satan (the Accuser)]]. The spirit at work in first-century false teachers is the same spirit that will be embodied at the end.

## How the Church Discerns and Stands
Believers are guarded not by special knowledge but by the truth they already hold: the apostolic confession of the Son, the anointing of the Spirit ("ye have an unction from the Holy One," 1 John 2:20, 27), and abiding in what they "heard from the beginning." The remedy for antichrist teaching is to abide in Christ and the apostolic word. See [[Faith]], [[The Word]], [[Repentance]].

## Notes
"Antichrist" is a term unique to John's letters (1 John 2:18, 22; 4:3; 2 John 7) and never appears in Revelation, though the figures there match its sense. In 1 John, "antichrist" is not only a final opponent but a present pattern of Christ-denying teaching — which is why John can speak of both "antichrist" (singular, yet to come) and "many antichrists" (already here).

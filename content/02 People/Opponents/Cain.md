# Cain

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Firstborn of [[Adam]] and [[Eve]], first murderer
- **Family:** Son of Adam and Eve, brother of [[Abel]] and [[Seth]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Cain was the firstborn son of [[Adam]] and [[Eve]], a tiller of the ground. When he and his brother [[Abel]] brought offerings to God, the LORD had respect for Abel and his offering but not for Cain's. In jealousy and anger, Cain murdered Abel in the field — the first act of violence in human history.

After confronting Cain, God offered him a chance to sin no more, but Cain refused. God marked him with a protective sign and banished him to the land of Nod, east of Eden. Despite the curse on the ground, Cain built a city and started a lineage that included Enoch, Irad, Mehujael, Methushael, Lamech, and Tubal-cain.

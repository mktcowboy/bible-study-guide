# Commander of the LORD's Army

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Divine warrior figure appearing to Joshua before Jericho
- **Family:** Heavenly host
- **Key Books:** [[Joshua]]

## Biography
Before Jericho falls, Joshua sees a man with a drawn sword and asks whether he is for Israel or for Israel's enemies. The answer is "Nay," because he comes as commander of the LORD's host. Joshua worships, and the ground is declared holy.

## Key Events
- [[Renewal at Gilgal]] — Appears after covenant renewal and before Jericho
- [[Fall of Jericho]] — His appearance frames the victory as the LORD's battle

## Relationships
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Receives the encounter

## Important Verses
> "Nay; but as captain of the host of the LORD am I now come." — Joshua 5:14

## Notes
The holy-ground command recalls Moses at the burning bush and signals that Joshua's conquest is under divine authority.

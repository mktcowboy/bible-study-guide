# Ezekiel (Prophet)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Prophet and priest among the exiles in Babylon; watchman for Israel
- **Family:** Son of Buzi the priest
- **Key Books:** [[Ezekiel]]

## Biography

Ezekiel was a priest carried into exile in Babylon before the final destruction of Jerusalem. He prophesied among the exiles by the Chebar River, where the heavens opened and he saw visions of God. His ministry begins with a staggering vision of the glory of the LORD: living creatures, wheels within wheels, a crystal expanse, a throne, and the appearance of divine glory.

Ezekiel's priestly background shapes his message. He is deeply concerned with the temple, holiness, impurity, idolatry, and the departure and return of God's glory. He is appointed as a watchman, responsible to warn the wicked and the righteous. His task is not merely to predict events, but to expose why judgment is just and why restoration must be God's work.

Ezekiel uses dramatic sign acts. He lies on his side, eats measured food, shaves his head, packs baggage as an exile, and speaks through embodied symbols. The book is full of visions: the abominations in the temple, the glory departing from Jerusalem, the valley of dry bones, the joined sticks of Judah and Israel, Gog of Magog, and the restored temple.

Ezekiel's wife dies as a sign to the exiles when Jerusalem's sanctuary is struck. The severity of his ministry is matched by extraordinary promises: a new heart, a new spirit, cleansing from uncleanness, resurrection-like restoration, one shepherd from David's line, and the return of God's presence among his people.

## Notes
- Ezekiel is the prophet of divine glory in exile: God's presence is not trapped by Jerusalem's fall.
- His ministry insists that Israel's restoration requires inner transformation, not merely return to land.
- The new heart and new spirit promises are foundational for later biblical teaching about regeneration and the Spirit.
- Ezekiel's temple vision is one of the Bible's richest images of restored worship, holiness, and divine presence.

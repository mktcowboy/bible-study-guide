# Simeon

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Righteous and devout man in Jerusalem who held the infant Jesus and proclaimed the Nunc Dimittis
- **Family:** No family details given in Scripture
- **Key Books:** [[Luke]]

## Biography
Simeon appears only in Luke 2:25–35 and yet delivers one of the most theologically dense speeches in the entire New Testament. He is described as "righteous and devout, waiting for the consolation of Israel," and the Holy Spirit was upon him. He had received a divine revelation that he would not die before seeing the Lord's Christ. When Mary and Joseph brought the infant Jesus to the temple for the prescribed purification rites and dedication of the firstborn, Simeon came into the temple courts, led by the Spirit.

Taking the child in his arms, Simeon uttered the prayer that has become known in the Western church as the Nunc Dimittis (from the Latin opening words: "Now you are letting your servant depart"): "Lord, now you are letting your servant depart in peace, according to your word; for my eyes have seen your salvation that you have prepared in the presence of all peoples, a light for revelation to the Gentiles, and for glory to your people Israel" (Luke 2:29–32). This brief hymn accomplishes remarkable theological work. It identifies the infant as "your salvation" — a person who is God's act of saving, not merely the agent of it. It anticipates the universal scope of that salvation, placing Gentiles before Israel in the phrase, a pattern consistent with Luke's Gentile mission emphasis throughout his Gospel and Acts.

Simeon then blessed Mary and Joseph and addressed a specific prophecy to [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]]: "This child is appointed for the fall and rising of many in Israel, and for a sign that is opposed — and a sword will pierce through your own soul also" (Luke 2:34–35). The sword that would pierce Mary's soul is most naturally understood as the pain she would witness at the crucifixion — the death of her son, rejected by the people he came to save. This prophetic word casts a shadow of suffering over the nativity narrative and anticipates the passion.

Simeon's age is not specified in Scripture, though tradition depicts him as an elderly man. The description of his eagerness to die after seeing the fulfillment of God's promise places him among the figures whose entire lives were oriented by a single awaited reality — the redemption of Israel.

## Key Events
- Receives revelation from the Holy Spirit that he will see the Lord's Christ before dying (Luke 2:26)
- Led by the Spirit to enter the temple as Mary and Joseph arrive with Jesus (Luke 2:27)
- Takes the infant Jesus in his arms and praises God (Luke 2:28)
- Delivers the Nunc Dimittis, identifying Jesus as salvation for Gentiles and Israel (Luke 2:29–32)
- Prophesies to [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]] about the sword that will pierce her soul (Luke 2:34–35)

## Notes
- Simeon's Nunc Dimittis has been sung as a canonical evening prayer (Compline) in the Western church since at least the 4th century
- His description of Jesus as "a light for revelation to the Gentiles" positions the infant as the fulfillment of Isaiah's Servant Songs (Isaiah 42:6; 49:6)
- The parallel between Simeon and [[Anna]] (also present; both devout, Spirit-led, and waiting for redemption) creates a literary pair — male and female witnesses validating the infant's identity, echoing the two-witness principle

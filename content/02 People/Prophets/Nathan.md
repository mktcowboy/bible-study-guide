# Nathan

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Prophet during David's reign
- **Family:** Not specified
- **Key Books:** [[2 Samuel]], [[1 Kings]], [[1 Chronicles]]

## Biography
Nathan was a prophet in David's court. He delivered the Davidic covenant, confronted David over his sin with Bathsheba, and later helped secure Solomon's succession.

## Key Events
- [[Davidic Covenant]] - Nathan delivers God's promise to David
- [[Nathan's Confrontation]] - Confronts David with "Thou art the man"
- [[Solomon's Succession]] - Supports Solomon against Adonijah

## Relationships
- [[David]] - King Nathan counsels and confronts
- [[Bathsheba]] - Works with her to secure Solomon's throne
- [[Solomon]] - Supported as David's successor

## Important Verses
- 2 Samuel 7:12-16 - Nathan gives the covenant promise.
- 2 Samuel 12:7 - Nathan confronts David.

## Cross-References
- Mentioned in: [[2 Samuel]], [[1 Kings]], [[1 Chronicles]]

Nathan shows prophetic ministry as both promise and confrontation.

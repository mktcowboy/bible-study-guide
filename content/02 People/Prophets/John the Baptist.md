# John the Baptist

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Forerunner of [[Jesus Christ]]; prophet of repentance; baptizer in the wilderness
- **Family:** Son of [[Zechariah (Priest)]] and [[Elizabeth]]
- **Key Books:** [[Matthew]], [[Mark]], [[Luke]], [[John]], [[Acts]]

## Biography

John the Baptist was born to Zechariah and Elizabeth after angelic announcement. His birth was surrounded by prophetic expectation: he would be filled with the Holy Spirit from the womb, turn many in Israel to the Lord, and go before the Lord in the spirit and power of Elijah. His birth story stands at the hinge between Old Testament promise and New Testament fulfillment.

John appeared in the wilderness preaching repentance and baptizing in the Jordan. His clothing and location evoke Elijah, and the Gospels identify him with the promised voice crying in the wilderness from Isaiah and the messenger promised in Malachi. His baptism was not a mere ritual washing; it was a public sign of repentance in preparation for the coming kingdom.

John's message was direct and searching. He warned religious leaders not to rely on descent from Abraham, called for fruits worthy of repentance, and announced one coming after him who was mightier than he. John baptized with water, but the coming one would baptize with the Holy Spirit and fire.

John's greatest moment came at the [[Baptism of Jesus]]. Though he felt unworthy to baptize Jesus, he obeyed. The heavens opened, the Spirit descended like a dove, and the Father's voice declared Jesus to be his beloved Son. John's Gospel also presents John as a witness who identifies Jesus as the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world.

John's faithfulness cost him his life. He rebuked [[Herod Antipas]] for taking Herodias, his brother's wife, and was imprisoned. Herodias's daughter requested John's head after a banquet oath, and John was executed. Jesus honored John as more than a prophet and said that among those born of women none had arisen greater, yet the least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he.

## Notes
- John is the final great prophetic bridge: he belongs to Israel's prophetic hope while directly pointing to Christ.
- His humility is central: "He must increase, but I must decrease."
- John's preaching joins repentance, judgment, kingdom hope, and Christ-centered witness.
- His death shows that prophetic faithfulness still confronts rulers and can still bring suffering.

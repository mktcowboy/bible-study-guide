# Artaxerxes I

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Persian king associated with Ezra's commission and Nehemiah's mission
- **Family:** Achaemenid ruler after Xerxes
- **Key Books:** [[Ezra]], [[Nehemiah]]

## Biography
Artaxerxes I appears in the post-exilic narratives as the Persian king under whom Ezra traveled to Jerusalem with authority to teach the Law and under whom Nehemiah received permission to rebuild Jerusalem's walls.

Ezra 4 also preserves opposition correspondence from the Artaxerxes period, showing that Persian authority could be invoked against Jerusalem before being used by God for restoration.

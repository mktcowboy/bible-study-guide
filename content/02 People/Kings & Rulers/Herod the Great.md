# Herod the Great

## Overview
Herod the Great was the client king of Judea when [[Jesus Christ]] was born. In [[Matthew]], he appears as the paranoid ruler who sought to destroy the newborn king of the Jews.

## Biblical Significance
Herod's violence in Matthew 2 frames Jesus' infancy as a conflict between earthly power and God's promised Messiah. His slaughter of the children in Bethlehem echoes Pharaoh's oppression in [[Exodus]].

## Key Events

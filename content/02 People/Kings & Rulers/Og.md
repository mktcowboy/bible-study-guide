# Og

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** King of Bashan; Amorite giant; defeated by Moses east of the Jordan
- **Key Passages:** Numbers 21:33–35; Deuteronomy 3:1–11; Psalm 135:11; 136:20
- **Associated Book:** [[Numbers]], [[Deuteronomy]]

## Summary
Og, king of Bashan, was the last of the Rephaim — the giant race of the ancient world. His defeat east of the Jordan River at Edrei was Israel's second major conquest victory, following the defeat of [[Sihon]] the Amorite. Moses led Israel against him at the LORD's command: "Fear him not: for I have delivered him into thy hand." The entire city of Bashan was devoted to destruction. Og's enormous iron bedstead (or sarcophagus) — nine cubits long (~13.5 feet) by four cubits wide — was preserved in Rabbath of Ammon as a monument to his extraordinary size (Deuteronomy 3:11).

Og's defeat was remembered as a foundational demonstration of divine faithfulness, celebrated in the Psalms (135:11; 136:20) as proof that the God who defeated the greatest earthly powers would give Israel the promised land. His territory (Bashan) became part of the portion allotted to the half-tribe of Manasseh.

# Pharaoh (of the Exodus)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Ruler of Egypt during Israel's enslavement and deliverance
- **Family:** Unknown
- **Key Books:** [[Exodus]]

## Biography
"Pharaoh" was the title of Egypt's ruler, not a personal name. The Pharaoh of the Exodus is unnamed in Scripture, representing the archetype of human opposition to God. Two different pharaohs appear in Exodus: the unnamed one who enslaved Israel, and the one who confronted [[Moses]].

This Pharaoh repeatedly refused to "let My people go" despite escalating divine judgment through ten plagues. Scripture says God hardened his heart — both by Pharaoh's own stubbornness and by divine sovereignty. Each plague offered a chance to repent, but Pharaoh hardened his heart ten times.

Only the death of his firstborn son broke his resistance. He released Israel, then changed his mind and pursued them with 600 chariots — only to be destroyed in the Red Sea.

Pharaoh represents worldly power in rebellion against God — a theme that recurs throughout Scripture as a type of Satan and end-times rebellion.

## Key Events
- Enslaved Israelites
- Refused to release Israel (10 times)
- Survived ten plagues
- Died in the Red Sea pursuit

# Darius the Mede

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Ruler associated with Babylon after Belshazzar's fall and Daniel's lions' den
- **Family:** Called son of Ahasuerus, of Median descent
- **Key Books:** [[Daniel]]

## Biography
Darius the Mede appears after the fall of Babylon in Daniel 5 and organizes the kingdom under satraps in Daniel 6. Under his administration, jealous officials manipulate a royal decree against prayer, leading to [[Daniel]] being thrown into the lions' den.

His precise historical identification is debated. The biblical narrative presents him as the ruler under whom Daniel's faithfulness is publicly vindicated and the God of Israel is proclaimed throughout the kingdom.

## Notes
The identity of Darius the Mede is one of Daniel's major historical questions, but his narrative role is clear: imperial law cannot silence faithful prayer.

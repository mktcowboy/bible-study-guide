# Lot

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Nephew of [[Abraham]], dweller in [[Sodom]], rescued by divine intervention
- **Family:** Son of Haran (Abraham's brother), nephew of Abraham, father of Moab and Ben-ammi
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Lot was the son of Haran and nephew of [[Abraham]]. After Haran died in [[Ur]], Lot traveled with Abraham's family through [[Haran]] to [[Canaan]]. When their flocks grew too large to dwell together, Lot chose the well-watered Jordan plain, pitching his tents "little by little toward [[Sodom]]."

When Sodom and five neighboring cities were destroyed by God, Abraham interceded. Angels visited Lot, warning him to flee. Lot hesitated, but angels grabbed him and his two daughters and brought them to [[Zoar]]. In desperation, Lot's daughters committed incest with him, producing Moab (father of Moabites) and Ben-ammi (father of Ammonites).

Later, during a battle between kings, Lot was captured. Abraham rescued him, and Lot returned to live among the Edomites.

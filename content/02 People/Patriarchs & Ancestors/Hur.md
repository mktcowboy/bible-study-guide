# Hur

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Helper and supporter of Moses
- **Family:** Son of Miriam (according to 1 Chronicles 2:19), making him Moses' nephew
- **Key Books:** [[Exodus]], [[Deuteronomy]]

## Biography
Hur is one of the most underappreciated figures in the Bible. During the battle with Amalek at Rephidim, Hur (along with Aaron) supported Moses' arms as he held the staff of God. Whenever Moses held up his hand, Israel prevailed; whenever he let it down, Amalek prevailed. Hur built a stone for Moses to sit on, and held up his hands until sunset, ensuring Israel's victory (Exodus 17:8–13). He is also mentioned as one of the seventy elders who saw God in the tabernacle (Exodus 24:1). Hur represents the vital role of faithful supporters behind every leader.

## Key Events
- Battle with Amalek — Held up Moses' arms during the battle at Rephidim
- [[Ten Plagues]] — Present at the foot of Mount Sinai during covenant ratification

## Relationships
- [[Moses]] — Uncle, the leader he supported
- [[Aaron]] — Brother-in-law, co-supporter of Moses' arms
- [[Miriam]] — Mother (per 1 Chronicles 2:19)

## Important Verses
- Exodus 17:12 — "Moses' hands were heavy... so they put a stone under him, and he sat thereon... and Hur and Aaron stayed up his hands."

## Cross-References
- Mentioned in: [[Exodus]], [[Deuteronomy]]

## Notes
Hur's name may mean "rock" or "help." He embodies the principle that leadership requires support, and that faithful assistance is itself a form of heroism.

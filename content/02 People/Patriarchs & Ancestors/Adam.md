# Adam

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** First man, first human, progenitor of humanity
- **Family:** Son of God (created directly), father of [[Cain]], [[Abel]], [[Seth]], husband of [[Eve]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Adam was the first human being, created directly by God from the dust of the ground with the breath of life (Genesis 2:7). His name means "man" or "ground" in Hebrew. God placed him in the Garden of [[Eden]] to tend and keep it, giving him dominion over all creation and only one prohibition — not to eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

After the [[Fall of Man]], Adam and Eve were expelled from Eden. Despite the consequences of sin, God clothed Adam and Eve in animal skins — the first act of atonement. Adam lived 930 years, the longest of the pre-flood patriarchs after Methuselah.

## Key Events
- [[Creation]] — Formed from dust, given life by God's breath
- [[Fall of Man]] — Ate the forbidden fruit, brought sin into the world
- Expulsion from the Garden of Eden
- Birth of [[Cain]], [[Abel]], and [[Seth]]

## Relationships
- [[Eve]] — Wife, created from his rib as "bone of my bones"
- [[Cain]] — Firstborn son, killed [[Abel]]
- [[Abel]] — Second son, murdered by Cain
- [[Seth]] — Third son, born after Abel's death, ancestor of the covenant line

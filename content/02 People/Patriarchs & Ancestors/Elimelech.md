# Elimelech

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Naomi's husband
- **Family:** Husband of [[Naomi]], father of Mahlon and Chilion
- **Key Books:** [[Ruth]]

## Biography
Elimelech was a man of Bethlehem who took his family to [[Moab]] during famine. He died there, leaving Naomi widowed and setting the stage for Ruth's loyalty and Boaz's redemption.

## Key Events
- [[Famine in Bethlehem]] - Leaves Bethlehem for Moab
- [[Deaths in Moab]] - Dies in Moab

## Relationships
- [[Naomi]] - Wife
- [[Ruth (Person)|Ruth]] - Daughter-in-law through Mahlon
- [[Boaz]] - Kinsman connected to redemption

## Important Verses
- Ruth 1:2-3 - Elimelech goes to Moab and dies there.

## Cross-References
- Mentioned in: [[Ruth]]

## Notes
Elimelech's name means "my God is king," an important irony in a book set during the days when Israel had no king.

# Aholiab

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Tabernacle craftsman and co-leader of construction alongside Bezalel; from the tribe of Dan
- **Family:** Son of Ahisamach; from the tribe of Dan
- **Key Books:** [[Exodus]]

## Biography
Aholiab son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan, was appointed by God alongside [[Bezalel]] as the co-craftsman responsible for building and furnishing the tabernacle. While [[Bezalel]] is given the title of chief craftsman and described more fully, Aholiab's role is integral: God says, "I have appointed with him Oholiab, the son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan. And I have given to all able men ability, that they may make all that I have commanded you" (Exodus 31:6).

Aholiab's specific skills are enumerated in Exodus 35:34–35: he was gifted to teach others as well as to work, and his abilities extended to engraving, designing, embroidering in blue and purple and scarlet yarn and fine linen, and weaving. This makes him particularly associated with the textile and decorative arts of the tabernacle — the curtains, the priestly garments, and the embroidered screens that formed the boundaries of God's dwelling place.

One of the theologically significant aspects of Aholiab's appointment is tribal. [[Bezalel]] came from Judah — the royal tribe. Aholiab came from Dan — a tribe often associated with problematic episodes in Israel's history (Judges 18; the golden calf at Dan). Yet both were Spirit-equipped for the same holy work. This pairing demonstrates that God's enabling grace distributes gifts across all of Israel, not merely to the most honored tribes.

Together, [[Bezalel]] and Aholiab supervised all the craftsmen who contributed to the tabernacle project. Exodus 36 records that the people brought so many freewill offerings that [[Moses]] had to restrain them from bringing more, showing the enthusiastic cooperation between the Spirit-gifted craftsmen and the Spirit-moved congregation.

## Key Events
- Appointed by God alongside [[Bezalel]] to build the tabernacle (Exodus 31:6)
- Named again in the summary of craftsmen's qualifications (Exodus 35:34–35)
- Leads craftsmen in constructing the tabernacle and its furnishings (Exodus 36)
- Associated specifically with embroidery and textile work for priestly garments and curtains (Exodus 38:23)

## Notes
- Aholiab's tribe of Dan was not among the most prominent in Israel, yet he received the same Spirit-filling as [[Bezalel]] of Judah — a theological signal that gifts are distributed by grace, not status
- His specific abilities in embroidery and teaching make him as important for the aesthetic beauty of the tabernacle as [[Bezalel]] was for its structural elements
- Together, Aholiab and [[Bezalel]] model collaborative, Spirit-led craftsmanship in service of worship

# Joseph (Husband of Mary)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Husband of [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]]; legal father and guardian of [[Jesus Christ]]; carpenter from [[Nazareth]]
- **Family:** Descended from [[David]] (Matthew 1:1-16, Luke 3:23-38); husband of Mary; legal guardian of Jesus
- **Key Books:** [[Matthew]], [[Luke]]

## Biography
Joseph was a righteous man (*dikaios*) of the line of [[David]], living in [[Nazareth]] of Galilee. When he discovered that [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]] was with child before their marriage was consummated, he purposed to put her away privily rather than expose her to public shame — "not willing to make her a publick example" (Matthew 1:19). An angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream and confirmed that the child was conceived of the Holy Ghost, directing him to take Mary as his wife and name the child Jesus. Joseph obeyed immediately: "Then Joseph being raised from sleep did as the angel of the Lord had bidden him" (Matthew 1:24).

Joseph is the silent guardian of the infancy narratives. He received divine guidance through dreams at each critical juncture: the direction to take Mary as his wife (Matthew 1:20-21), the warning to [[Flight to Egypt|flee to Egypt]] before [[Herod the Great]]'s slaughter (Matthew 2:13), the command to return after Herod's death (Matthew 2:19-20), and the redirection to [[Galilee]] rather than [[Judea]] (Matthew 2:22). Four times warned; four times obeyed without question or recorded speech.

Joseph is present with Mary at the [[Presentation of Jesus]] and at the discovery of the [[Boy Jesus in the Temple]]. In Luke 2:33 he is called "his father" and in Luke 2:48 Mary says "thy father and I have sought thee sorrowing." After the temple episode, Joseph drops from the narrative entirely; he is generally understood to have died before Jesus's public ministry.

## Key Events
- [[Annunciation]] — learned of Mary's miraculous conception; chosen to be guardian of the Messiah (Matthew 1:18-25)
- [[Birth of Jesus]] — present at Bethlehem; received the shepherds (Luke 2:1-20)
- [[Presentation of Jesus]] — took Jesus to the temple on the eighth day and for purification (Luke 2:22-38)
- [[Visit of the Magi]] — present when the Magi came; soon after received warning to flee
- [[Flight to Egypt]] — led family to Egypt to escape Herod's massacre
- [[Boy Jesus in the Temple]] — with Mary, found twelve-year-old Jesus in the temple (Luke 2:41-52)

## Notes
- Joseph never speaks in the recorded Gospels — all his communication with God is through dreams and all his action is immediate obedience
- His Davidic lineage is what qualifies Jesus as the legal heir to the throne of David
- Tradition suggests he was considerably older than Mary and died before the public ministry; he is absent from the Passion narrative where Mary appears

# Seth

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Third son of Adam and Eve; forefather of Noah's line
- **Family:** Son of [[Adam]] and [[Eve]]; father of Enosh; ancestor of [[Noah]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Seth was born to [[Adam]] and [[Eve]] after the murder of [[Abel]] by [[Cain]], and his name means "appointed" or "granted," reflecting Eve's declaration that God had appointed her another offspring in Abel's place. He represents the continuation of the godly line through which humanity would eventually receive [[Noah]] and, ultimately, the messianic promise.

The genealogy of Genesis 5 traces a direct line from Seth through Enosh, Kenan, Mahalalel, Jared, [[Enoch]], Methuselah, Lamech, and [[Noah]]. This line is often called the "Sethite line" and is contrasted in the broader narrative with the line of Cain. It is in Seth's generation, with the birth of his son Enosh, that people "began to call upon the name of the LORD" (Genesis 4:26), suggesting that corporate worship of God traces its earliest roots to Seth's family.

Seth lived 912 years according to the Genesis genealogy, fathering Enosh at age 105. While Scripture gives him little narrative beyond his genealogical role, his importance is structural: without Seth there is no line from Adam to Abraham, and ultimately no fulfillment of God's promise. In the New Testament, Luke's genealogy of Jesus traces back through Seth to Adam (Luke 3:38), placing Seth firmly in the lineage of the Messiah.

## Key Events
- [[Eve]] names him "Seth," saying God has appointed him as a replacement for Abel (Genesis 4:25)
- Birth of Enosh, after which people begin calling on the name of the LORD (Genesis 4:26)
- Genealogical bridge from [[Adam]] to [[Noah]] (Genesis 5:3–8)

## Relationships
- [[Adam]] - father
- [[Eve]] - mother
- [[Abel]] - murdered brother, in whose place Seth was given
- [[Cain]] - elder brother
- [[Noah]] - distant descendant through the Sethite line
- [[Enoch]] - descendant

## Notes
- Seth's lineage preserves the thread of covenant faithfulness from creation to [[Noah]] and beyond
- The note in Genesis 4:26 about calling on God's name in Seth's generation suggests early organized worship
- Luke deliberately traces Jesus' ancestry through Seth back to Adam, emphasizing Jesus as the Son of Man for all humanity

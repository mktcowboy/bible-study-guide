# Jacob (Israel)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Third patriarch, father of the 12 tribes of Israel, renamed "Israel"
- **Family:** Son of [[Isaac]] and [[Rebekah]], husband of [[Rachel]] and [[Leah]], father of 12 sons
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Jacob was born grasping his twin brother [[Esau]]'s heel — a fitting introduction for a man who would spend his life grasping and deceiving. He bought Esau's birthright for a bowl of stew, then deceived his blind father [[Isaac]] to steal Esau's blessing, forcing him to flee to his uncle Laban in Haran.

In Haran, Jacob worked 20 years for Laban to marry his daughters [[Leah]] and [[Rachel]]. Through both wives (and their maids Bilhah and Zilpah), Jacob fathered 12 sons who became the tribes of Israel, plus a daughter Dinah. Despite Laban's repeated deception, God blessed Jacob abundantly.

When Jacob returned to Canaan, he feared Esau's wrath but was reconciled with him. On the way, at [[Bethel]], Jacob wrestled with God all night and was renamed "Israel" (he who struggles with God). Jacob favored [[Joseph]], leading to Joseph being sold into slavery by his brothers.

Jacob's family moved to [[Egypt]] during famine, where Joseph had risen to power. Jacob died in Egypt at age 147, asking to be buried in Canaan.

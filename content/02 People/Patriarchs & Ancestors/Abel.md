# Abel

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Second son of [[Adam]] and [[Eve]], first martyr, shepherd
- **Family:** Son of Adam and Eve, brother of [[Cain]] and [[Seth]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Abel was the second son of [[Adam]] and [[Eve]], a keeper of sheep. He brought the firstlings of his flock and their fat portions as an offering to God, which the LORD looked upon with favor. In contrast, his brother [[Cain]]'s offering of fruit from the ground was not accepted.

Jealous and angry, Cain lured Abel into the field and murdered him — making Abel the first human victim of violence and the first martyr. Though dead, Abel's faith still speaks, and his blood cried out from the ground for justice.

## Key Events
- [[Cain and Abel]] — Offering accepted by God, murdered by Cain
- His blood crying from the ground — first mention of blood vengeance/justice

## Relationships
- [[Adam]] & [[Eve]] — Parents
- [[Cain]] — Older brother, murderer
- [[Seth]] — Younger brother, born after Abel's death

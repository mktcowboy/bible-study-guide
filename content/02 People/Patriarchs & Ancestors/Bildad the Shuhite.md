# Bildad the Shuhite

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Second of Job's three friends, arguing from inherited tradition
- **Family:** Shuhite
- **Key Books:** [[Job]]

## Biography
Bildad appeals to the wisdom of former generations, insisting that God does not pervert justice and that suffering signals guilt. His speeches are shorter and sharper than Eliphaz's, and he presses the logic that Job's children must have sinned if they died under judgment.

By the third cycle, Bildad's contribution shrinks to a brief statement about human impurity before God, showing the friends' theology running out of explanatory power.

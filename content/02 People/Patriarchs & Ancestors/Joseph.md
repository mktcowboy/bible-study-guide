# Joseph

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Favored son of [[Jacob]], dreamer, slave, prisoner, vizier of Egypt, savior of his family
- **Family:** Son of Jacob and [[Rachel]], brother of Benjamin and 9 half-brothers, father of Manasseh and Ephraim
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Joseph was the firstborn son of Jacob and Rachel, making him Jacob's favorite. Jacob gave him a coat of many colors, fueling his brothers' jealousy. At age 17, Joseph had dreams of supremacy — his sheaf bowing to his brothers', then his sun, moon, and 11 stars bowing to him.

His brothers sold him into slavery to Ishmaelite traders for 20 shekels of silver, telling Jacob he was torn apart by a wild animal. In Egypt, Joseph served Potiphar but was falsely accused by Potiphar's wife and thrown into prison. In prison, he interpreted dreams accurately.

When Pharaoh had troubling dreams, Joseph interpreted them — 7 years of plenty followed by 7 years of famine. At age 30, Joseph rose to second-in-command of all Egypt. He stored grain during plenty, then controlled distribution during famine.

When his brothers came to Egypt for food, Joseph tested them, then revealed himself, weeping. He forgave them completely: "You meant evil against me, but God meant it for good." He brought his entire family to Egypt, settling them in Goshen.

Joseph died at age 110, asking his brothers to carry his bones out of Egypt when God brought them to the Promised Land — a request fulfilled centuries later during the Exodus.

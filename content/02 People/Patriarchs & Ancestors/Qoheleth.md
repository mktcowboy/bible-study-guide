# Qoheleth

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Speaker of Ecclesiastes, traditionally identified with Solomon
- **Family:** Presented as "son of David, king in Jerusalem"
- **Key Books:** [[Ecclesiastes]]

## Biography
Qoheleth is the Hebrew title behind "the Preacher" or "the Teacher" in Ecclesiastes. The speaker takes up a royal Solomonic voice to test wisdom, pleasure, labor, wealth, and legacy under the sun.

Whether read as Solomon directly or as a later wisdom writer using Solomonic persona, Qoheleth's role is to press human wisdom to its limits and drive the reader toward fearing God and keeping his commandments.

## Key Events
- Experiments with wisdom, pleasure, and works
- Times and seasons meditation
- Final call to fear God

## Notes
Qoheleth's voice is intentionally searching and unsettling, making Ecclesiastes a necessary companion to Proverbs' confident wisdom.

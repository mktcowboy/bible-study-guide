# Eliphaz the Temanite

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** First and most prominent of Job's three friends
- **Family:** Temanite, likely associated with Edom
- **Key Books:** [[Job]]

## Biography
Eliphaz comes to comfort [[Job]] after his catastrophic losses. He begins with a comparatively restrained appeal to experience and a frightening night vision, arguing that no mortal can be pure before God.

Across the dialogue cycles, Eliphaz moves from general retributive wisdom to direct accusation. By Job 22 he charges Job with exploitation and injustice without evidence, which is why the LORD later rebukes him and the other friends for not speaking rightly.

## Key Events
- Job's friends arrive and sit in silence
- First cycle of speeches
- Third cycle accusation

Eliphaz represents religious explanation at its most polished and dangerous: true general principles become false witness when applied without knowledge.

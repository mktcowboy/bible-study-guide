# Esau (Edom)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Firstborn of [[Isaac]], sold his birthright, father of the Edomites
- **Family:** Son of [[Isaac]] and [[Rebekah]], twin brother of [[Jacob]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Esau was born first, emerging red and hairy, with Jacob grasping his heel. Named "hairy" in Hebrew (same root as Edom, "red"), he was a skilled hunter favored by his father [[Isaac]]. His mother [[Rebekah]] favored Jacob.

At age 30+, Esau returned hungry from the field. Jacob was cooking lentil stew. Esau, desperate, traded his birthright for a meal — despising his privilege. Years later, when Isaac planned to bless Esau, Rebekah and Jacob deceived Isaac, and Jacob received the blessing meant for Esau.

Enraged, Esau planned to kill Jacob after their parents' funeral. Rebekah sent Jacob away, and he fled to Haran for 20 years. Before returning, Jacob feared Esau's wrath, but they embraced and wept — reconciled. Esau took the land of Seir and became known as Edom, founding a nation that would often oppose Israel.

## Key Events
- Sold birthright for stew
- Deprived of blessing by Jacob's deception
- Planned to kill Jacob
- Reconciled with Jacob after 20 years
- Took Seir — became Edom

## Relationships
- [[Isaac]] & [[Rebekah]] — Parents

# Ephraim

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Son of Joseph; tribe whose name often represents the northern kingdom
- **Family:** Son of [[Joseph]], brother of Manasseh
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]], [[Joshua]], [[Hosea]]

## Biography
Ephraim was Joseph's younger son, blessed by [[Jacob]] ahead of his older brother Manasseh. The tribe of Ephraim became one of the leading tribes in Israel and, in the prophets, "Ephraim" often functions as shorthand for the northern kingdom.

In [[Hosea]], Ephraim is the beloved yet wayward son whom God cannot simply give up. The name carries both judgment and deep divine grief.

## Key Events
- Jacob blesses Ephraim and Manasseh
- Northern kingdom apostasy and exile
- Hosea's restoration promises

## Notes
Hosea's repeated "Ephraim" language makes the northern kingdom personal: not merely a failed state, but a beloved son under discipline.

# Melchizedek

## Overview
Melchizedek was king of Salem and priest of the Most High God who blessed [[Abraham]] after Abraham's victory in Genesis 14.

## Biblical Significance
Melchizedek becomes a major typological figure in [[Hebrews]], where his priesthood is used to explain Christ's eternal priesthood beyond the Levitical order.

## Key Events
- [[Abrahamic Covenant]] — Melchizedek appears in the wider Abraham narrative

## Notes
Psalm 110 and Hebrews develop Melchizedek as a pattern for the Messiah's royal priesthood.

# Isaac

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Son of promise, second patriarch, child of miracle
- **Family:** Son of [[Abraham]] and [[Sarah]], husband of [[Rebekah]], father of [[Jacob]] and [[Esau]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Isaac was born to [[Abraham]] and [[Sarah]] when they were 100 and 90 years old — a miraculous child of God's promise. Named "laughing," he was the child Abraham was willing to sacrifice on Mount Moriah, where God provided a ram instead.

Isaac married [[Rebekah]] at age 40, arranged by Abraham's servant. When Rebekah became pregnant, twins struggled within her. God revealed that two nations were in her womb and the older would serve the younger. Esau was born first, followed by Jacob grasping Esau's heel.

Isaac repeated Abraham's mistake of claiming his wife was his sister in Gerab. He favored Esau (a hunter) while Rebekah favored Jacob. In old age and blindness, Isaac attempted to bless Esau, but Rebekah and Jacob deceived him. After the deception, Isaac blessed both sons with prophetic words that shaped the future of Israel and Edom.

Isaac died at age 180, buried by Jacob and Esau in the cave of Machpelah.

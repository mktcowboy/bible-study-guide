# Enoch

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both
- **Role:** Antediluvian patriarch who "walked with God" and was taken by God without dying
- **Family:** Son of Jared; father of Methuselah; ancestor of [[Noah]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]], [[Hebrews]], [[Jude]]

## Biography
Enoch is one of the most intriguing figures in all of Scripture. Appearing in the genealogy of Genesis 5, he is the seventh from Adam through the Sethite line, a detail noted with significance by Jude. Among the long-lived antediluvian patriarchs, Enoch's 365 years stands as the shortest lifespan in the list — and yet his entry ends with a remarkable distinction: "Enoch walked with God, and he was not, for God took him" (Genesis 5:24).

The phrase "walked with God" occurs elsewhere in Genesis only of [[Noah]] (Genesis 6:9), and it conveys a life of intimate communion, moral alignment, and faithful obedience to God. Unlike every other name in the Genesis 5 genealogy, Enoch's entry does not say "and he died." Instead, the text states he "was not" — a Hebrew idiom for ceasing to exist on earth — because God took him. This mysterious departure has made Enoch one of the two figures in the Bible (along with Elijah) who were translated to God without experiencing death.

Enoch's son, Methuselah, is famous for having the longest recorded lifespan at 969 years, dying in the very year of the flood. This suggests that Methuselah's name — possibly meaning "his death shall bring" — may carry prophetic significance, with his lifespan serving as a divine countdown to judgment.

The New Testament elevates Enoch significantly. Hebrews 11 includes him in the roll call of faith: "By faith Enoch was taken up so that he should not see death, and he was not found, because God had taken him. Now before he was taken he was commended as having pleased God" (Hebrews 11:5). Jude 14–15 quotes a prophecy attributed to Enoch — "Behold, the Lord comes with ten thousands of his holy ones, to execute judgment" — making him one of the earliest prophets in the biblical tradition, though this quotation comes from the extra-canonical Book of Enoch.

## Key Events
- Born to Jared at his 162nd year (Genesis 5:18)
- Fathers Methuselah at age 65 (Genesis 5:21)
- Walks with God for 300 years after Methuselah's birth (Genesis 5:22)
- Taken by God at age 365 without dying (Genesis 5:24)
- Commended in the faith hall of fame (Hebrews 11:5)
- Quoted as an early prophet in Jude 14–15

## Notes
- Enoch and Elijah are the only two biblical figures translated to God without experiencing death, a pattern some connect to the two witnesses of Revelation 11
- His "walking with God" becomes a model of covenantal faithfulness, invoked by Noah, Abraham, and others
- The extra-canonical Book of Enoch circulated widely in the Second Temple period and is directly quoted in the letter of Jude

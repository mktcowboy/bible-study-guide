# Dathan

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Rebel leader from the tribe of Reuben
- **Family:** Son of Eliab, member of the tribe of Reuben
- **Key Books:** [[Numbers]]

## Biography
Dathan was a Reubenite who, along with his brother [[Abiram]] and [[Korah]] (a Levite), led a rebellion against the authority of [[Moses]] and [[Aaron]]. They challenged Moses' exclusive leadership, claiming "all the congregation are holy, every one of them, and the LORD is among them: wherefore then lift ye yourselves up above the congregation of the LORD?" (Numbers 16:3). God judged the rebellion dramatically — the earth opened and swallowed Dathan, Abiram, and their households, while fire consumed the 250 men who offered incense. Dathan and Abiram's names appear again during the wilderness complaints (Numbers 20:1–5), suggesting their followers continued the spirit of rebellion.

## Key Events

## Notes
Dathan's name means "he who destroys." Ironically, he was the one destroyed. His rebellion represents the danger of pride and rejecting God-appointed authority.

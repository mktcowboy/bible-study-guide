# Benjamin

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Youngest son of [[Jacob]], full brother of [[Joseph]], father of the tribe of Benjamin
- **Family:** Son of Jacob and [[Rachel]], brother of Joseph
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Benjamin was the youngest son of [[Jacob]] and [[Rachel]], born during the journey from Bethel to Ephrathah (Bethlehem). His mother Rachel died giving birth to him, and he was originally named "Ben-oni" (son of my sorrow), but Jacob renamed him "Benjamin" (son of my right hand).

Benjamin was the only full brother of Joseph — both were Rachel's sons. This made him Jacob's most treasured child after Joseph disappeared. When famine struck, Jacob refused to let Benjamin go to Egypt with his brothers, only agreeing when food ran out.

In Egypt, Benjamin was used as bait by Joseph to test his brothers' loyalty. Unlike the first time when they sold Joseph, the brothers now defended Benjamin with their lives — showing their transformation. Benjamin remained in Egypt with the family during the famine years.

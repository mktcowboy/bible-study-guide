# Joshua (Son of Nun)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Moses' aide, warrior, faithful spy, and successor as leader of Israel
- **Family:** Son of Nun, of the tribe of Ephraim
- **Key Books:** [[Exodus]], [[Numbers]], [[Deuteronomy]], [[Joshua]]

## Biography
Originally named Hoshea ("salvation"), [[Moses]] changed his name to Joshua ("Yahweh saves"). Joshua began as Moses' personal attendant ("minister"), serving him at the tabernacle.

At the battle with Amalek, Joshua led Israel's forces while Moses prayed on the hill — Israel prevailed as long as Moses' hands were held up. Joshua was one of the twelve spies sent to scout [[Canaan]], and only he and [[Caleb]] gave a faithful report; because of his faith, God promised him the land.

After Moses' death, God commissioned Joshua to lead Israel into the Promised Land. He led the crossing of the Jordan, the conquest of Jericho, Ai, and Gibeon, divided the land among the tribes, and renewed the covenant at Shechem before his death at 110.

## Key Events
- Served as Moses' aide and minister
- Fought Amalek at Rephidim
- Faithful spy in Canaan with [[Caleb]]
- Appointed Moses' successor

# Tychicus

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Trusted messenger and co-worker of Paul; carried the letters to Ephesians, Colossians, and Philemon; described as "beloved brother"
- **Family:** From Asia (province); likely from Ephesus or vicinity
- **Key Books:** [[Acts]], [[Ephesians]], [[Colossians]], [[2 Timothy]], [[Titus]]

## Biography
Tychicus was one of Paul's most trusted couriers and co-workers in the later stages of his missionary career. He is consistently described with warm affirmation: "beloved brother and faithful minister in the Lord" (Ephesians 6:21) and "beloved brother and faithful minister and fellow servant in the Lord" (Colossians 4:7). These repeated descriptions suggest a man whose character had been tested and proven over years of partnership with Paul.

He first appears in Acts 20:4 as one of the delegation of representatives from various churches who accompanied Paul on his final journey to Jerusalem, likely carrying the collection for the Jerusalem church. Tychicus is identified as being "from Asia" — the Roman province in western Asia Minor — pointing to a base in or around Ephesus. This geography makes him a natural choice for carrying the circular letters addressed to churches in that region.

Paul entrusted Tychicus with carrying two of his prison letters — Ephesians and Colossians — and specifically instructed each congregation to hear Tychicus deliver the full report of Paul's situation that no letter could fully convey. Ephesians 6:21–22 states: "So that you also may know how I am and what I am doing, Tychicus the beloved brother and faithful minister in the Lord will tell you everything. I have sent him to you for this very purpose, that you may know how we are, and that he may encourage your hearts." The same personal function is assigned in Colossians 4:7–9, where Tychicus travels with [[Onesimus]] back to the church at Colossae.

In the pastoral letters, Paul mentions him twice more. 2 Timothy 4:12 notes that "Tychicus I have sent to Ephesus" — possibly to relieve [[Timothy]] there. Titus 3:12 indicates Paul was planning to send either Artemas or Tychicus to Titus in Crete so Titus could join Paul. This versatility — able to carry letters, deliver oral reports, stand in for other workers, and pastor churches temporarily — makes Tychicus an example of the kind of reliable, multi-skilled co-worker that sustained Paul's network of churches during his imprisonments.

## Key Events
- Travels with Paul's delegation to Jerusalem, carrying the collection (Acts 20:4)
- Carries the letter to Ephesus with authorization to answer all questions orally (Ephesians 6:21–22)
- Carries the letter to Colossae together with [[Onesimus]] (Colossians 4:7–9)
- Sent to Ephesus, possibly to relieve [[Timothy]] (2 Timothy 4:12)
- Considered for assignment to Crete to relieve [[Titus]] (Titus 3:12)

## Notes
- Tychicus appears in five separate New Testament books, making him one of the most widely attested co-workers in Paul's network
- His role as letter carrier was not merely logistical — he was expected to answer questions, provide oral context, and encourage the congregations, meaning Paul trusted his judgment as well as his loyalty
- The combination of "beloved" and "faithful" in Paul's descriptions is significant — many people are beloved; fewer are consistently faithful enough to be entrusted with critical cross-church communications

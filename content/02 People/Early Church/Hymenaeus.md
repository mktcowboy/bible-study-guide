# Hymenaeus

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** False teacher whom Paul handed over to Satan; claimed the resurrection had already occurred
- **Family:** No background details given; a member of or teacher in the Ephesian church
- **Key Books:** [[1 Timothy]], [[2 Timothy]]

## Biography
Hymenaeus appears twice in Paul's letters to [[Timothy]], both times as a warning example of false teaching and its consequences. He is one of the most clearly identified false teachers in the New Testament, named explicitly alongside specific theological errors — a practice Paul sometimes employed as a public warning to the churches.

In 1 Timothy 1:19–20, Paul writes: "By rejecting this, some have made shipwreck of their faith, among whom are Hymenaeus and Alexander, whom I have handed over to Satan that they may learn not to blaspheme." The language of "handing over to Satan" echoes Paul's instruction in 1 Corinthians 5:5 about the immoral man in Corinth — a form of excommunication, removing someone from the protection of the Christian community so that the consequences of their choices might bring them to repentance. The purpose was disciplinary rather than merely punitive: "that they may learn not to blaspheme."

In 2 Timothy 2:17–18, Paul names Hymenaeus again alongside [[Philetus]] and specifies their error more precisely: "their talk will spread like gangrene. Among them are Hymenaeus and Philetus, who have swerved from the truth, saying that the resurrection has already happened. They are upsetting the faith of some." The denial of a future bodily resurrection was a significant theological error. Whether they were teaching a purely spiritual or realized eschatology — that the resurrection referred to spiritual regeneration or had occurred metaphorically — is not specified, but the effect was to undermine the foundational Christian hope of bodily resurrection (1 Corinthians 15).

The specific identification of this error as "gangrene" (the Greek literally means the rotting flesh disease) is striking in its physical imagery — Paul is saying that this teaching spreads, corrupts, and kills spiritual vitality the way gangrene destroys living tissue. Hymenaeus's story is a cautionary portrait of a teacher whose trajectory moved from shipwrecked faith to active infection of others.

## Key Events
- "Handed over to Satan" by Paul for blasphemy (1 Timothy 1:20)
- Named with [[Philetus]] as teaching that the resurrection has already happened (2 Timothy 2:17–18)
- Teaching identified as spreading "like gangrene" and upsetting faith (2 Timothy 2:17–18)

## Notes
- Hymenaeus is one of very few individuals in the New Testament named in a formal context of church discipline, making his story one of the clearest case studies of apostolic correction of false teaching
- The "resurrection has already happened" error likely anticipated later Gnostic tendencies that spiritualized resurrection and denied bodily hope — Paul's sharp response signals how central bodily resurrection is to Christian theology
- The fact that Hymenaeus appears in both pastoral letters (years apart) suggests his influence persisted and required repeated public correction

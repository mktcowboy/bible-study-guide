# Stephanas

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Paul's first convert in Achaia (Corinth); his household baptized by Paul; visited Paul in Ephesus with Fortunatus and Achaicus
- **Family:** Head of a household in Corinth; no individual family details given
- **Key Books:** [[1 Corinthians]]

## Biography
Stephanas heads one of the few households that Paul explicitly mentions baptizing personally during his Corinthian ministry (1 Corinthians 1:16). Paul normally delegated baptism to colleagues, so this exception signals Stephanas's special place in the founding of the Corinthian church. More significantly, Paul identifies the household of Stephanas as "the first converts in Achaia" (1 Corinthians 16:15) — the literal first fruits of the gospel in the entire region of Greece south of Macedonia, making Stephanas's household a landmark in the expansion of the church into Europe.

Paul's description of Stephanas and his household goes further than noting their priority in time. He writes that "they have devoted themselves to the service of the saints" (1 Corinthians 16:15), indicating a sustained, self-giving orientation toward the community's practical needs. This voluntary service — diaconal ministry before there was a formal diaconate — led Paul to urge the Corinthians to "be subject to such as these, and to every fellow worker and laborer" (1 Corinthians 16:16). Paul was asking the fractious Corinthian church to submit to the spiritual authority of those who had proven their character through humble service.

Stephanas, along with [[Fortunatus]] and [[Achaicus]], visited Paul during his Ephesian ministry — a visit that refreshed Paul greatly. They may have been carrying the Corinthians' letter to Paul (to which 1 Corinthians is largely a reply), or they may have come independently to consult with him. Either way, Paul writes that "they refreshed my spirit as well as yours" (1 Corinthians 16:18) and urges the Corinthians to "give recognition to such men."

## Key Events
- His household baptized personally by Paul during the founding of the Corinthian church (1 Corinthians 1:16)

- His household devoted to service of the saints (1 Corinthians 16:15)
- Visits Paul in Ephesus with [[Fortunatus]] and [[Achaicus]] (1 Corinthians 16:17–18)

## Notes
- Being the "first converts in Achaia" places Stephanas's household at the absolute beginning of Greek Christianity, making them historically significant beyond their individual roles
- Paul's instruction to submit to people like Stephanas (1 Corinthians 16:16) grounds ecclesiastical authority not in office or appointment but in demonstrated service and character
- The visit by Stephanas, [[Fortunatus]], and Achaicus is the most specific personal context provided in 1 Corinthians for the letter's composition — they were Paul's source of information about Corinthian problems

# Aristarchus

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Macedonian from Thessalonica; travel companion of Paul from Greece to Jerusalem, and on the voyage to Rome
- **Family:** From Thessalonica in Macedonia
- **Key Books:** [[Acts]], [[Colossians]], [[Philemon]]

## Biography
Aristarchus was a Macedonian from Thessalonica who became one of Paul's most faithful long-distance travel companions. He is first mentioned in Acts 19:29, where he and a companion named Gaius were seized by the Ephesian mob during the riot instigated by Demetrius the silversmith. The crowd dragged them into the theater — a terrifying and potentially lethal experience — while Paul himself was being restrained from entering by his friends. Aristarchus thus shared bodily danger on Paul's behalf before he even appears in a missionary role.

Acts 20:4 lists Aristarchus among the delegation that accompanied Paul on his final journey from Greece to Jerusalem, carrying the collection for the poor among the saints there. He is identified specifically as "Aristarchus and Secundus of the Thessalonians," confirming his Thessalonian origin. This journey was particularly fraught — Paul had already received multiple warnings that imprisonment awaited him in Jerusalem.

Aristarchus appears again in Acts 27:2, where he is explicitly named as accompanying Paul on the voyage to Rome: "And embarking in a ship of Adramyttium, which was about to sail to the ports along the coast of Asia, we put to sea, accompanied by Aristarchus, a Macedonian from Thessalonica." This makes Aristarchus a witness to the famous storm and shipwreck on the island of Malta (Acts 27–28), one of the most dramatic narratives in Scripture.

In Paul's prison letters, Aristarchus is described as a "fellow prisoner" (Colossians 4:10) and a "fellow worker" (Philemon 24). Whether "fellow prisoner" is literal imprisonment or a metaphorical description of willing captivity to Christ is debated, but the pattern of his appearances consistently places him at Paul's side in dangerous circumstances.

## Key Events
- Seized by the Ephesian mob during the riot at the theater (Acts 19:29)
- Travels with Paul's delegation from Greece to Jerusalem (Acts 20:4)
- Accompanies Paul on the voyage to Rome (Acts 27:2)
- Survives the shipwreck at Malta with Paul (Acts 27–28)
- Named as "fellow prisoner" and greeter in Colossians (Colossians 4:10)
- Named as "fellow worker" in Philemon (Philemon 24)

## Notes
- Aristarchus is consistently present at the most dangerous moments of Paul's later ministry — the Ephesian riot, the Jerusalem journey, the Rome voyage — suggesting an exceptional willingness to share Paul's suffering
- His Thessalonian origin connects him to the earliest Pauline churches; the faith of the Thessalonian congregation had broad and lasting fruit
- As a witness to the Rome voyage, Aristarchus would have been among the eyewitnesses behind the extraordinarily detailed shipwreck narrative in Acts 27

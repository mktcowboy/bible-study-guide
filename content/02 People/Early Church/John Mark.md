# John Mark

## Overview
John Mark was an early Christian worker associated with [[Peter]], [[Barnabas]], and [[Paul (Apostle)]]. Christian tradition identifies him as the author of [[Mark]].

## Biblical Significance
Acts presents John Mark as a companion whose early departure created tension between Paul and Barnabas, while later letters show him restored as useful for ministry.

## Key People Associated
- [[Peter]] — Traditional source behind Mark's Gospel
- [[Barnabas]] — Kinsman and early ministry companion
- [[Paul (Apostle)]] — Later valued Mark's service

## Related Notes
- [[Mark]]
- [[Acts]]

## Notes
John Mark's story is a quiet witness to restoration and usefulness after ministry failure.

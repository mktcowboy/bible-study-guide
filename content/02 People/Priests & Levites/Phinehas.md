# Phinehas

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Priest, son of Eleazar and grandson of Aaron
- **Family:** Son of [[Eleazar]], grandson of [[Aaron]]
- **Key Books:** [[Numbers]], [[Joshua]], [[Judges]]

## Biography
Phinehas is remembered for zeal in stopping Israel's sin at Baal-peor, for which the LORD grants him a covenant of peace and continuing priesthood. In Joshua, he helps investigate the altar built by the eastern tribes and receives their explanation before civil war breaks out.

## Key Events
- [[Baal Peor]] — Acts zealously to stop covenant rebellion
- [[Eastern Tribes Return]] — Leads the inquiry into the altar by the Jordan
- [[Death of Eleazar]] — Continues the priestly line after Eleazar

## Relationships
- [[Eleazar]] — Father
- [[Aaron]] — Grandfather
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Contemporary leader in the conquest generation

## Important Verses
> "Phinehas, the son of Eleazar, the son of Aaron the priest, hath turned my wrath away..." — Numbers 25:11

## Cross-References
- Mentioned in: [[Numbers]], [[Joshua]], [[Judges]]

## Notes
Phinehas combines priestly zeal with careful investigation, especially in Joshua 22.

# Nadab

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Firstborn son of Aaron, priest
- **Family:** Eldest son of Aaron and Elisheba, grandson of Amram
- **Key Books:** [[Exodus]], [[Leviticus]]

## Biography
Nadab was the firstborn son of [[Aaron]] and [[Elisheba]]. He was consecrated as a priest alongside his father and brothers on the eighth day of the tabernacle dedication (Leviticus 8). On that same day, Nadab and his brother [[Abihu]] offered "strange fire" before the Lord — fire that God had not commanded. Fire came out from the presence of the Lord and consumed them, and they died before the Lord (Leviticus 10:1–2). This shocking event established the principle that worship of God must be on His terms alone. Nadab had children (1 Chronicles 6:3), so his lineage continued despite his untimely death.

## Notes
Nadab's name means "he will give" or "he gives." His death serves as a sobering reminder that God takes worship seriously and demands approach on His terms alone.

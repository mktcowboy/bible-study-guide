# Eleazar

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** High priest (succeeded Aaron)
- **Family:** Son of Aaron and Elisheba, grandson of Amram
- **Key Books:** [[Leviticus]], [[Numbers]], [[Joshua]]

## Biography
Eleazar was the third son of [[Aaron]] and [[Elisheba]]. After the deaths of his brothers [[Nadab]] and [[Abihu]] in the fire of the Lord (Leviticus 10), and eventually after [[Aaron]]'s death on [[Mount Hor]], Eleazar became the high priest of Israel. He served as the chief priest throughout the wilderness wanderings and into the conquest of Canaan. He received the breastplate of judgment from his father Aaron (Numbers 8:25) and cast the lots for dividing the Promised Land among the tribes (Joshua 18:10). The priestly line through Eleazar became the dominant line, and the Messiah would eventually descend from this lineage (according to Matthew's genealogy).

## Key Events
- [[Tabernacle Consecration]] — Ordained as priest alongside his father and brothers
- [[Death of Nadab and Abihu]] — Survived while his brothers perished
- [[Death of Aaron]] — Succeeded as high priest on Mount Hor
- Division of Canaan — Cast lots for tribal inheritances

## Relationships
- [[Aaron]] — Father, first high priest
- [[Moses]] — Close ally and mediator
- [[Ithamar]] — Brother, fellow priest

## Important Verses

- Joshua 14:1 — "These are the countries which Eleazar the priest... divided for inheritance."

## Notes
Eleazar's name means "God will help." He represents continuity of the priesthood and faithful service through transition of leadership.

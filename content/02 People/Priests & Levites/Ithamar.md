# Ithamar

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Priest
- **Family:** Youngest son of Aaron and Elisheba
- **Key Books:** [[Leviticus]], [[Numbers]], [[1 Samuel]]

## Biography
Ithamar was the youngest of [[Aaron]]'s four sons. Along with his brother [[Eleazar]], he survived the judgment that consumed [[Nadab]] and [[Abihu]] when they offered strange fire before the Lord (Leviticus 10). After Aaron's death, Ithamar continued serving as a priest under Eleazar's leadership as high priest. The priestly line of Ithamar produced a significant branch — eventually [[Abiathar]], priest during [[David]]'s reign, descended from Ithamar (1 Chronicles 24:3–6). The twenty-four priestly courses established by David included eight from Eleazar's line and sixteen from Ithamar's, showing Ithamar's line grew substantially.

## Notes
Ithamar's name means "pillar of the sea." His priestly line outlasted Eleazar's in certain branches and produced notable priests during the monarchy.

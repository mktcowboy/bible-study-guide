# Zechariah (Priest)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Levitical priest of the division of Abijah; father of [[John the Baptist]]; husband of [[Elizabeth]]
- **Family:** Husband of [[Elizabeth]] (a descendant of Aaron); father of [[John the Baptist]]
- **Key Books:** [[Luke]]

## Biography
Zechariah and [[Elizabeth]] were both "righteous before God, walking in all the commandments and ordinances of the Lord blameless" (Luke 1:6), yet they were childless and well advanced in years — a situation that echoed the stories of [[Abraham]] and [[Sarah]], [[Elkanah]] and [[Hannah]], setting the reader to expect extraordinary divine intervention.

While serving his priestly division in the temple, Zechariah was chosen by lot to burn incense in the Holy Place — a once-in-a-lifetime honor for a common priest. The angel [[Gabriel]] appeared to him and announced that his wife would bear a son to be named John: great before the Lord, filled with the Holy Ghost from his mother's womb, turning many of the children of Israel to God, coming "in the spirit and power of [[Elijah]]" to make ready a people prepared for the Lord. Zechariah questioned the promise ("I am an old man, and my wife well stricken in years," Luke 1:18) and was struck mute until the promise was fulfilled.

Zechariah remained mute throughout the pregnancy. At the circumcision of the child, when the family expected to name him after his father, Elizabeth declared his name was John. The relatives appealed to Zechariah, who asked for a writing table and wrote "His name is John" (Luke 1:63). Immediately his mouth was opened and his tongue loosed, and he praised God. His prophetic canticle — the Benedictus (Luke 1:68-79) — is among the great NT hymns, tracing the promised redemption from David through to his son's role as prophet preparing the way.

## Key Events
- [[Annunciation]] — Gabriel's announcement to Zechariah in the temple of the coming birth of John (Luke 1:8-22)
- [[Visitation]] — his wife Elizabeth's encounter with Mary (Luke 1:39-56); Zechariah is still mute
- [[Birth of John the Baptist]] — his muteness broken; the Benedictus spoken (Luke 1:57-80)

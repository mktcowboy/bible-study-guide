# John (Apostle)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** One of the Twelve Apostles; brother of [[James (son of Zebedee)]]; member of Jesus' inner three; traditionally identified as the beloved disciple and author of [[John]], [[1 John]], [[2 John]], [[3 John]], and [[Revelation]]
- **Family:** Son of Zebedee; brother of [[James (son of Zebedee)]]
- **Key Books:** [[Matthew]], [[Mark]], [[Luke]], [[John]], [[Acts]], [[1 John]], [[2 John]], [[3 John]], [[Revelation]]

## Biography

John was a Galilean fisherman, the son of Zebedee and brother of [[James (son of Zebedee)]]. He and James were called by [[Jesus Christ]] while mending nets beside the Sea of Galilee. They left their father, the boat, and the hired servants to follow Jesus. Together with [[Peter]] and James, John became part of the inner circle that witnessed events not shown to all the disciples.

Jesus gave James and John the name Boanerges, meaning "sons of thunder" (Mark 3:17). The title fits their early zeal. When a Samaritan village refused to receive Jesus, the brothers wanted to call down fire from heaven, but Jesus rebuked them. Their mother also asked that they sit at Jesus' right and left hand in the kingdom. John therefore appears not as naturally gentle from the beginning, but as a fervent disciple whose zeal had to be reshaped by the cross.

John was present at the raising of Jairus's daughter, the [[Transfiguration]], and [[Gethsemane]]. In the Gospel of John, he is traditionally identified with "the disciple whom Jesus loved." This disciple reclined near Jesus at the [[Last Supper]], stood near the cross with [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]], received Jesus' charge to care for her, ran with Peter to the empty tomb, and recognized the risen Lord by the Sea of Tiberias.

In Acts, John appears with Peter as a leading witness in Jerusalem. He went with Peter to the temple when the lame man was healed, stood before the council, and helped confirm the Samaritan mission. Paul later calls John one of the "pillars" of the Jerusalem church. The Johannine writings emphasize Jesus as the Word made flesh, the Son of God, the giver of life, the light of the world, and the one whose love shapes the church's life.

Church tradition remembers John as the last surviving apostle, associated especially with Ephesus and exile on Patmos. [[Revelation]] presents him as receiving visions of Christ's glory, the church's struggle, judgment, and the final new creation.

## Notes
- John is both a son of thunder and the apostle most associated with love; the contrast shows discipleship as formation, not merely temperament.
- The Gospel of John never names the beloved disciple directly, but historic Christian tradition strongly associates him with John the apostle.
- John is central for high Christology: Word, Son, Lamb, light, life, truth, vine, shepherd, and risen Lord.
- His writings hold together doctrinal clarity and pastoral warmth: truth and love are not rivals.

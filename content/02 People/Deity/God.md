# God

> **God = the LORD = Yahweh.** This is the single canonical node for the one true God. "God" (Elohim) is the general name; "the LORD" (small caps in the KJV) renders the covenant name; "Yahweh" / "Jehovah" ("I AM") *is* that covenant name; "Lord" (Adonai) is the title meaning master. All point to the same Person — link to `[[God]]` everywhere (aliases: LORD, Yahweh, Jehovah, Lord all resolve here).

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both — the same God throughout the whole canon
- **Names:** God (Elohim) · the LORD / GOD in small caps (the covenant name) · Yahweh / Jehovah — "I AM THAT I AM" (Exodus 3:14) · Adonai ("Lord," master) · and many compound titles (see below)
- **Nature:** One God (Deuteronomy 6:4) — eternal, self-existent Spirit (John 4:24), the uncreated Creator of all things
- **Revealed as:** Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — one God in three persons; fully made known in [[Jesus Christ]] and by [[The Holy Spirit]]
- **Role:** Creator, covenant-maker, lawgiver, judge, redeemer, king, and the everlasting dwelling of His people
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]], [[Exodus]], [[Deuteronomy]], [[Psalms]], [[Isaiah]], [[John]], [[Romans]], [[Revelation]] (developed in every book)

## Biography
God is the eternal, self-existent Creator who speaks the universe into being (see [[Creation]]), judges the world's sin in [[The Flood]] and at the [[Tower of Babel]], and then sets in motion a single rescue plan. He calls [[Abraham]] and binds Himself by oath in the [[Abrahamic Covenant]]; He hears Israel's cry, reveals His personal name Yahweh to [[Moses]] at the [[Burning Bush]], and delivers His people in [[The Exodus]] and the [[Red Sea Crossing]]. At [[Mount Sinai]] He gives the Law (see [[Giving of the Law]]) and comes to dwell among His people in tabernacle and [[The Temple]]. He raises up [[David]] and swears the [[The Davidic Covenant]], speaks through the prophets ([[Isaiah (Prophet)|Isaiah]], [[Jeremiah (Prophet)|Jeremiah]], [[Ezekiel (Prophet)|Ezekiel]]), and disciplines and restores Israel. Finally He reveals Himself most fully in the incarnation of His Son, [[Jesus Christ]], the eternal [[The Word]] made flesh — and He will dwell with His people forever in [[The New Creation]]. The story of God is the story of the whole Bible: holy and just, yet "merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth" (Exodus 34:6).

## The Names and Titles of God
God's names are not labels but self-revelations of His character. See the theme hubs [[The Name of God]] and [[The Shema]].

**The primary names**
- **Elohim** — the general word "God," a plural of majesty; the mighty Creator (Genesis 1:1).
- **YHWH / the LORD (Yahweh, Jehovah)** — the personal covenant name, "I AM THAT I AM" (Exodus 3:14–15), rendered **LORD** in small capitals in the KJV. It speaks of God's eternal, self-existent, ever-present being.
- **Adonai (Lord)** — "master, owner," the One to whom all submission is due.
- **El** — the simple word for "God / Mighty One," used in compounds:
  - **El Shaddai** — "God Almighty" (Genesis 17:1).
  - **El Elyon** — "the most high God" (Genesis 14:18–20).
  - **El Roi** — "thou God seest me" (Genesis 16:13).
  - **El Olam** — "the everlasting God" (Genesis 21:33).

**The Jehovah-compound names** (each revealed at a moment of need)
- **Jehovah-jireh** — "the LORD will provide" (Genesis 22:14).
- **Jehovah-rapha** — "the LORD that healeth thee" (Exodus 15:26).
- **Jehovah-nissi** — "the LORD my banner" (Exodus 17:15).
- **Jehovah-shalom** — "the LORD is peace" (Judges 6:24).
- **Jehovah-raah** — "the LORD is my shepherd" (Psalm 23:1); see [[The Shepherd]].
- **Jehovah-tsidkenu** — "THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS" (Jeremiah 23:6).
- **Jehovah-shammah** — "the LORD is there" (Ezekiel 48:35).
- **The LORD of hosts (Jehovah-Sabaoth)** — commander of heaven's armies (1 Samuel 1:3); see [[Angels]].

**Other great titles**
- **The Holy One of Israel** — a favorite name in [[Isaiah]].
- **The Most High** · **the Almighty** · **the Ancient of Days** (Daniel 7:9).
- **The Rock** (Deuteronomy 32:4) · **the King eternal** (1 Timothy 1:17).
- **Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending** (Revelation 1:8).

## The Triune God
Scripture insists that **God is one** — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD" (Deuteronomy 6:4; see [[The Shema]]) — yet reveals that the one God exists eternally as **Father, Son, and Holy Spirit** (the Trinity).
- Hints appear early: "Let **us** make man in **our** image" (Genesis 1:26).
- The **Son** is God: the eternal [[The Word]] who "was God" and "was made flesh" (John 1:1, 14) — [[Jesus Christ]], "the image of the invisible God" (Colossians 1:15).
- The **Spirit** is God: the [[The Holy Spirit]] who moved over the waters (Genesis 1:2) and indwells believers.
- All three appear together at the baptism of Jesus (Matthew 3:16–17), in the Great Commission "in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost" (Matthew 28:19), and in the apostolic blessing (2 Corinthians 13:14).

## The Attributes of God
- **Eternal / self-existent** — "from everlasting to everlasting, thou art God" (Psalm 90:2); He "inhabiteth eternity" (Isaiah 57:15).
- **Almighty / omnipotent** — "there is nothing too hard for thee" (Jeremiah 32:17); see [[The Name of God]].
- **Omniscient** — all things "are naked and opened unto the eyes of him" (Hebrews 4:13); He declares "the end from the beginning" (Isaiah 46:10).
- **Omnipresent** — "Whither shall I go from thy spirit?... thou art there" (Psalm 139:7–10).
- **Sovereign** — "he hath done whatsoever he hath pleased" (Psalm 115:3); see [[The Kingdom of God]].
- **Holy** — "Holy, holy, holy, is the LORD of hosts" (Isaiah 6:3); "Ye shall be holy; for I... am holy" (Leviticus 19:2).
- **Righteous and just** — "all his ways are judgment... just and right is he" (Deuteronomy 32:4); see [[Judgment]].
- **Immutable / unchanging** — "I am the LORD, I change not" (Malachi 3:6); "no variableness, neither shadow of turning" (James 1:17).
- **Love** — "God is love" (1 John 4:8); "God so loved the world" (John 3:16).
- **Merciful and gracious** — His own self-description: "merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth" (Exodus 34:6–7); see [[Repentance]].
- **Faithful** — "great is thy faithfulness" (Lamentations 3:22–23); see [[Covenant Faithfulness]].
- **Good** — "The LORD is good, a strong hold in the day of trouble" (Nahum 1:7).
- **Wise** — "O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God!" (Romans 11:33); see [[Wisdom and Folly]].
- **Truth** — "God is not a man, that he should lie" (Numbers 23:19).
- **Jealous** — "the LORD, whose name is Jealous, is a jealous God" (Exodus 34:14).
- **Wrathful against sin** — "the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness" (Romans 1:18); see [[The Day of the LORD]].
- **Spirit and invisible** — "God is a Spirit" (John 4:24); "whom no man hath seen, nor can see" (1 Timothy 6:16).

## God's Acts in Redemptive History
- **Creator of all** — "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth" (Genesis 1:1); he creates by His [[The Word]]. See [[Creation]].
- **Judge of the world** — He judges human sin in [[The Flood]] and scatters the nations at the [[Tower of Babel]]; final reckoning belongs to Him (see [[Judgment]], [[The Day of the LORD]]).
- **Covenant-maker** — He binds Himself by promise: see [[The Covenant]], the [[Abrahamic Covenant]] with [[Abraham]], the Sinai covenant ([[Giving of the Law]], [[The Decalogue]]), and the [[The Davidic Covenant]] with [[David]].
- **Self-revealer and deliverer** — He names Himself at the [[Burning Bush]] and saves Israel in [[The Exodus]] and the [[Red Sea Crossing]].
- **Lawgiver** — at [[Mount Sinai]] He gives His holy law and orders the worship of His people.
- **Indweller** — He comes to dwell among His people in tabernacle and [[The Temple]], His glory filling the holy place.
- **Speaker through the prophets** — He sends His word by [[Isaiah (Prophet)|Isaiah]], [[Jeremiah (Prophet)|Jeremiah]], [[Ezekiel (Prophet)|Ezekiel]], [[Elijah]], and others; see [[Prophecy and Fulfillment]].
- **Redeemer in the Son** — He enters His own world in [[Jesus Christ]], providing atonement (see [[Sacrifice and Atonement]], [[The Blood]]) and raising Him from the dead (see [[The Resurrection]]).
- **King and consummator** — He reigns ([[The Kingdom of God]]) and will make all things new ([[The New Creation]]), dwelling with His people forever.

## God Made Known in the Son and the Spirit
- **[[Jesus Christ]]** — "the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person" (Hebrews 1:3); to see the Son is to see the Father (John 14:9). The full and final revelation of who God is.
- **[[The Holy Spirit]]** — God present and active in the world and in His people, applying the work of the Son.

## Worship and Response
The right response to God is wholehearted love, reverent fear, trust, and obedience.
- **Love Him supremely** — "thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart" (Deuteronomy 6:5); see [[The Shema]].
- **Fear Him** — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom" (Proverbs 9:10); see [[Wisdom and Folly]].
- **Trust Him** — respond in [[Faith]] and turn from sin in [[Repentance]].
- **Call upon Him** — see [[Prayer]].
- **Approach Him rightly** — through atoning sacrifice, fulfilled in [[Jesus Christ]]; see [[Sacrifice and Atonement]].

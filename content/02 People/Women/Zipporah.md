# Zipporah

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Wife of [[Moses]], Midianite
- **Family:** Daughter of [[Jethro]], wife of Moses, mother of Gershom and Eliezer
- **Key Books:** [[Exodus]]

## Biography
Zipporah was the daughter of [[Jethro]], a Midianite priest. [[Moses]] married her after fleeing Egypt and spending years as a shepherd in Midian. She bore two sons: Gershom ("I am a stranger here") and Eliezer ("My father's God was my help").

Zipporah's most dramatic moment came on the journey back to Egypt. At a lodging place, God confronted Moses and was about to kill him — likely because he had not circumcised his son. Zipporah quickly performed circumcision on their son, touching Moses' feet with the blood and saying "Surely thou art a bridegroom of blood to me." The attack was averted.

This incident highlights the seriousness of covenant obedience and Zipporah's faithfulness in a critical moment.

## Key Events
- Married Moses in Midian
- Circumcised their son — saved Moses' life
- Returned to Egypt with Moses

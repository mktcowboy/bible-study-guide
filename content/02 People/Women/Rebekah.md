# Rebekah

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Wife of [[Isaac]], mother of [[Jacob]] and [[Esau]], matriarch
- **Family:** Daughter of Bethuel, granddaughter of Nahor (Abraham's brother), wife of Isaac
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Rebekah was chosen as Isaac's wife through divine providence. Abraham's servant prayed at a well, and Rebekah was the first girl to offer water both to the servant and his camels — a sign that God had answered the prayer. She agreed to leave her family immediately, asking only "how long shall I tarry with you?"

When pregnant with twins, God revealed that two nations were in her womb. The twins struggled in her belly — Esau (red, hairy) born first, then Jacob grasping his heel. Rebekah received a prophecy that the older would serve the younger.

When Isaac, old and blind, planned to bless Esau, Rebekah helped Jacob deceive his father, taking Esau's blessing. This caused a family rift — Isaac sent Jacob away to find a wife, and Jacob fled from Esau's wrath.

## Key Events
- Chosen at the well — divine selection for Isaac
- Twin pregnancy — prophecy of two nations
- Jacob's deception — helped Jacob receive Esau's blessing
- Sent Jacob to Laban — arranged marriage with her brother's sons

# Rachel

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Beloved wife of [[Jacob]], mother of [[Joseph]] and [[Benjamin]], matriarch
- **Family:** Daughter of Laban, wife of Jacob, sister of [[Leah]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Rachel was the younger daughter of Laban and the love of Jacob's life. When Jacob saw her at the well, he wept and agreed to work 7 years for the right to marry her — years that "seemed unto him but a few days for the love he had to her."

But Laban deceived Jacob, giving him the older daughter [[Leah]] instead. Jacob worked another 7 years for Rachel. Though Rachel initially bore no children, she eventually gave birth to [[Joseph]] and later [[Benjamin]], who died during childbirth.

Rachel was jealous of Leah her whole life, offering her maid Bilhah to Jacob. She stole her father's household gods when fleeing. She begged Jacob "Give me children, or I die." Rachel died giving birth to Benjamin on the road to [[Ephrathah (Bethlehem)]], buried on the way. Her tomb became a landmark mentioned centuries later.

## Key Events
- Met Jacob at the well — Jacob wept with joy
- Deception at wedding — Laban gave Leah first
- Bare [[Joseph]] and [[Benjamin]]
- Stole Laban's household gods
- Died in childbirth — buried near Bethlehem

- [[Jacob]] — Husband, beloved

- [[Benjamin]] — Second son, born at her death
- Bilhah — Maid, mother of Dan and Naphtali

# Leah

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** First wife of [[Jacob]], mother of 6 of the 12 tribes
- **Family:** Daughter of Laban, wife of Jacob, older sister of [[Rachel]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Leah was the older daughter of Laban, described as "tender-eyed." When Jacob came to marry Rachel, Laban gave Leah to Jacob instead, covering her with a veil at night. Though Jacob loved Rachel, God "opened Leah's womb" and she bore six sons: Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, and Zebulun, plus a daughter Dinah.

Leah was unloved by Jacob and bitterly jealous of Rachel. Each time she bore a son, she gave him a name reflecting her desire for Jacob's love — Reuben ("he has seen my affliction"), Judah ("this time will I praise the LORD"). When she stopped bearing children, she gave her maid Zilpah to Jacob.

Despite being unloved, Leah's line produced the greatest gifts: Levi (priesthood), Judah (kingdom and Messiah), and through Judah, the lineage of Jesus Christ.

## Key Events

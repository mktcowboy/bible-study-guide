# Eve

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** First woman, mother of all living
- **Family:** Wife of [[Adam]], mother of [[Cain]], [[Abel]], [[Seth]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Eve was the first woman, created by God from a rib taken from [[Adam]] while he slept (Genesis 2:21-22). God brought her to Adam, who called her "Woman" because she was taken out of Man. Her name "Eve" (Hebrew: Chavah, meaning "living" or "life") was given after the Fall, meaning "mother of all living."

She was tempted by the serpent in the Garden of [[Eden]] and ate the forbidden fruit, then gave some to Adam. Despite the judgment that followed, God promised a Redeemer who would bruise the serpent's head — the first gospel promise (proto-evangelium, Genesis 3:15).

## Key Events
- [[Creation]] — Formed from Adam's rib
- [[Fall of Man]] — Tempted by serpent, ate forbidden fruit
- Birth of [[Cain]] — "I have gotten a man with the help of the LORD"
- Birth of [[Abel]] and [[Seth]]

## Relationships
- [[Adam]] — Husband, "bone of my bones and flesh of my flesh"
- [[Cain]] — Firstborn son
- [[Abel]] — Second son, murdered by Cain
- [[Seth]] — Third son, through whom the covenant line continued

- "And the rib, which the LORD God had taken from man, made he a woman, and brought her unto the man." — Genesis 2:22

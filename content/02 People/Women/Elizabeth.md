# Elizabeth

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Role:** Wife of Zacharias the priest; mother of John the Baptist; kinswoman of Mary; filled with the Holy Spirit at Mary's greeting
- **Family:** Daughter of Aaron's priestly line; wife of Zacharias; mother of [[John the Baptist]]; relative of [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]]
- **Key Books:** [[Luke]]

## Biography
Elizabeth was the wife of Zacharias, a priest of the division of Abijah, and she herself was "from the daughters of Aaron" — meaning she came from the priestly tribe. Luke describes both Zacharias and Elizabeth as "righteous before God, walking blamelessly in all the commandments and statutes of the Lord" (Luke 1:6). This is among the most positive characterizations of any couple in the New Testament. Yet despite their righteousness, Elizabeth was barren and both were "advanced in years" — their childlessness was a source of social reproach (Luke 1:25) in a culture where barrenness was often interpreted as divine disfavor.

The angel [[Gabriel]]'s announcement to Zacharias in the temple that Elizabeth would bear a son set the entire birth narrative of Luke 1 in motion. When Zacharias doubted and was struck mute, the promise still stood — and Elizabeth conceived. Her response was one of gratitude and private devotion: she hid herself for five months, saying "Thus the Lord has done for me in the days when he looked on me, to take away my reproach among people" (Luke 1:25).

The visit of [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]] to Elizabeth in the sixth month of Elizabeth's pregnancy is one of the most Spirit-charged moments in the Gospel. When Mary arrived and greeted Elizabeth, the infant leaped in Elizabeth's womb, and Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit. Her Spirit-inspired words to Mary constitute the first New Testament beatitude: "Blessed are you among women, and blessed is the fruit of your womb! And why is this granted to me that the mother of my Lord should come to me?" (Luke 1:42–43). She also beatified Mary's faith: "Blessed is she who believed that there would be a fulfillment of what was spoken to her from the Lord" (Luke 1:45).

Elizabeth's role as the one who confirms and blesses [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]]'s experience — before the birth of either child — establishes her as a prophetic figure. She "heard the greeting of Mary" and immediately knew, by the Spirit, that the child Mary carried was "my Lord." This recognition, spoken to an unmarried teenager who had not yet said a word about her own situation, gives Elizabeth the distinction of being the first person in Luke's Gospel to recognize the identity of the unborn Christ.

## Key Events
- Both she and Zacharias described as righteous and blameless (Luke 1:6)
- Zacharias receives the angelic announcement of her conception (Luke 1:8–20)
- Conceives in her old age; gives thanks and hides for five months (Luke 1:24–25)
- Visited by [[Mary (Mother of Jesus)]]; filled with the Holy Spirit (Luke 1:39–45)
- Her baby leaps at Mary's greeting (Luke 1:41)
- Speaks the first beatitude of the New Testament over Mary (Luke 1:42–45)
- Confirms the name "John" for her son at his circumcision (Luke 1:60)
- [[John the Baptist]] born; Zacharias regains his speech and prophesies (Luke 1:57–79)

## Notes
- Elizabeth's verbal recognition of the unborn Jesus as "my Lord" makes her the first person in Luke's Gospel to confess Christ's identity — a distinction her son [[John the Baptist]] would later fulfill in adult public ministry
- Her Spirit-inspired speech to Mary parallels the pattern of Old Testament inspired utterance (she "was filled with the Holy Spirit" before speaking), signaling the dawn of a new prophetic era
- The story of barren Elizabeth conceiving in old age deliberately echoes [[Sarah]], [[Rebekah]], [[Hannah]], and other barren-then-fruitful women of the Old Testament, framing John's birth within the great pattern of miraculous divine provision

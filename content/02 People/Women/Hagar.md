# Hagar

## Overview
Hagar was Sarah's Egyptian servant and the mother of Ishmael by [[Abraham]].

## Biblical Significance
In Genesis, Hagar's story reveals both human conflict in the covenant household and God's care for the afflicted. In [[Galatians]], Paul uses Hagar and Sarah allegorically to contrast slavery and promise.

## Key People Associated
- [[Abraham]]
- [[Sarah]]
- Ishmael

## Notes
Hagar is seen by God in the wilderness and becomes a major figure in Paul's covenant argument.

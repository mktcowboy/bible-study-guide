# Sarah (Sarai)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Wife of [[Abraham]], mother of [[Isaac]], matriarch of Israel
- **Family:** Wife of Abraham, daughter of Terah, sister of [[Haran]]
- **Key Books:** [[Genesis]]

## Biography
Born Sarai, she was both the daughter and wife of Abraham (Terah's children). Barren from youth, she gave her maid Hagar to Abraham, resulting in Ishmael. At age 90, God fulfilled His promise and she bore Isaac — so miraculous that she laughed when she heard the news (Genesis 18:12).

God changed her name from Sarai ("my princess") to Sarah ("princess"). Despite moments of doubt and impatience, Sarah demonstrated faith. She laughed again at Isaac's birth, saying "God has made laughter for me." She requested that Hagar and Ishmael be sent away, and later arranged for Isaac's marriage to [[Rebekah]].

Sarah died at age 127 in Kiriath-arba (Hebron). Abraham bought the cave of Machpelah as her burial place — the first property owned by Israelites in Canaan.

# Gomer

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Role:** Hosea's wife, living sign of Israel's unfaithfulness and God's covenant love
- **Family:** Daughter of Diblaim; wife of [[Hosea]]
- **Key Books:** [[Hosea]]

## Biography
Gomer is the woman Hosea is commanded to marry as a prophetic sign-act. Her relationship with Hosea becomes an embodied parable of Israel's spiritual adultery and the LORD's wounded yet pursuing covenant love.

Hosea 3 portrays Hosea buying her back and calling her to remain with him, mirroring God's promise to woo and restore his unfaithful people.

## Key Events
- Marriage to Gomer commanded
- Three children named
- Buying back Gomer

## Relationships
- [[Hosea (Prophet)|Hosea]] - Husband and prophet
- Jezreel, Lo-Ruhamah, Lo-Ammi - Children whose names bear prophetic meaning

## Notes
Gomer's story is painful because it refuses to leave covenant unfaithfulness abstract; Hosea must feel the metaphor he preaches.

# Mount Nebo

## Overview
- **Type:** Geographic Feature / Mountain
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Jabal an-Nuweybiya, Jordan, overlooking the Jordan Valley and Jericho

## Biblical Significance
Mount Nebo is where [[Moses]] climbed to view the Promised Land one final time before his death. From this vantage point, God showed Moses all of Canaan — from [[Gilgal]] to Dan, all of Ephraim, all of Judah, the south, and the western sea (Deuteronomy 34:1–4). Though Moses could see the land, God had previously told him he would not enter it (Deuteronomy 3:27). Moses died there at the age of 120, "his eye was not dim, nor his natural force abated" (Deuteronomy 34:7). God Himself buried him in a valley, and "no man knoweth of his sepulchre unto this day" (Deuteronomy 34:6). Mount Nebo symbolizes both divine faithfulness (the promise kept for the people) and the cost of disobedience (Moses barred from entering).

## Notes
Mount Nebo is a place of both blessing and limitation — the view without the entry. It reminds readers that God's promises are real, even when personal failure creates distance.

# Mount of Olives

## Overview
- **Type:** Ridge / ascent east of Jerusalem
- **Testament:** Both
- **Modern Location:** East of Jerusalem

## Biblical Significance
In [[2 Samuel]] the ascent of Olivet is the road of David's weeping flight from [[Jerusalem]] during [[Absalom's Rebellion]]. The place later becomes significant again in the life of Christ.

## Key Events
- [[Absalom's Rebellion]] - David ascends the mount weeping and barefoot

## Key People Associated
- [[David]] - Flees in sorrow
- [[Absalom]] - The rebellion forcing the flight
- [[Hushai]] - Meets David in the crisis

## Important Verses
> "And David went up by the ascent of mount Olivet, and wept as he went up..." - 2 Samuel 15:30

## Cross-References
- [[2 Samuel]]
- [[Jerusalem]]

## Notes
The Mount of Olives in 2 Samuel is a hill of grief, humility, and dependence rather than triumph.

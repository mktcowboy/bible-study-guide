# Mount Sinai

## Overview
- **Type:** Mountain
- **Testament:** Old
- **Also Known As:** Horeb

## Biblical Significance
Mount Sinai is the mountain where God meets [[Moses]], delivers covenant law to Israel, and formally shapes the redeemed people into a covenant nation. In [[1 Kings]] the same mountain, called Horeb, becomes the place where [[Elijah]] is stripped of spectacle and hears the LORD in a still small voice.

## Notes
- Mount Sinai is one of the defining covenant locations in Scripture. Here Israel is formally constituted as the LORD's people after redemption from Egypt.
- The mountain combines revelation and danger: thunder, fire, cloud, trumpet, and boundaries all underline the holiness of God and the seriousness of approaching Him.
- Sinai also exposes the tension in the human heart. Even while the covenant is being given, the people turn to the [[Golden Calf]], showing the need for a deeper work of grace.
- Later biblical reflection, especially in Hebrews, uses Sinai to contrast the old covenant's fearsome mediation with the better access secured through Christ.

# Uz

## Overview
- **Type:** Region
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Uncertain; often associated with Edom, Aram, or north Arabia

## Biblical Significance
Uz is the land where [[Job]] lived. Its uncertain location contributes to the book's universal feel: Job is not framed through Israel's national institutions but as a righteous sufferer before God.

## Key Events
- Job's testing and restoration

## Key People Associated
- [[Job]]
- [[Eliphaz the Temanite|Eliphaz]]

## Important Verses
- Job 1:1
- Lamentations 4:21

## Cross-References
- [[Job]]
- [[Edom]]

## Notes
The setting outside explicit Israelite institutions helps Job address suffering as a human and theological question before it is a national one.

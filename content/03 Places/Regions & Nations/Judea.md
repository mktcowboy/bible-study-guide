# Judea

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both (historical Judah in OT; Roman province Judaea in NT)
- **Location:** Southern region of the land of Israel; includes Jerusalem, Bethlehem, Hebron, the Dead Sea, the Wilderness of Judea
- **Key Passages:** Matthew 2:1; Luke 1:5; John 3:22; Acts 1:8
- **Associated Book:** All four Gospels, Acts

## Summary
Judea (also "Judaea") is both the ancient tribal territory of Judah and the Roman province established after the death of Herod the Great (4 BC). Under Roman administration, Judea was governed first by procurators (including Pontius Pilate, AD 26–36) and later by direct Roman rule. Jerusalem, the religious and political capital, lay within Judea.

In the NT, Judea is one of the three regions of the promised land alongside [[Galilee]] and [[Samaria]] — the Acts 1:8 commission moves outward from Jerusalem to Judea, Samaria, and the ends of the earth. Much of Jesus's Passion narrative unfolds in Judea: the Triumphal Entry, the cleansing of the Temple, the Last Supper, Gethsemane, and the crucifixion at [[Golgotha]] all occur within or near Jerusalem in Judea.

The Wilderness of Judea — the arid region east of Jerusalem toward the Dead Sea — is where [[John the Baptist]] preached, where Jesus was tempted, and where communities like Qumran (likely the Essenes) awaited the coming of the LORD.

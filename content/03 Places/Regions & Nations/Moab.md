# Moab

## Overview
- **Type:** Nation / region east of the Dead Sea
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** In the region of modern Jordan

## Biblical Significance
[[Moab]] is both a recurring enemy-neighbor of Israel and the homeland of [[Ruth]]. In Ruth, Moab becomes the place of exile, grief, and the unlikely starting point for a line that leads to [[David]] and ultimately Christ.

## Key Events
- [[Deaths in Moab]] - Naomi loses her husband and sons there
- [[Ruth's Vow to Naomi]] - Ruth leaves Moab for the God of Israel

## Key People Associated
- [[Ruth]] - Native of Moab
- [[Naomi]] - Sojourned there in famine
- [[Elimelech]] - Led his family there

## Important Verses
> "And they came into the country of Moab, and continued there." - Ruth 1:2

## Cross-References
- [[Ruth]]
- [[Judges]]
- Deuteronomy 23:3-6

## Notes
Moab is a place of famine-flight and funeral sorrow, yet God turns even this outsider setting into part of His redemptive plan.

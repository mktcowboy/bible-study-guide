# Egypt

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Type:** Nation / Land
- **Biblical Name:** Mizraim

## Biblical Significance
Egypt is the land where Israel was enslaved and from which God delivered them through the Exodus. It becomes one of the Bible's strongest historical memories of bondage, oppression, false security, and the saving power of God. At the same time, Egypt also appears as a temporary refuge in the stories of Joseph and the child Jesus.

## Notes
- Egypt functions both as a historical nation and as a theological memory. Israel is repeatedly told to remember the house of bondage so that obedience, gratitude, and compassion will be shaped by redemption.
- The prophets can treat Egypt as a literal political power, but biblical theology also uses it as a recurring image of oppressive security apart from trusting God.
- The temporary flight of Jesus into Egypt gives the place a surprising role in redemptive history: the place once associated with slavery becomes part of the Messiah's preservation.

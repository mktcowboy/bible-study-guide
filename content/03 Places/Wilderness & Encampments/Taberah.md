# Taberah

## Overview
- **Type:** Wilderness location
- **Testament:** Old
- **Primary Book:** [[Numbers]]

## Biblical Significance
Taberah was named after the Lord's fire burned among the complaining Israelites. The name means "burning" and marks an early rebellion after Israel left Sinai.

## Key Events
- [[Complaining at Taberah]] — The people complained and were judged by fire

## Key People Associated
- [[Moses]] — Interceded when the people cried out

## Cross-References
- [[Numbers]]
- [[Camp of Israel]]

## Notes
Taberah begins the wilderness pattern of grumbling, judgment, and intercession.

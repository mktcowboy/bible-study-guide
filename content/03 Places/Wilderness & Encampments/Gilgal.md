# Gilgal

## Overview
- **Type:** Camp / Sacred Site
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Near the Jordan Valley, east of Jericho

## Biblical Significance
Gilgal is Israel's first major camp in Canaan and the site of covenant renewal after the [[Crossing the Jordan]]. In [[1 Samuel]] it later becomes a testing ground where [[Saul]]'s impatience and disobedience are exposed.

## Key Events
- [[Crossing the Jordan]] - Memorial stones set up
- [[Renewal at Gilgal]] - Circumcision and Passover observed
- [[Gibeonite Deception]] - Treaty made from Israel's camp
- [[Saul Anointed]] - Kingdom renewal celebrated there
- [[Saul Spares Agag]] - Saul is rejected after disobedience there

## Key People Associated
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] - Leads Israel from Gilgal
- [[Commander of the LORD's Army]] - Appears near Jericho after Gilgal renewal
- [[Saul]] - Fails a major test there
- [[Samuel]] - Rebukes Saul there

## Important Verses
> "This day have I rolled away the reproach of Egypt from off you." - Joshua 5:9

## Notes
Gilgal's name is tied to the rolling away of Egypt's reproach. In [[1 Samuel]] it also becomes a place where the danger of hollow ritual is exposed.

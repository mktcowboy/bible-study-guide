# Wilderness of Judea
## Overview
- **Type:** Region
- **Testament:** Both
- **Modern Location:** The arid desert east of the Judean hills, descending to the Dead Sea, in Israel and the West Bank
## Biblical Significance
The Wilderness of Judea is the rugged, sun-scorched desert that falls away from the central highlands of Judah eastward toward the Dead Sea. Its barren ravines and caves made it a refuge for the desperate and a school for the soul. There David fled and hid from Saul, and from those dry and thirsty places he cried out, "O God, thou art my God; early will I seek thee: my soul thirsteth for thee, my flesh longeth for thee in a dry and thirsty land, where no water is" (Psalm 63, title and verse 1). Centuries later this same wilderness became the stage for the forerunner of the Messiah: "In those days came John the Baptist, preaching in the wilderness of Judaea, and saying, Repent ye: for the kingdom of heaven is at hand" (Matthew 3:1-2). John grew strong in spirit and dwelt in the deserts until the day of his showing unto Israel (Luke 1:80). The wilderness thus links David's longing and John's preaching, a place of testing, repentance, and dependence on God.
## Key Events
- Preaching of John the Baptist, calling Israel to repentance (Matthew 3:1-2)
- David's refuge from Saul, the setting of Psalm 63

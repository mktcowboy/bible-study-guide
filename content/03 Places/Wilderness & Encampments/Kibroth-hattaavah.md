# Kibroth-hattaavah

## Overview
- **Type:** Wilderness location
- **Testament:** Old
- **Primary Book:** [[Numbers]]

## Biblical Significance
Kibroth-hattaavah means "graves of craving." Israel named the place after the Lord judged the people who craved meat and despised the manna.

## Key Events
- [[Complaining at Taberah]] — The same wilderness sequence includes the craving for quail and the plague

## Key People Associated
- [[Moses]] — Burdened by the people's complaints

## Notes
The place name preserves the moral memory of disordered desire in the wilderness.

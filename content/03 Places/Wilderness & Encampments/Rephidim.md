# Rephidim

## Overview
- **Type:** Wilderness campsite
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Unknown, between the Red Sea and Sinai

## Biblical Significance
Rephidim ("rest") was where two critical events occurred during Israel's wilderness journey. First, the people complained of having no water, and God instructed Moses to strike a rock at Horeb, bringing forth water. Moses named it Massah ("testing") and Meribah ("quarreling").

Second, Amalek attacked Israel at Rephidim. [[Moses]] commanded [[Joshua]] to lead battle while he stood on a hill with the rod of God. When Moses held his hands up, Israel prevailed; when he lowered them, Amalek gained. [[Aaron]] and [[Hur]] held up Moses' hands until victory.

## Key Events
- Water from the rock — God provides
- Battle with Amalek — First military victory

## Key People Associated
- [[Moses]] — Struck rock, held up hands
- [[Joshua]] — Led battle against Amalek
- [[Aaron]] & Hur — Held up Moses' arms

# En-mizpah
## Overview
- **Type:** Region / Town
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** The locale of Mizpah in Benjamin, north of Jerusalem (likely Tell en-Nasbeh)
## Biblical Significance
"En-mizpah" denotes the spring or locale of Mizpah, whose name means "watchtower," a height in the territory of Benjamin that served Israel for centuries as a place of assembly, prayer, and judgment. There all Israel gathered to take counsel and execute justice after the outrage at Gibeah (Judges 20:1; 21:1). There Samuel called the nation to repentance, drew water and poured it out before the Lord, and prayed as the Philistines were routed, after which he set up the stone Ebenezer (1 Samuel 7:5-12). Mizpah was also where Saul was presented to Israel as king (1 Samuel 10:17). After Jerusalem's fall, the Babylonians made Mizpah the administrative seat of Gedaliah, whom they appointed governor over the poor of the land (2 Kings 25:23); it was there that Gedaliah was treacherously murdered by Ishmael (Jeremiah 40-41). Mizpah thus stands as a place of gathering before God, of righteous judgment, and of the troubled remnant after the exile.

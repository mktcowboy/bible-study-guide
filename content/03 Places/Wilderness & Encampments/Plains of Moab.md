# Plains of Moab

## Overview
- **Type:** Region
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** East bank of the Jordan River, opposite Jericho, in modern Jordan

## Biblical Significance
The Plains of Moab were where Israel camped for the final year before entering the Promised Land. It was here that [[Moses]] delivered his three great farewell sermons (the substance of [[Deuteronomy]]), renewed the covenant with the new generation, and pronounced the blessings and curses of the covenant. The twelve stones set up at Gilgal (Deuteronomy 27) were prepared from this location. This was also where the tribes of Reuben, Gad, and half of Manasseh were sent back across the Jordan to their inheritance (Deuteronomy 3).

## Notes
The Plains of Moab represent a threshold — between wilderness wandering and promised inheritance, between Moses' leadership and Joshua's, between the old generation and the new.

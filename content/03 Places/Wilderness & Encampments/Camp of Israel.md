# Camp of Israel

## Overview
- **Type:** Region / Encampment
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Various locations throughout the wilderness

## Biblical Significance
The Camp of Israel was the organized military and religious encampment of the twelve tribes during the wilderness wanderings. The tabernacle stood at the center, surrounded by the tribe of Levi. The twelve tribes were arranged in four groups of three, each group under a colored standard: East (Judah, Issachar, Zebulun), South (Reuben, Simeon, Gad), West (Ephraim, Manasseh, Benjamin), and North (Dan, Asher, Naphtali) (Numbers 2). The camp of the Gershonites and Merarites (who carried the tabernacle furniture) was on the south side. This arrangement symbolized order, unity, and God at the center of His people. The camp moved as a unified body, following the cloud of God's presence.

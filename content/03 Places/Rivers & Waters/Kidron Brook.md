# Kidron Brook

## Overview
- **Type:** Valley / brook crossing
- **Testament:** Both
- **Modern Location:** East of Jerusalem

## Biblical Significance
The Kidron marks a threshold in [[Absalom's Rebellion]]. David and those loyal to him cross it as they leave [[Jerusalem]], symbolizing exile, vulnerability, and trust in God.

## Key Events
- [[Absalom's Rebellion]] - David and the people pass over Kidron in flight

## Key People Associated
- [[David]]
- [[Ittai]]
- [[Zadok]]

## Important Verses
> "And all the country wept with a loud voice, and all the people passed over: the king also himself passed over the brook Kidron..." - 2 Samuel 15:23

## Notes
Kidron is one of the sorrowful crossings of Scripture, where royal humiliation becomes visible to all.

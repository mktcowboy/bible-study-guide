# Jordan River

## Overview
- **Type:** River
- **Testament:** Both
- **Modern Location:** Runs from the region of Galilee south to the Dead Sea

## Biblical Significance
The Jordan is a boundary-crossing place in Scripture: Israel crosses into Canaan, [[Elijah]] and [[Elisha]] cross it, [[Naaman]] is healed in it, and [[Jesus Christ]] is baptized there.

## Key Events
- [[Crossing the Jordan]] — Israel enters the land on dry ground
- [[Eastern Tribes Return]] — Boundary at the heart of the altar controversy
- [[Elijah Taken to Heaven]] — Elijah and Elisha cross before the ascent
- [[Naaman Healed]] — Naaman is cleansed here

## Key People Associated
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] — Leads Israel across
- [[Elijah]] — Parts the waters with his mantle
- [[Elisha]] — Returns across the river
- [[Naaman]] — Healed after washing
- [[John the Baptist]] — Baptizes in the Jordan in the New Testament
- [[Jesus Christ]] — Baptized in the Jordan

## Important Verses
> "The waters which came down from above stood and rose up upon an heap..." — Joshua 3:16

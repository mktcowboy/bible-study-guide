# Jacob's Well

## Overview
- **Testament:** Both (historical site; NT significance)
- **Location:** Near [[Sychar]] in Samaria, at the foot of Mount Gerizim; modern Bir Ya'qub
- **Key Passages:** Genesis 33:18–19 (land purchase); John 4:5–42
- **Associated Book:** [[John]], [[Genesis]]

## Summary
Jacob's Well is the well dug by the patriarch [[Jacob]] on the plot of land he purchased from the sons of Hamor near Shechem (Genesis 33:18–19), later given to [[Joseph]] (Joshua 24:32). It sits at the junction of valleys beneath Mount Gerizim, the Samaritan holy mountain. The well is among the few sites whose location is undisputed — a real well ~32 meters deep has been excavated at modern Bir Ya'qub since antiquity.

In John 4, Jesus rests at this well at noon when a Samaritan woman comes to draw water. The conversation moves from physical water to the living water Jesus offers — "whoever drinketh of the water that I shall give him shall never thirst." The deep historical resonance is intentional: Jacob's Well is the meeting place of Israel's patriarchal past (the gift of water, the gift of land) and the new covenant that transcends ethnic boundaries. Jesus, the true heir of Jacob, offers what the well of the fathers only prefigured.

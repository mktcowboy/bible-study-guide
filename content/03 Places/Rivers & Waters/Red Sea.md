# Red Sea (Yam Suph / Sea of Reeds)

## Overview
- **Type:** Body of water
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Eastern Mediterranean, between Egypt and Arabian Peninsula

## Biblical Significance
The crossing of the Red Sea (Hebrew: Yam Suph, "Sea of Reeds") is the defining moment of the Exodus — God's most dramatic act of deliverance. After Pharaoh released Israel, he changed his mind and pursued with 600 chariots. Trapped between the sea and the army, Israel feared.

God commanded Moses to stretch out his staff. He divided the waters with a strong east wind all night, creating a dry path. Israel crossed, then God closed the sea on Pharaoh's army, drowning every man. Not one survived.

This event became Israel's defining memory of God's salvation, retold in worship throughout their history. It foreshadows baptism and ultimate deliverance in Christ.

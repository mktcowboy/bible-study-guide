# Most Holy Place

## Overview
- **Type:** Tabernacle inner sanctuary
- **Testament:** Old
- **Primary Books:** [[Exodus]], [[Leviticus]]

## Biblical Significance
The Most Holy Place was the innermost chamber of the [[Tabernacle]], separated by the veil and containing the Ark of the Covenant. The high priest entered it only on the Day of Atonement.

## Key Events
- [[Day of Atonement Established]] — Annual entrance with sacrificial blood
- [[Tabernacle Consecration]] — The sanctuary was prepared for God's presence

## Key People Associated
- [[Aaron]] — First high priest to enter on the appointed day
- [[Moses]] — Received the sanctuary pattern

## Cross-References
- [[Holy Place]]
- [[Tabernacle]]

## Notes
The Most Holy Place dramatizes both God's nearness and the seriousness of approaching his holiness.

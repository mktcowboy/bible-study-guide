# Altar of Burnt Offering

## Overview
- **Type:** Tabernacle furnishing
- **Testament:** Old
- **Primary Books:** [[Exodus]], [[Leviticus]]

## Biblical Significance
The Altar of Burnt Offering stood in the courtyard of the [[Tabernacle]]. It was the place where Israel's sacrifices were offered, making it the first point of approach for worshipers entering the sanctuary space.

## Key Events
- [[Burnt Offering]] — Sacrifices consumed on the altar
- [[Priesthood Ordination]] — Aaron and his sons consecrated with sacrifices
- [[First Service]] — Divine fire consumed the first offerings

## Key People Associated
- [[Aaron]] — First high priest
- [[Moses]] — Received the instructions and oversaw ordination

## Notes
The altar teaches that access to God's dwelling begins with atonement and consecration.

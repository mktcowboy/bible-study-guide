# Garden of Eden

## Overview
- **Type:** Sacred Garden
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Unknown — lost after the Fall

## Biblical Significance
The Garden of Eden was God's perfect creation for humanity's first home. Placed in the east, it contained the tree of life and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil. A river flowed out of Eden, dividing into four heads: Pishon, Gihon, Hiddekel (Tigris), and Euphrates.

God placed [[Adam]] in Eden "to dress it and to keep it," giving him free access to every tree except one. After the [[Fall of Man]], Adam and [[Eve]] were expelled, and God placed cherubim and a flaming sword to guard the way to the tree of life.

Eden represents perfect fellowship with God, lost through sin but ultimately restored in the New Jerusalem (Revelation 22).

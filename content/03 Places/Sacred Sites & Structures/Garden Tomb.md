# Garden Tomb
## Overview
- **Type:** Landmark
- **Testament:** New
- **Modern Location:** Just outside the walls of Jerusalem (the modern "Garden Tomb" near Damascus Gate is one proposed site)
## Biblical Significance
The Garden Tomb was the burial place of Jesus, a new sepulchre set in a garden near the site of the crucifixion. John records, "Now in the place where he was crucified there was a garden; and in the garden a new sepulchre, wherein was never man yet laid. There laid they Jesus therefore because of the Jews' preparation day; for the sepulchre was nigh at hand" (John 19:41-42). The tomb belonged to Joseph of Arimathaea, a rich man and secret disciple, who with Nicodemus took the body, wrapped it in linen with spices, and laid it in his own freshly hewn rock tomb, rolling a great stone to the door (Matthew 27:57-60). That this tomb had never held another body underscored its fitness for the sinless Son of God. From this very garden sepulchre Christ rose on the third day, and there the angels declared to the women, "He is not here: for he is risen" (Matthew 28:6). The Garden Tomb is thus forever joined to the triumph of the resurrection.

# The Gates of Bethlehem

## Overview
- **Type:** City gate / public legal space
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Ancient Bethlehem in Judah

## Biblical Significance
The gate is where [[Boaz]] gathers elders, confronts [[The Closer Kinsman]], and completes the legal redemption that secures Naomi's land and Ruth's future.

## Key Events
- [[The Kinsman-Redeemer]] - Public redemption and witness before the elders

## Key People Associated
- [[Boaz]] - Conducts the legal transaction
- [[Ruth]] - Beneficiary of the redemption
- [[Naomi]] - Her land and family line are redeemed

## Important Verses
> "Then went Boaz up to the gate, and sat him down there..." - Ruth 4:1

## Cross-References
- [[Ruth]]
- [[Bethlehem]]

## Notes
The gate turns private loyalty into public covenant action. Ruth's redemption is not sentimental only; it is legally witnessed and communally confirmed.

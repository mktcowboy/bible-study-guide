# Cenchreae
## Overview
- **Type:** City / Port
- **Testament:** New
- **Modern Location:** Kechries, the eastern harbor of Corinth on the Saronic Gulf, Greece
## Biblical Significance
Cenchreae was the eastern seaport of Corinth, serving as its gateway to the Aegean and the lands to the east. It appears in Acts 18:18, where Paul, having spent a long season in Corinth, "sailed thence into Syria... having shorn his head in Cenchrea: for he had a vow." This act, associated with the discipline of a Nazarite-style vow, shows the apostle's continued reverence for personal devotion even amid Gentile ministry. The port also had a Christian congregation early on, for in Romans 16:1 Paul commends "Phebe our sister, which is a servant of the church which is at Cenchrea," urging the Romans to receive her and assist her, since she had been a helper of many, including Paul himself. Cenchreae thus witnesses both to Paul's personal piety and to the spread of the church into Corinth's harbor district.

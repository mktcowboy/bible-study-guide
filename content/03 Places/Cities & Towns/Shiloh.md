# Shiloh

## Overview
- **Type:** Worship Site / City
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Usually identified with Khirbet Seilun in the hill country of Ephraim

## Biblical Significance
Shiloh becomes the central place where the tabernacle is set up after the conquest. In [[1 Samuel]] it is the setting for [[Hannah's Prayer]], the childhood of [[Samuel]], and the corruption of [[Eli]]'s priestly house.

## Key Events
- [[Division of the Land]] - Tabernacle set up and land allotments continue
- [[Levitical Cities]] - Worship order is spread through Israel
- [[Hannah's Prayer]] - Hannah pours out her heart before the LORD
- [[Corruption of Eli's Sons]] - Priesthood defiled at the tabernacle
- [[Ark Captured]] - Shiloh's spiritual collapse comes into view

## Key People Associated
- [[Joshua (Son of Nun)]] - Oversees allotment at Shiloh
- [[Eleazar]] - Priest involved in distribution
- [[Hannah]] - Prays and dedicates Samuel there
- [[Eli]] - Priest serving at Shiloh
- [[Samuel]] - Raised before the LORD there

## Important Verses
> "And the whole congregation of the children of Israel assembled together at Shiloh, and set up the tabernacle..." - Joshua 18:1

## Cross-References
- [[Tabernacle]]
- [[1 Samuel]]

## Notes
Shiloh becomes especially important in [[1 Samuel]], where the ark and priesthood are centered there. It is both a place of prayerful dedication and a warning that sacred institutions can decay without obedience.

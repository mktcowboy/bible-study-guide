# Beer-sheba
## Overview
- **Type:** City
- **Testament:** Both
- **Modern Location:** Be'er Sheva, in the Negev of southern Israel
## Biblical Significance
Beer-sheba lay at the southern edge of the Promised Land and gave its name to the proverbial expression "from Dan to Beer-sheba," marking the full extent of Israel from north to south (Judges 20:1; 1 Samuel 3:20). Its name means "well of the oath" or "well of seven," recalling the covenant Abraham made with Abimelech, sealed with seven ewe lambs and an oath beside a well (Genesis 21:31). It was a place of patriarchal worship: Abraham planted a grove and called on the everlasting God there (Genesis 21:33), Isaac built an altar and dug a well after the Lord appeared to him (Genesis 26:23-25), and Jacob offered sacrifices there on his way down to Egypt, where God spoke to him in visions of the night (Genesis 46:1-4). The city thus stands as a southern boundary marker and a wellspring of the faith of the fathers.

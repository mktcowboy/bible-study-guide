# Babylon

## Overview
- **Type:** Imperial capital
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Iraq region, associated with ancient Mesopotamia

## Biblical Significance
Babylon becomes the final instrument of judgment in [[2 Kings]]. It receives Judah's exiles, destroys [[Jerusalem]], and frames the book's final glimpse of restrained hope in the release of [[Jehoiachin]]. Across the prophets and writings, Babylon stands for imperial pride, human arrogance, and the painful reality of covenant judgment.

## Notes
- Babylon is one of the Bible's great city-symbols. From [[Babel]] onward, it represents concentrated human ambition that exalts itself against God.
- Historically, Babylon marks the collapse of Judah's kingdom and the loss of land, temple, and monarchy as Israel had known them.
- Yet exile in Babylon does not end God's covenant purposes. In books like [[Daniel]] and [[Ezekiel]], the Lord proves that His rule extends even into foreign courts and distant lands.

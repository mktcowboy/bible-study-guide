# Bethlehem

## Overview
- **Type:** Town
- **Testament:** Both
- **Modern Location:** Bethlehem, south of Jerusalem

## Biblical Significance
Bethlehem appears in Judges and Ruth before becoming the city of David and the birthplace of Jesus. In [[Ruth]] it moves from famine and emptiness to harvest, redemption, and the beginning of the Davidic line. In the prophets and Gospels it becomes a place of quiet but world-changing messianic significance.

## Notes
- Bethlehem ties together kingship and redemption. It is the home of David's family line and therefore the fitting birthplace of David's greater Son.
- The town's importance grows gradually. Scripture first presents it in fragile and ordinary settings before revealing its enduring role in the story of covenant hope.
- The movement from famine to fullness in [[Ruth]] makes Bethlehem a memorable setting for God's providence in ordinary faithfulness.

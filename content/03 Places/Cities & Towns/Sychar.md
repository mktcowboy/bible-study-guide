# Sychar

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Location:** Samaria; near Jacob's Well and Mount Gerizim; likely modern Askar or ancient Shechem
- **Key Passages:** John 4:5–42
- **Associated Book:** [[John]]

## Summary
Sychar is the Samaritan city near which Jesus meets the woman at the well (John 4). The narrative identifies it as "near to the parcel of ground that Jacob gave to his son Joseph" — grounding the encounter in the deep covenant history of the land. Jacob's Well, at which Jesus rests and speaks, was a real well long associated with the patriarch, located at the foot of Mount Gerizim. The woman is surprised that a Jewish man would ask a Samaritan woman for water — the double barrier of ethnic and gender separation.

The conversation at Sychar becomes the occasion for Jesus's fullest pre-Resurrection self-disclosure in John: "I that speak unto thee am he" — explicitly claiming to be the Messiah. The woman becomes one of the most effective evangelists in the Gospels, bringing the whole city out to Jesus: "He told me all things that ever I did." Many Samaritans believe because of her witness, then directly from Jesus's own words during his two-day stay in Sychar.

# Bahurim

## Overview
- **Type:** Village
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Benjaminite area near Jerusalem

## Biblical Significance
Bahurim is tied to divided loyalties in David's kingdom. It appears in the transfer of [[Michal]], in [[Shimei]]'s cursing of [[David]], and in the hidden movements during [[Absalom's Rebellion]].

## Key Events
- [[Civil War]] - Michal is taken from her husband as David asserts his claim
- [[Absalom's Rebellion]] - Shimei curses David there
- [[David's Return]] - Shimei meets David again in fear and petition

## Key People Associated
- [[David]]
- [[Michal]]
- [[Absalom]]

## Important Verses
> "And when king David came to Bahurim, behold, thence came out a man..." - 2 Samuel 16:5

## Cross-References
- [[2 Samuel]]

## Notes
Bahurim is a place where political fracture becomes personal and public.

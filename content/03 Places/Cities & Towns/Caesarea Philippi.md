# Caesarea Philippi

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Location:** Northern Israel, at the foot of Mount Hermon; source of the Jordan River; ~25 miles north of the Sea of Galilee
- **Key Passages:** Matthew 16:13–20; Mark 8:27–30
- **Associated Book:** [[Matthew]], [[Mark]]

## Summary
Caesarea Philippi is the Gentile city in the far north of Israel, built by Philip the Tetrarch near the ancient shrine of Pan (Banias/Paneas), where Peter makes his great confession: "Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God." The location is deliberate — at the gate of the pagan world, with Rome's power visible in the city's name, Jesus asks who people say he is, then who the disciples say he is. The contrast between popular guesses (John the Baptist, Elijah, Jeremiah) and Peter's confession underscores the uniqueness of the revelation.

Jesus's response to Peter — "upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it" — carries geographical resonance: the gates of Hades was a name for the grotto at Banias, an actual cave believed to be an entrance to the underworld. Jesus announces his church's victory over death at the very location associated with death's gate.

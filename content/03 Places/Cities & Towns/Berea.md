# Berea
## Overview
- **Type:** City
- **Testament:** New
- **Modern Location:** Veria, in northern Greece (Macedonia)
## Biblical Significance
Berea was a Macedonian city to which Paul and Silas were sent away by night after opposition arose in Thessalonica during the second missionary journey (Acts 17:10). The Bereans became a model of faithful hearing: Luke records that they "were more noble than those in Thessalonica, in that they received the word with all readiness of mind, and searched the scriptures daily, whether those things were so" (Acts 17:11). Their willingness to test apostolic preaching against Scripture, rather than rejecting it outright, led many of them to believe, including honourable Greek women and men. The peace was broken when Jews from Thessalonica came to stir up the crowds, forcing Paul to depart toward Athens while Silas and Timothy remained behind for a time (Acts 17:13-15). Berea endures as the Bible's enduring picture of diligent, Scripture-searching faith.

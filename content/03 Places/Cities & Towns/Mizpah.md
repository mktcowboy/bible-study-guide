# Mizpah

## Overview
- **Type:** Assembly Site
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** Several biblical Mizpah locations exist; Judges uses it as a gathering place

## Biblical Significance
Mizpah is associated with [[Jephthah]]'s leadership and with Israel's national assembly against Benjamin. In [[1 Samuel]] it becomes the gathering place for repentance, prayer, and victory over the Philistines.

## Key Events
- [[Jephthah's Vow]] - Jephthah returns to Mizpah after battle
- [[War Against Benjamin]] - Israel gathers at Mizpah
- [[Samuel's Judgeship]] - Israel assembles there in repentance
- [[Saul Anointed]] - Saul is publicly identified by lot

## Key People Associated
- [[Jephthah]] - Gileadite judge
- [[Benjamin]] - Tribe judged after Gibeah
- [[Samuel]] - Leads repentance and intercession there
- [[Saul]] - Publicly chosen there

## Important Verses
> "And they gathered together to Mizpeh, and drew water, and poured it out before the LORD..." - 1 Samuel 7:6

## Cross-References
- [[Judges]]
- [[1 Samuel]]

## Notes
Mizpah often functions as a place of public reckoning. In [[1 Samuel]] it becomes both an altar of repentance and a stage for national transition.

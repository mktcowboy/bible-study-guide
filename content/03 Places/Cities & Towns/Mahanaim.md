# Mahanaim

## Overview
- **Type:** Transjordan city / refuge
- **Testament:** Old
- **Modern Location:** East of the Jordan in Gilead

## Biblical Significance
Mahanaim serves first as the base of [[Ishbosheth]] under [[Abner]] and later as a refuge for [[David]] during [[Absalom's Rebellion]].

## Key Events
- [[Civil War]] - Ishbosheth is brought there as rival king
- [[Absalom's Rebellion]] - David regroups there

## Key People Associated
- [[Ishbosheth]] - Rules from Mahanaim
- [[Abner]] - Supports Ishbosheth there
- [[David]] - Finds refuge there

## Important Verses
> "But Abner the son of Ner, captain of Saul's host, took Ishbosheth the son of Saul, and brought him over to Mahanaim." - 2 Samuel 2:8

## Cross-References
- [[2 Samuel]]
- [[Jordan River]]

## Notes
Mahanaim is one of 2 Samuel's reversal cities: it houses Saul's fading cause and later shelters David in his lowest royal hour.

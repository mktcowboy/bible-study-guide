# Leviticus

> "Be ye holy; for I the LORD your God am holy." — Leviticus 19:2 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Author:** [[Moses]]
- **Date Written:** ~1446 BC (year after Exodus, at Sinai)
- **Key Themes:** Holiness, sacrifice, priesthood, atonement, worship, cleanliness, feast days
- **Chapters:** 27

## Summary

[[Leviticus]] is the most misunderstood book in the Bible — and arguably the most Christ-centered. It's God's instruction manual for how a holy God can dwell among sinful people. The answer: through sacrifice, priesthood, and ritual purity. Every regulation points forward to Christ, who is our perfect sacrifice, our great High Priest, and our holiness.

The book flows from the tabernacle outward:
- **Chapters 1–7: Sacrifices** — Five offerings, from the altar
- **Chapters 8–10: Priesthood** — Aaron and sons ordained, first service
- **Chapters 11–15: Clean & Unclean** — Food, disease, bodily discharges
- **Chapters 16–17: Day of Atonement** — The great annual sacrifice
- **Chapters 18–27: Holiness Code** — Daily living as a holy people
- **Chapter 27: Vows & Tithes** — Addendum

## Notes & Reflections

- "The LORD spake unto Moses" appears 33 times — this is God's word, not Moses'
- Every sacrifice requires blood — "without shedding of blood is no remission" (Hebrews 9:22)
- The Day of Atonement is the most important day in the Jewish calendar
- Holiness = "set apart" — not moral perfection but belonging to God
- The clean/unclean laws teach that God is involved in every aspect of life
- Christ fulfills every sacrifice: perfect Lamb, our High Priest, our holiness
- The Holiness Code (18–26) shows that worship and ethics are inseparable
- Leviticus is the center of the Pentateuch — sacrifice at the heart of salvation

# Ruth

- **Chapters:** 4
- **Source Text:** [[Ruth (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[Ruth]] is a brief, beautiful counterpoint to the darkness of [[Judges]]. While Israel was descending into moral chaos, this quiet story of a Moabite widow shows God's faithful love working behind the scenes. Set during the time of the judges, Ruth tells how a foreign widow clings to her Israelite mother-in-law, works faithfully in the fields of a wealthy relative, and becomes the great-grandmother of King [[David]] — and ultimately an ancestor of Christ.

The book is a masterclass in divine providence. Every "coincidence" is orchestrated by God (who is only named four times in the narrative). Ruth, a Moabite — from a nation God commanded Israel not to intermarry with — becomes a kinsman-redeemer story that foreshadows Christ's redemption of all peoples.

## Notes & Reflections

- Ruth is a kinsman-redeemer story that foreshadows Christ: Boaz is wealthy, willing, and able to redeem — just as Christ is
- Ruth is a Moabite — the most unlikely ancestor of Christ. Grace extends to all nations
- God is barely mentioned in the narrative, yet His providence is everywhere — every "coincidence" is divine orchestration
- The book contrasts with Judges: while Israel falls apart, God is quietly building the line of David
- Naomi goes from "full" to "empty" (Mara = bitter) to "full" again — restoration
- Ruth's hesed (loyal love) mirrors God's covenant love
- The genealogy ending with David sets up the monarchy — Ruth is the bridge between judges and kings
- Four women in Christ's lineage (all unusual): Tamar, Rahab, Ruth, Bathsheba — grace for the unexpected

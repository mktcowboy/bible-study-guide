# Ezra

> "Blessed be the LORD God of our fathers, that hath put such a thing as this in the king's heart, to beautify the house of the LORD which is at Jerusalem." — Ezra 7:27 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Author:** [[Ezra]] (first-person narrative in chapters 7–10)
- **Date Written:** ~430 BC
- **Key Themes:** Return from exile, temple rebuilding, divine providence, spiritual renewal, separation from idolatry, Law
- **Chapters:** 10
- **Source Text:** [[Ezra (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[Ezra]] records the first two waves of return from Babylonian exile and the rebuilding of the temple. The book begins with Cyrus's decree (linking directly from 2 Chronicles 36) and traces the restoration of worship in Jerusalem through opposition, encouragement, and completion.

Two movements:
- **Chapters 1–6: First Return & Temple Rebuilding** — Cyrus's decree, Zerubbabel leads 50,000 back, foundation laid, opposition halts work, Haggai and Malachi encourage, temple completed.
- **Chapters 7–10: Ezra's Arrival & Spiritual Renewal** — Seventy years later, Ezra arrives with a second group, discovers intermarriage with idolaters, leads national repentance.

## Notes & Reflections

- Ezra 7:10 is a model for every believer: "set his heart to seek, do, and teach"
- The temple took 22 years from exile to completion — perseverance matters
- Opposition is inevitable — but God's purposes prevail
- The joy and tears at the foundation (old vs. young) shows the pain of exile and hope of restoration
- Ezra's repentance over intermarriage shows that worship requires separation
- The book bridges exile and restoration — God keeps His promises

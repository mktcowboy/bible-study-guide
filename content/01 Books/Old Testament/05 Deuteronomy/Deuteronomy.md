# Deuteronomy

> "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD: And thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thine soul, and with all thine might." — Deuteronomy 6:4-5 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Author:** [[Moses]]
- **Date Written:** ~1406 BC (final month before death, age 120)
- **Key Themes:** Covenant renewal, obedience, love for God, blessings & curses, choice between life and death
- **Chapters:** 34
- **Source Text:** [[Deuteronomy (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[Deuteronomy]] means "second law" — Moses' farewell sermons to the new generation standing at the edge of the Promised Land. It's the most personal, passionate book of the Pentateuch. Moses reviews Israel's history, restates the law, and calls them to choose life. The Shema (6:4-5) becomes the central prayer of Judaism and Christianity.

The structure mirrors a suzerain-vassal treaty:
- **Chapters 1–4: Historical Prologue** — Review of wilderness journey
- **Chapters 5–26: Laws & Stipulations** — Restatement of the covenant
- **Chapters 27–30: Blessings & Curses** — Covenant consequences
- **Chapters 31–34: Moses' Death & Succession** — Transition

## Notes & Reflections

- Deuteronomy 6:4-5 (Shema) is the most quoted OT passage in the NT
- Jesus quotes Deuteronomy during His temptation (Matt. 4)
- "Prophet like unto me" (18:15) — Jesus fulfills this (Acts 3:22)
- The blessings/curses of chapter 28 were fulfilled in AD 70
- "Choose life" (30:19) — the gospel in a nutshell
- Moses dies at 120, "his eye was not dim, nor his natural faculty abated"
- The Song of Moses (ch. 32) is a witness — God anticipates rebellion
- Deuteronomy closes the Pentateuch — law given, now faith required

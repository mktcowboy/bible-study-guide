# Numbers

> "And the LORD said unto Moses, I will not come up into the midst of thee; for thou art a stiffnecked people: lest I consume thee in the way." — Numbers 14:12 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Author:** [[Moses]]
- **Date Written:** ~1446–1406 BC (wilderness period)
- **Key Themes:** Rebellion, judgment, grace, divine provision, wilderness wandering, covenant faithfulness
- **Chapters:** 36
- **Source Text:** [[Numbers (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[Numbers]] traces Israel's 40-year wilderness journey from Sinai to the plains of Moab. The name "Numbers" comes from the two censuses (chapters 1–4 and 26). Between them lies a generation of rebellion, punishment, and wandering. The book shows what happens when God's people refuse to trust Him — and what happens when they finally do.

The structure:
- **Chapters 1–10: Sinai** — Organization, census, departure
- **Chapters 11–25: Wilderness Rebellion** — Complaining, spying, 40 years
- **Chapters 26–36: Moab** — New generation, preparation for Canaan

## Notes & Reflections

- The two censuses bookend a generation — same number, different people
- Israel complained 12+ times in the wilderness — the pattern is rebellion
- The bronze serpent is the clearest OT type of Christ's cross
- Balaam's "Star out of Jacob" is the earliest messianic prophecy after Genesis 3:15
- Numbers shows that God's grace persists despite repeated rebellion
- The red heifer ritual is unique — purification from death contamination
- Korah's rebellion shows that equality isn't the same as equity
- Caleb and Joshua — faith vs. fear, the only two who enter

# Nehemiah

> "Then said I unto them, Ye see the waste, and the fire, and ye would lay the stones thereof, and repair the wall. My God, I pray thee, let now thine ear hear, and thine eyes see, and give success to this work, I, pray thee, now." — Nehemiah 2:17-18 (KJV)

## Summary

[[Nehemiah]] is a masterpiece of leadership and prayer. Nehemiah, cupbearer to the king of Persia, learns that Jerusalem's walls are broken and the people in affliction. He prays, requests permission to return, and leads the reconstruction of the wall in just 52 days — despite intense opposition, mockery, and threats.

The book divides into two parts:
- **Chapters 1–7: Rebuilding the Wall** — Nehemiah's prayer, royal commission, inspection, organization, opposition (Sanballat, Tobiah, Sanballat again), completion.
- **Chapters 8–13: Spiritual Reform** — Ezra reads the Law, feast of tabernacles, covenant renewal, social justice reforms, personal dedication.

## Notes & Reflections

- Nehemiah prays before he acts — 17 prayers in 13 chapters
- Opposition is inevitable — mockery, threats, deception, fatigue tactics
- Nehemiah organizes by families — everyone has a role
- 52 days — focused, determined, prayerful leadership
- Spiritual reform follows physical rebuilding — both matter
- Nehemiah refuses bread of the governor — integrity under pressure
- The book shows that God uses ordinary people in extraordinary ways
- Nehemiah's final reforms show that leadership doesn't end when the project is done

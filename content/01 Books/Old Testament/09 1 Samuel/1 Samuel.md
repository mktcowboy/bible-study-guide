# 1 Samuel

> "But Samuel said, Hath the LORD as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying the voice of the LORD? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams." - 1 Samuel 15:22 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** Old
- **Author:** [[Samuel]], with possible additions by [[Nathan]] and [[Gad]]
- **Date Written:** ~1000 BC
- **Key Themes:** Transition from judges to monarchy, obedience over sacrifice, God looks at the heart, anointing, rebellion, faithfulness
- **Chapters:** 31
- **Source Text:** [[1 Samuel (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[1 Samuel]] bridges the era of [[Judges]] and the establishment of the monarchy. It begins with the birth of [[Samuel]] - the last and greatest judge - and traces Israel's turbulent transition to kingship. The arc moves from divine faithfulness in Samuel's ministry, to human failure in [[Saul]]'s rebellion, to the quiet rise of [[David]].

The book contains three major movements:
- **Chapters 1-7: Samuel's Rise** - [[Hannah's Prayer]], [[Samuel's Calling]], [[Corruption of Eli's Sons]], Philistine pressure, [[Ark Captured]], [[Ark in Philistia]], and [[Samuel's Judgeship]].
- **Chapters 8-12: The Monarchy Established** - [[Demand for a King]], [[Saul Anointed]], Saul's early victory, and Samuel's farewell speech.
- **Chapters 13-31: Saul's Reign and David's Rise** - Saul's disobedience, [[Saul Spares Agag]], [[David Anointed]], [[David & Goliath]], [[David & Jonathan]], [[David's Flight]], [[Saul & the Witch of Endor]], and [[Death of Saul & Jonathan]].

[[1 Samuel]] shows that the deepest problem is not the form of government but the condition of the heart. Israel wanted a king like the nations, while the LORD desired covenant obedience.

## Notes & Reflections

- [[Samuel]] is the last judge and the bridge to the monarchy
- [[Hannah's Prayer]] foreshadows later songs of reversal like Mary's Magnificat in Luke 1
- [[Saul]] represents appearance without enduring obedience
- [[David]] represents the kind of heart God desires, though he will still need grace
- [[1 Samuel]] repeatedly shows that God's purposes advance despite human failure
- [[David's Flight]] proves David's character before it proves his kingship
- [[Saul & the Witch of Endor]] shows how far a leader can fall when he will not repent
- The book ends in grief at [[Mount Gilboa]], leaving [[2 Samuel]] to answer what faithful kingship should become

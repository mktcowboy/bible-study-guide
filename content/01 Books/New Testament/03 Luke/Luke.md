# Luke

> "For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost." — Luke 19:10 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Author:** [[Luke]], physician and companion of [[Paul (Apostle)|Paul]]
- **Date Written:** ~AD 58–62
- **Key Themes:** Jesus as Savior of all, compassion, outcasts, Holy Spirit, prayer, joy
- **Chapters:** 24
- **Source Text:** [[Luke (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[Luke]] is the most detailed, most human Gospel — written by a physician for a Greek audience (Theophilus). Luke emphasizes Jesus' compassion for the poor, women, Samaritans, and outcasts. The Gospel contains the most parables (14 unique to Luke), the most prayer references, and the most Spirit-filled narrative. Jesus is the Savior of all people — Jew, Gentile, rich, poor, clean, unclean. Together with [[Acts]], Luke's two-volume work traces salvation from Bethlehem to Rome.

## Notes & Reflections

- Only Gospel with birth narrative AND ascension account
- 14 parables unique to Luke — most of any Gospel
- Emphasizes prayer — Jesus prays at every major moment
- Women feature prominently more than any other Gospel
- Genealogy traces to Adam (not Abraham) — Jesus is Savior of all humanity
- Luke 15 is the most beloved chapter — three parables of divine joy
- "Theophilus" — "lover of God," may be a real person or all believers
- Together with Acts, traces salvation from Bethlehem to Rome
- Most historical, most detailed — "most orderly account" (1:3)

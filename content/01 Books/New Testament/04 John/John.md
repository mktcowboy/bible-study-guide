# John

> "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life." — John 3:16 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Author:** [[John]] (son of Zebedee, the beloved disciple)
- **Date Written:** ~AD 85–95
- **Key Themes:** Eternity, belief, divine identity, seven "I AM" statements, signs, light vs. darkness
- **Chapters:** 21
- **Source Text:** [[John (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[John]] is the most theological, most spiritual Gospel — written last, it complements the Synoptics rather than repeating them. While Matthew, Mark, and Luke focus on what Jesus did, John focuses on who Jesus IS. The Gospel contains seven "I AM" statements, seven "signs," and the longest recorded sermons. John wrote with a single purpose: "That ye might believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God; and that believing ye might have life through his name" (20:31).

## Notes & Reflections

- Written last (~AD 85–95) — complements, doesn't repeat Synoptics
- Only Gospel with extended theological prologue ("In the beginning")
- 7 "I AM" statements echo Exodus 3:14 — divine identity
- 7 signs point to Jesus' divine nature
- John 17 is the longest prayer in Scripture — Jesus for His people
- No parables — uses extended dialogues and discourses instead
- "Believe" (pisteuō) appears 98 times — more than any other NT book
- The Gospel ends not with ascension but with purpose (20:31) + appendix (ch. 21)
- "The disciple whom Jesus loved" — traditional identification with John

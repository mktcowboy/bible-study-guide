# Matthew

> "For where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them." — Matthew 18:20 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Author:** [[Matthew]] (Levi), tax collector and apostle
- **Date Written:** ~AD 50–60
- **Key Themes:** Kingdom of heaven, fulfillment of prophecy, Jesus as Messiah/King, sermons, discipleship
- **Chapters:** 28
- **Source Text:** [[Matthew (KJV Source)]]

## Summary

[[Matthew]] is the most Jewish of the Gospels — written to prove that Jesus is the promised Messiah. It opens with "The book of the generation of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham" (1:1) — a deliberate echo of Genesis's *toledot* ("generations") formula — and contains more OT quotations than any other Gospel (60+). The signature fulfillment formula, "that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet," appears 14 times. Matthew's primary audience is Jewish readers who need to see that Jesus does not abolish the law but fulfills it (5:17).

The book's most distinctive structural feature is its **five major discourses**, each ending with "And it came to pass, when Jesus had ended these sayings..." — deliberately mirroring the five books of Moses:

1. **Sermon on the Mount** (chs. 5–7) — The ethics of the Kingdom
2. **Mission Discourse** (ch. 10) — Instructions for the Twelve
3. **Parables Discourse** (ch. 13) — Seven parables of the Kingdom
4. **Church Discourse** (ch. 18) — Community life, forgiveness, restoration
5. **Olivet Discourse** (chs. 24–25) — End times, judgment, the Son of Man

Surrounding these discourses is narrative that shows Jesus exercising authority — over demons, disease, death, nature, and ultimately sin — as the divine Son of David who has the right to make these demands. The Gospel culminates in the Great Commission (28:19–20), sending a Jewish story to all nations.

## Notes & Reflections

- Matthew quotes the OT 60+ times — most of any NT book
- Five major sermons mirror the five books of Moses
- "Kingdom of heaven" used 32 times (Matthew's Jewish sensitivity vs. "kingdom of God")
- The Genealogy traces Jesus through David to Abraham — legal right to throne
- Sermon on the Mount (5–7) is Jesus' most comprehensive teaching
- Olivet Discourse (24–25) is the primary end-times teaching
- Matthew 18:20 — Promise of Christ's presence in gathering
- The Gospel ends with a command, not a question — "Go"

# Mark

> "For even the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many." — Mark 10:45 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Author:** [[John Mark]] (companion of [[Peter]], per tradition)
- **Date Written:** ~AD 55–65
- **Key Themes:** Suffering Servant, action, secrecy, discipleship, cost of following
- **Chapters:** 16

## Summary

[[Mark]] is the shortest, fastest, most action-packed Gospel — the "Gospel of the suffering Servant." Written for Roman audiences, Mark emphasizes what Jesus DID rather than what He said. The word "immediately" appears 40+ times. Half the Gospel (chapters 8–16) covers the final week. Jesus is the suffering Son of Man who came "not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many."

## Notes & Reflections

- "Immediately" (euthus) appears 40+ times — urgency, action
- Mark has no birth narrative — begins at baptism, ends at resurrection
- The "Messianic Secret" — Jesus silences demons and healed people
- Half the Gospel covers the final week — the cross is the point
- The earliest ending (16:8) ends with fear — longer ending adds appearances
- Mark emphasizes discipleship failure — they don't understand
- The centurion at the cross is the first to confess (15:39)
- Shortest Gospel, but most direct — no sermons, just action

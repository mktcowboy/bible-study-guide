# 1 John

> "That which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes, which we have looked upon, and our hands have handled, of the Word of life; (For the life was manifested, and we have seen it, and bear witness)." — 1 John 1:1–2 (KJV)

## Overview
- **Testament:** New
- **Author:** [[John]] the Apostle
- **Date Written:** ~AD 85–95, from Ephesus
- **Key Themes:** Fellowship with God, tests of genuine Christianity, God is light/love/life, the incarnation against Gnostic denial, victory through faith
- **Chapters:** 5

## Summary

1 John is not a letter in the conventional Pauline sense — it has no opening address or sender identification, no travel plans, no named recipients. It reads more like a circular homily or theological manifesto, addressed to a community John knows intimately. The occasion: a group of teachers with Gnostic leanings had broken away from the community ("they went out from us" — 2:19), denying the full humanity of Christ (docetism — Christ only "seemed" to have a body) and claiming a superior spiritual knowledge that placed them beyond ordinary ethical standards. Their departure left the remaining believers destabilized.

John's response is both polemical and pastoral. He grounds everything in the **physical, historical reality of the incarnation**: "that which we have heard, seen with our eyes, looked upon, and our hands have handled" (1:1). The Word of life was tangible. Any "Christian" teaching that denies this is the spirit of antichrist (4:2–3).

Against this background, John lays out three interlocking **tests of genuine fellowship**: the **moral test** (walking in light, not darkness; obedience to commandments), the **social test** (love for the brothers), and the **doctrinal test** (confession of Jesus Christ come in the flesh). These tests spiral through the letter rather than appearing sequentially — John revisits each in deepening ways.

**Chapter 1** — God is light; no darkness in him; if we claim fellowship while walking in darkness, we lie; the blood of Jesus cleanses from all sin; confession leads to forgiveness. **Chapter 2** — New/old commandment: love one another; world is passing away; antichrists have gone out. **Chapter 3** — We are children of God; the world doesn't know us; sin is lawlessness; love not in word but in deed and truth. **Chapter 4** — Test the spirits; confess Christ in the flesh; God is love; perfect love casts out fear. **Chapter 5** — Faith overcomes the world; the Spirit and water and blood testify; eternal life in the Son; confidence in prayer; he that has the Son has life.

## Notes & Reflections

- 1 John uses "know" (*ginōskō*) 39 times and "love" (*agapaō*) 46 times — the letter is a sustained meditation on the epistemology of spiritual certainty and love
- The Gnostics claimed superior *gnōsis* (knowledge) — John reclaims "knowing" for the orthodox: genuine knowledge of God is evidenced by obedience, love, and right doctrine
- "Born of God does not sin" (3:9) — the tension with 1:8–10 ("if we say we have no sin, we deceive ourselves") is best resolved by seeing 3:9 as referring to a *pattern* of life (continuous sinning) vs. the *reality* of occasional failure
- "Perfect love casts out fear" (4:18) — not that we achieve perfect love, but that God's love for us, fully received, removes the torment of judgment-fear
- The "Johannine comma" (5:7 KJV: "the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one") is not present in early Greek manuscripts — it is widely recognized as a later Latin insertion

# Prophetic Timeline

## Overview
The prophets spoke into specific covenant crises. This timeline places them near the historical pressures their messages address.

## Eighth Century BC
| Setting | Prophets | Major Themes |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Assyrian pressure on Israel and Judah | [[Amos]], [[Hosea]], [[Isaiah]], [[Micah]], [[Jonah]] | Social injustice, covenant lawsuit, holy remnant, Zion hope, Nineveh's repentance. |
| Northern kingdom nearing collapse | [[Hosea]], [[Amos]] | Idolatry, exploitation, and the coming fall of Samaria. |

## Seventh Century BC
| Setting | Prophets | Major Themes |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Assyria's decline and Judah's danger | [[Nahum]], [[Zephaniah]], [[Habakkuk]], [[Jeremiah]] | Fall of Nineveh, Day of the LORD, Babylon as instrument of judgment, righteous faith. |
| Jerusalem before exile | [[Jeremiah]] | Temple false confidence, covenant breach, coming siege, new covenant promise. |

## Exilic Period
| Setting | Prophets | Major Themes |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Babylonian exile | [[Ezekiel]], [[Daniel]], [[Obadiah]] | Glory departing and returning, judgment on Edom, kingdoms under God's rule. |
| Fall of Jerusalem | [[Lamentations]], [[Jeremiah]] | Grief, confession, and hope in God's faithfulness. |

## Post-Exilic Period
| Setting | Prophets | Major Themes |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Temple rebuilding | [[Haggai]], [[Zechariah]] | Build the house, future glory, priestly cleansing, coming king. |
| Restored community's spiritual drift | [[Malachi]] | Corrupt worship, covenant marriage, tithes, messenger, and day of the LORD. |

## Major Prophetic Anchors
- [[The Suffering Servant]] - Isaiah's servant hope
- [[New Covenant]] - Jeremiah's promise of inward renewal
- [[The Ancient of Days]] - Daniel's vision of heavenly judgment
- [[Day of the LORD on All Nations]] - Judgment and restoration horizon
- [[Outpouring of the Spirit]] - Joel's promise fulfilled at Pentecost

## Notes
Prophecy is not detached prediction. It is covenant preaching in historical crisis, with future hope rooted in God's promised faithfulness.

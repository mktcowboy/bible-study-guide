# The Day of the LORD

## Overview
The Day of the LORD is the sovereign moment when God breaks into history to judge wickedness, vindicate the righteous, and restore his covenant people. It is both terror and hope — darkness for the proud and dawn for the humble.

## Canonical Development
The Old Testament prophets sound the alarm with increasing urgency: Amos warns Israel not to desire the Day of the LORD, for it is darkness and not light (Amos 5:18-20); Joel calls the nation to repentance as locusts, fire, and armies herald its approach (Joel 2); Zephaniah describes that day as a day of wrath, trouble, and distress upon the whole earth (Zephaniah 1:15); and Isaiah and Malachi declare that it will level the proud and purify a remnant. In the New Testament, 2 Peter 3 echoes the prophetic voice, warning that the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night and the heavens shall pass away with a great noise; and Revelation unfolds the Day in its fullness through the opening of the seven seals, the bowls of wrath, and the final judgment before the new creation.

## Notes
The Day of the LORD holds both judgment and salvation in tension: it destroys all that is corrupt and consummates God's kingdom. Those who call upon the name of the LORD shall be delivered (Joel 2:32), making repentance the only fitting response in every age.

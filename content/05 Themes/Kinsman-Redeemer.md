# Kinsman-Redeemer

## Summary
The kinsman-redeemer is a near relative with the responsibility to act on behalf of a vulnerable family member - redeeming land, protecting inheritance, and preserving the family name. In [[Ruth]], [[Boaz]] fulfills this role for [[Naomi]] and [[Ruth]], making the theme one of the clearest Old Testament pictures of redemption.

## Key Passages
- Leviticus 25:25
- Ruth 3-4
- Jeremiah 32:6-12
- Ephesians 1:7

## Biblical Pattern
- A true redeemer must be related closely enough to act.
- A true redeemer must be willing to bear the cost.
- A true redeemer must be able to complete the work publicly and lawfully.
- [[Boaz]] embodies this pattern in part; [[Jesus Christ]] fulfills it perfectly.

## Notes
Ruth's story gives the theme a human face. Redemption is not abstract; it restores inheritance, family, dignity, and future hope.

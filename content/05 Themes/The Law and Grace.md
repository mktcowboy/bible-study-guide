# The Law and Grace

## Overview
The Law and Grace traces how God's commands reveal his holiness, expose sin, guide covenant life, and ultimately point to the grace fulfilled in Jesus Christ.

## Canonical Development
The law is given to Israel as covenant instruction after redemption from Egypt. It is holy, just, and good, yet it cannot give life to sinners. The New Testament does not treat grace as lawlessness; grace fulfills what the law anticipated by uniting believers to Christ and forming Spirit-produced obedience.

## Notes
Grace does not make holiness optional. It supplies the redemption, new identity, and Spirit-given life that the law could command but not create.

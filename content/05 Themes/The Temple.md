# The Temple

## Overview
The temple is God's appointed dwelling place among His people, first instituted as the portable Tabernacle in the wilderness and culminating in the eternal New Jerusalem where God dwells with redeemed humanity face to face.

## Canonical Development
God commanded Moses to build the Tabernacle so that He might "dwell among them" (Exodus 25:8), establishing the pattern of sacred space where heaven meets earth. Solomon fulfilled the promise to David by constructing a permanent house for the LORD in Jerusalem, and at its dedication the glory of God filled the temple so that the priests could not stand to minister (1 Kings 8:11). After the exile, Ezekiel received a vision of a restored and glorified temple measuring far beyond Solomon's original, pointing toward an eschatological fulfilment that no earthly building could achieve (Ezekiel 40-48). Jesus declared himself the true temple when he said "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up" (John 2:19), and the apostles extended this to the church as a holy temple built of living stones (Ephesians 2:21; 1 Peter 2:5), until the New Jerusalem descends from heaven and the LORD God Almighty and the Lamb are themselves the temple thereof (Revelation 21:22).

## Notes
The temple theme binds together worship, atonement, and the presence of God across all of Scripture; every earthly sanctuary is a shadow of the ultimate reality in which the Lamb himself is both priest and temple in the holy city that needeth no sun.
